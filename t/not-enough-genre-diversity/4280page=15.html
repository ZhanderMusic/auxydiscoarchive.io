Fwiw, and speaking as someone who (in my own opinion) creates music outside the norm for this forum…

Being ‘one’ isn’t a problem. The problem is that it’s hard to get traction and grow the level of engagement and appreciation with that genre when you’re starting from zero. That’s partly down to the make-up of this forum.

It’s not that simple or easy to ‘start a trend’.

(If Lenberg doesn’t like your music, then it’s even harder; potentially impossible as far as starting a trend on this community goes.)

It comes down to whether it’s about changing/expanding the types of music existing members make or attracting makers from a more diverse range of genres.

Both are unlikely.

akabillposters April 5, 2018, 8:06am #289

But, again, that comes down to expecting producers of certain genres to diversify.

If that’s not already how you instinctively approach making music, then it can be a bit forced and insincere, which isn’t likely to lead to worthwhile music, just clumsy attempts at different genres,

Plus, it’s not always effective to be diverse in your own output. It can hamper efforts to build an audience as most people listen to an artist expecting a certain genre or style.

It’s why artists often release different sounds under different names.

I’m in the process of doing that now.
I recently culled the vast majority of my SC tracks, to focus that account on a new name and a distinct section of my output.
I’ll probably be focusing it even more tightly, moving more tracks to other names/accounts.

I have the added bonus of none of my distinct styles being particularly popular here.
