akabillposters November 21, 2017, 7:16am #59

I appreciate what you an @enby are saying. It’s definitely worth doing more internally.
However, I expect internal initiatives will take longer or have less impact compared to an influx of new users from currently less appreciated genres.

@AzureOnyx can promote a track from ‘non-dominant’ genre, but that’s likely to add only a relatively small uptick in appreciation.
Unless your converting people from dominant genres to also actively develop an appreciation for non-dominant genres, and actively show that through plays, likes, reposts and maybe even producing some tracks in that genre, then the amount of likes an (e.g.) FB track attracts here is always likely to significantly outnumber that of non-dominant genres. They simply enjoy a larger number of fans, and consequently a larger degree of appreciation.

It’s unlikely that large portions of fans for whom [insert current ‘dominant’ genre] is their favourite genre will quickly start liking other genres enough to make and promote that genre to the extent that they do for their favourite genre.

It’s do-able, possibly. It’s certainly worth trying that too.
But it’s hard, uphill work to ‘convert’ non-fans into fans, compared to doing more to attract a broader range of genre appreciation into the forum - from outside.

This is where fans of ‘non-dominant’ genres and Auxy can - and arguably should - be doing more.

 
(The ‘but they’re only two people’ defense is not really valid, imo. Yes, if the company had more people, they could handle more things in-house. But, it’s more important that things get done, than whether they’re done in-house or not.
Empowering trusted and willing members of the user/fan community to volunteer their time and effort is one possible solution - just as they did when needing help managing and moderating this forum.)
