For producers block, I’d say, take a break for a while; go outside, take a bike ride, do something other than music for a while. (Sometimes I’ll take a two day to two week break.) When you come back, you come up with stuff you never thought you could.

For producers slump, I’d say everyone here has answered it pretty well. Force your self to make something, even if it isn’t that good. Akyut July 13, 2019, 6:51pm #14

I have two solutions for when I hit a block.

Solution one is simple, and involves throwing sh*t at a project until it makes good noise. This doesn’t always work, but I find that when it does it works well. And I mean really well. Some of my best work comes from that breakthrough push, even if the build-up is painful.

Second solution is also simple, but extremely difficult to do (at least for me.)

Cut yourself off from all music, or as close to as you can get. After a day or so, my head is just filled with melodies and ideas for music and I honestly can’t put that feeling into words. It’s not easy to go that long without music, however, so I tend to forego it.

Never listen to another person’s work for inspiration during a block. It just leads to endless procrastination.

Also, make sure you’re producing for the right reasons. Find what drives you when you are motivated, and try to remember that feeling. Hope I was somewhat helpful.
