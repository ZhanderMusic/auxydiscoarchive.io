GioMakyo February 21, 2021, 6:39am #6

Keep reverb off your low frequencies, below 200, otherwise you’re risking mud.

Regarding cutting EQ on everything, a lot of producers do cut everything below about 500 K except on the bass & kick, but I think that’s a bit extreme. There’s a lot of presence in the 200 to 500 range. You can use the HPF to cut below 500 (like 9 o’clock or 10 o’clock on the dial) on sounds like your snare, hats, cymbals, probably leads too.
Things like pads, you’re better off using the liow cut on the EQ to find the exact point where you want to roll it off a bit.

GioMakyo February 21, 2021, 6:43am #8

With reverb in general, ask yourself which Sound is the effect going to be the most noticeable on, and shape it to sound good with that. The other instruments should have reverb more or less in ballpark range, but if it’s a very low levels, I wouldn’t lose too much sleep over adjusting all of them one by one.

Reverb, when used subtly, its kind of the glue that holds all the sounds together and not make everything sound too discrete and separate. At heavier levels, it’s more of an effect, and in that case you don’t need to match the characteristics with what you consider your “overall“ reverb.
