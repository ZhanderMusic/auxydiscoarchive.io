Db909 May 9, 2018, 10:20pm #4

There‚Äôs a lot you can do and of course you can mix and match all these things to taste:

Keep some of thefoundational elements of the first verse (chords, bass, drums, whatever) and then do new melodies and/or everything else

Keep all the same stuff, but with different sounds

Keep everything but change the drums up

Make it a stripped down version of the first verse, perhaps something in the background of the first verse is now the focus

Have the same ideas going, but with different rhythms. For example verse one melody is primarily an 8th note feel. So verse two could be that same melody but with a 16th note feel, or whatever.

Or you could always just write a completely new verse. Something that keeps the energy moving forward, but different. You can still have your chorus/drop come in when expected to keep a sense of familiarity.

My favorites are the last two, but I do a little bit of all these things
