So according to this section, we have some basis on what Eurobeat is like. I read a little bit more of the Wikipedia page.

Eurobeat as we know it is actually more Japanese in origin than European. Original Eurobeat is more of a type of High-NRG/Italo Disco, but with a few common rules:

  • Eurobeat primarily utilizes an octave bass instead of any other sort of bass rhythm, though there can be some instances of gallop bass

  • Snare is almost non-existent in Eurobeat, especially J-Eurobeat, which is the style most people are familiar with

  • The beginning of a track is structured similarly to the chorus and bridge of the track. The riff is basically an instrumental version of the chorus, with a very technical melody that‚Äôs usually repeated after the first chorus.

  • the melos (short for melody) can be choruses or even verses, though the second melo (b melo) in any given track is mainly used as a bridge.
