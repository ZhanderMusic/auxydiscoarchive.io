Untitled (A Summer Jam) - (artist TBC)

Heard @foryou’s ‘Together we can overcome life’s hardships’ (Thread), which has a great retro, Lo-Fi Hip-Hop vibe, and was inspired to build on it and turn it into a Roy Ayers-type Summer jam.

It’s currently a rough draft, with some loose, initial ideas in there. Enough to make me want to develop it more – if @foryou’s OK with that.

 
#NotAuxy

For those who might be interested, I used sounds from Arturia’s Analog Lab 3 running inside Ableton.
A great, affordable vintage synth, pianos and organs library, which I recommend to all pro-DAW users.

 
P.S. A tweaked version, just for fun, with some Post Malone and Charlie Puth samples in there…
Again, quite rough

Thanks. Inspired largely by @foryou’s good groove.
So much soul in that, I couldn’t help but play on.

A good dose of Roy Ayers influence in there too - ‘Everybody Loves the Sunshine’ being one of the best Summer jams ever (the best, imo).

Had a lot of fun putting this together. Lost myself in the groove more than once.

 
Re: the clavinet
Some bits could benefit from a little tightening up, but I wouldn’t want to put too tight a swing on that part. Want it to feel lazy and really easy-going.

If anything, I might try it with a bit of a neo-soul broken swing, where the timing becomes quite loose, even a little ‘sloppy’.

Still, it’s currently only a short draft, so there may be some changes. It could also be extended to ~3’20”, so lots of room to play with.

…maybe even a more committed hip-hop version* and/or an Erykah/Jill Scott/Floetry-style vocal.

(* One of the bootleg ideas I tried out was with a couple of '90s rap acapellas. Works quite well, but it would need a different structure to best support a vocal.)

 
(Jeez, I need to fix up my artist identities. Still don’t have one for the jazzy/soulful/hip-hop stuff.)

 

your lead work as always is just too hot

Thanks. My playing ability is still poor, but it flows more easily when I’ve got real keys to play with, rather than a sequencer.

It certainly helps when you have the great sounds that I love most to play with.
As mentioned, this was put together in Ableton using a great vintage synth/keys/organ library, so the flow was much more natural. I get much more into it.

I’m starting to move into the kind of post-Auxy workflow where Auxy is just for sketching down chords*, leads, beats*, etc…, but then the work is done mostly using Ableton, some AUs and other iOS apps.

(* although, to be clear, the main chords, bassline and beats for this track came from Surf’s track)
