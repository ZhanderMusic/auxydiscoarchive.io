tornait March 27, 2019, 1:41am #29

oh! That’s right, I forgot to tell you what these drums were mainly created for (though you can use them in aaaaaany genre, I’m just giving some good examples.)

Python is a good all-rounder kit. You can use it in mainly EDM, if you want to add a Latin flair to your druns.

Graffiti is a really versatile kit, but I mainly created it for future bass and future trap. Also feel free to use it in EDM.

Commune is good for grooving, percussion-heavy tracks. Like a good cheese to a fine wine, i’d pair it best with a funky bassline.

Wrath is the harshest sounding of all the kits. It can bring a heavy, industrial edge to a track. I’d say it’s good for some heavy shuffle tracks, or maybe dubstep.

Jab is the premier electro house kit of the pack, though you can use it for like… glitch hop. Or complextro. (Someone’s gotta resurrect those genres…)

Major is my attempt at a percussion-heavy drumline. I mean, who doesn’t like marching bands? (That’s why it’s named so, after the drum major.) I’d also pair this with a funky bassline. It’s a genre-bending kit for sure.

Slam is another heavier kit. Really good for anthem-type anything. If it’s heavy and loud, this kit’ll work.

Cosmos is arguably the most lo-fi out of the bunch. I built it for nu-disco type tracks, but you can get so much use out of it for other genres, such as downtempo or even ambient stuff.
