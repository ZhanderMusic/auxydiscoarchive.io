There is a lot of talk about the Auxy bubble. Some general things I’ve learned:

  • There are lots of outlets that play creative independently produced electronic music. A lot of them are friendly to people that reach out to them politely, respectfully, and at least semi-professionally.
  • If you want to really break outside of that bubble your activity seems to need to be 80% promotion 20% production. Maybe or maybe not that is your thing and that is ok.
  • There are other bubbles, but they are bubbles too. Even within specific social platforms you may find many creative communities completely disconnected from each other. Try to reach into as many as you can if you like. But finding one (or a few) that you like might be plenty rewarding.
  • The skills for self promotion, pr, sales, project management, interpersonal skills, etc transfer to every single other thing you do in life. Its worth getting better at those things too. I am still.
  • You get what you give. Promoting other peoples music often gets them to notice yours (assuming its good). It feels good - like you are doing things “the right way”. I gave away copies of music from Bandcamp too people I’ve connected with along the way and have supported me.
  • Similarly, make sure you promote outlets that play your music (bloggers, playlisters, djs, etc). Its shocking how few people do that and it is important.
  • I hear it all the time but I’ll say it again, remember Auxy is just one of many tools. Not the sole definition of your music.
