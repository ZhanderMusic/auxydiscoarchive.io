Cheddr April 15, 2018, 2:44am #4

In a nutshell my goals are

  1. To build enough momentum / critical mass on to be noticed by enough [Spotify followers/ twitter followers/ spotify algorithms / public playlist curators] to get 1000+ plays on future releases.

It takes more than 30 seconds of listening to count as a play and it takes at least that many plays to just start to get noticed before music gets suggested to others automatically by spotify to others.

It’s an experiment and a challenge. Frankly maybe even a long shot. I’ve been critical of spotify in the past. But I hadn’t really given it an honest marketing effort. If I can’t get that many plays consistently, honestly I’ll probably publish there anyway in the future but will put most of my efforts somewhere else.

Why I want to do that is related to the second goal…

  1. To meet cool independent musicians along the way and help them too. I’ve already met dozens of people that have impressed the heck out of me. Regular people with unrecognized talents because they are bedroom producers with normal jobs and families. (Whatever normal is.) They have varied goals but most of them have a creative itch and a musically outlet of some kind, and like most of us are thrilled to death when literally anyone jams out on their music.

If I don’t reach goal 1, goal 2 still makes it worthwhile.

That’s my story for now
