I may know that there are a cluster of my fans in London for example, but I don’t know what to do with that.

The answer to that question is the same throughout the music industry… gig.
(Gigs and appearances - and mercy - are where the main money comes from these days.)

If the cluster is big enough, why not try to set up an appearances? Doesn’t have to be a kinder national tourist. The gig just needs to pay for itself - or at least be a good investment.

 
The key there would be to ensure you have enough online presence elsewhere, such as your own website or some place where you can push news out to listeners and communicate with them. Get your social on. Facebook page and definitely Twitter, at least.

Basically, places where listeners/fans can ‘Follow’ you.

(Does Spotify give you a geographical breakdown on Following numbers?)

As you point out, there are no/limited communication tools for artists and listeners in Spotify. It’s purely about building listener numbers through output.

(Aside: Is your Spotify artist bio as good as it could/should be?)

However, if your music starts to attract solid listen numbers, then the chances of them searching for more info online goes up significantly.

Make sure they have more than your SC to find. Something, as said, where you can put out news and updates - and directly communicate with them.

 

I’m sure you are spot on about climbing the ladder and the importance of playlists. It’s just not very transparent to me what they are doing or how they are doing it.

Jon Sine did a decent overview of how it functions recently. Worth checking out.

Plus, there are some articles on the subject of playlists over in the Landr blog.

 

…make it big. But if that’s not your goal…

Then, in that case, imo, being on a Spotify at all is just about vanity and the feel-good factor / bragging rights.
