Cheddr March 1, 2018, 9:47pm #21

Jump on in, the water’s fine.
It’s an achievable dream. Just go for it.

There is so much junk out there that no one is going to notice if they don’t like it. You can’t possibly embarrass yourself. People are very supportive. And there are like 6 billion people in the world. The chances that someone likes it is very good.

I think the quality of your stuff (both in production and content) is great. None of us are going to put out David Guetta quality music without spending ultra level money. The indie scene seems to thrive on “good enough” quality. There is some authenticity that comes with it.

But…
That all being said I think Spotify sucks. If you are not a subscriber you can’t always get it to play your music directly. I pushed play on my logo once and got Bruno Mars. It’s clunky and hard to use. On the plus side, it give you very good and interesting statistics with a free artist account. It’s easy to embed in other apps, websites, etc. Spotify is everywhere. Serious music fans may have subscriptions but I’ve had a hard time finding those people.

It’s much easier to just promote yourself on SoundCloud. Musicians all seems to use SoundCloud, so you could still make good (fun, interesting, productive) connections.

Normal people without Spotify subscriptions don’t like using soundcloud though. Friends acquaintances, causal listeners, etc. they like YouTube mostly.

I very much enjoy being on YouTube. You can do this easily directly in YouTube, but it is very easy to do it all through any of those distributors. Just about all of the services compress or down sample your work. The only way I’ve heard my music sound as good steaming as it good right out of auxy is on YouTube. YouTube is the easiest way for nonmusic obsessed people to find you.

You get paid close to nothing for all of these mentioned. Like .002 a play. But they give you access to listeners that you wouldn’t have had otherwise and that can be a rewarding experience.

There is some credibility that comes from being on iTunes and Apple Music, Amazon, google play, how much is debatable. You can actually get paid if people buy your music. Presumably after finding you through streaming.

Understand though that no one will find you just because you are on Spotify. I thought I would get some algorithmic plays, but Bruno Mars is more likely to show up on my feed than I am on his. I realized that when they played him instead of me on my feed. “You couldn’t have possibly intentionally tried to listen to Cheddr music, have some 24k magic instead” you won’t have that problem with most of the other services though. Seems like mostly a Spotify thing.

My two tenths of a cent.
