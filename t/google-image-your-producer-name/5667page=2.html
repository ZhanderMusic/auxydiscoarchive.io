My name is mainly associated with the physics topic, Lagrangian mechanics. This is essentially a rework of classical mechanics that was undertaken by Joseph-Louis Lagrange. Lagrangian mechanics works by using an equation that is essentially the sum of a system’s potential and kinetic energy. You can then use different methods of equation manipulation to get whatever you’re trying to learn about a system. Lagrange did this in order to simplify mathematical manipulation and Lagrangian mechanics ended up becoming so useful, that its concept extended into use beyond classical theories.

Here is the Lagrangian that describes the Standard Model in uncompressed form. It appears Wikipedia has it split up into more compressed parts here using the magic of summation notation. The Lagrangian of this system is so large because you’re essentially describing the universe. This equation is also sometimes used as a meme.

The basic form of the Lagrangian in classical mechanics is a lot less scary looking. It’s just L=K-U or the Lagrangian is equal to kinetic energy minus potential energy. (I know I just said that the Lagrangian is just the sum of a system’s potential and kinetic energy - it is. The reason for the negative sign is due to how potential energy is normally defined in coordinate systems.) You plug in for kinetic and potential energy depending on the situation; kinetic energy is pretty standard but how you describe potential energy varies on the forces present.
