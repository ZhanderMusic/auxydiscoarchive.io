But… but… I buy music!

As I understand it, it’s hard to make a living, let alone a really good living, from the sales/plays of the music alone — unless you’re huge on the mainstream and everything routinely gets millions of plays.
For lesser artists, a lot of the money they make will be from gigs/appearances and merchandise.

This is a problem for EDM, as so many in that genre are faceless bedroom producers. That’s why, especially in EDM, you’ll need to push.

It’s true what you say though about ‘buying music’. My recent Bandcamp purchases were a bit of an inconvenience, format-wise

I live in Spotify, so the first thing I did with those files was bring them into Spotify. I also bring my own stuff into Spotify as MP3s.

I think the game-changer is that anyone can distribute on Spotify, etc… now. In the early days of Spotify, if you had even slightly niche tastes in music, you weren’t gonna find it on Spotify. Even on popular, but non-mainstream bands (e.g. Squarepusher), albums and tracks would come and then go, because back then they were only licensed for a fixed period.
