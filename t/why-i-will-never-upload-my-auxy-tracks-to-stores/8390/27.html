Offcmd April 14, 2018, 12:07am #26

Let me chime in with another perspective.
I think limitations are a bless.
I used to produce all my music in Ableton, however I felt that with endless tools and VSTs I often got lost and instead of learning I got caught in the details of finding the right sound, tweaking notes endlessly and such.

The real joy came to me when I started using physical instruments, like drum machines, sampler, fx, a mixer, etc.

Auxy has allowed me to quickly sketch melodies on the go. ( Most of my Auxy tracks are done during commute on the subway and yes, I constantly miss my station )
I then may sample phrases or even single shots from Auxy and incorporate them into my live set. Other times I simply re-create a melody I made in Auxy on the sampler or keyboard.

Having a limited palette and hardware memory has allowed me to focus on composition and live performance and I’ve learned a lot more than years on a DAW.

I now even use Auxy on an iPad along physical instruments and trigger loops manually, often adjusting tempo of drum machine on the fly to match tempo, a skill that is lost with DAW’s

I am still looking forward to a feature that would allow Auxy to sync to a midi clock as a slave and without any computer involved (no ableton link please).

In the meantime, I can only get better at tempo syncing manually and become more dexterous.

Nothing wrong with using Auxy as a professional instrument or even the only source of your music, as long as is good stuff and you learn in the process!
