Auxy March 31, 2020, 9:04am #285

I am here!

What we care about is to help and inspire people to become better music makers. The intention of starting the forum was to give people a place where they could learn, get inspired, find collaborators and get feedback on their work. It has also been a way for us to learn more about the people using the app and get quality feedback. Looking back, I think it has been a great investment and a vital part of developing Auxy to where it is today.

While adding a social aspect to music making is a fantastic proposition, it is inherently hard to do well in practice. It usually works on a small scale, but it is hard to bring in lots of people and keep the interactions valuable and sane. Except if you are e.g. TikTok and make the network itself your main focus. For us, though, we have not found a way to go from “a nice place for hardcore Auxy fans” to something that creates value for the broader user base.

I have been going through all your post from the last couple of days and collected various topics that seem to really upset some of you. I might address some of these topics specifically later, but in general, they all seem to stem from a set of misaligned expectations.

For instance, some people are really upset by the fact that I am not posting stuff here as frequently as I used to and not addressing every question or concern directly. This is obviously because I was more active in the early days, which has raised your expectations to a certain level. And this is obviously my fault. Taking a step back, though, it seems a bit unfair to be criticized for not having everyday public conversations with users. We still keep track of what is said in the forum and we always answer all incoming emails. I think that is a pretty high bar for most app developers.

The same goes for general app complaints. To me it feels like some of you have grown your skills and expectations beyond the scope of Auxy. This is fine! Maybe it is time to try some new tools? Experiment. Find new collaborators and ways to share your music. Whatever you do, stop wasting your creativity and energy on being upset by the lack of certain features in Auxy. It is not helpful to neither us nor yourself.

'Fwiw, I still currently use Auxy as my go-to tool for early composition and development. It’s still a good tool, though I’d like to switch to a more powerful option. I do feel kinda trapped in Auxy by having so many tracks and ideas stored in it. The work/time required to get them all exported in a usable/portable format is daunting.

With a few key enhancements, (sound pack quality*, sound design/control incl. proper ADSR across all sounds, etc…) I’d likely be happy to stick with it, as it has a pretty good workflow, imo. Especially for a mobile/on-the-go tool, which is important for me, as I still ‘need’ to have a tool that I can use to kickstart something whenever/wherever inspiration strikes.

  • Recent sound packs seem quite lazy, with most sounding like tweaked versions of older sounds.

I don’t mean to sound mercenary about the forum, but at the end of the day, I wasn’t getting what I was hoping for from the community. I don’t feel like I was getting the kind of engagement that would help me grow as a music maker. I also felt like I was giving more than I was getting back. I tried to elevate the knowledge sharing and discussion and the lack of support that was getting (and the lack of meaningful engagement from the dev team with the community and product discussion) frustrated me, which came out in less-than-constructive ways.

I still consider the issues with the forum, the product, the marketing, the brand, etc… to be very ‘fixable’ and consider Auxy to be a product/company with a lot of potential. What could/should(?) be done still seems pretty clear to me (though I’m less ‘invested’ now, mainly from not being immersed in the community, so I’m less frustrated by it.)
I think the Auxy team have enough core resources to try to build Auxy into a pretty cool brand and product/content ecosystem. I’m not sure why they haven’t, but hey…

But, hey, maybe this is all stuff they’re now working on, though I only know what pops up as an in-app notification, as I don’t look in on the forum.

Either way, I’m not currently planning to return to the forum… assuming I’d even be allowed back in, which is unlikely. ;)’
