A bad case of the perfectionisms

Production Talk
Pinewoodog January 18, 2018, 2:03am #1

So recently I’ve been trying to make my next song, but I can’t seem to put one together. Every time I get close to finishing a song, I feel like it’s not good enough, I could have made it better to I delete the whole project. It’s not a lack of inspiration: I have plenty of ideas that are in the form of 4 bar loops. But the problem is, when I work on a project for a while, it just isn’t good enough to be released and ends up in the trash. Does anybody have any suggestions on how to fix this?

TheRealJFalc January 18, 2018, 2:09am #2

Do something. A song doesn’t need to be perfect as much as people would disagree with me. That’s a hard thing to accept as well. Everyone wants to be better than everyone else, but music is subjective. So put out something you can be happy with, regardless of whether or not it’s “perfect” or not.
