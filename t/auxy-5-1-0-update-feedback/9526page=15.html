akabillposters April 27, 2018, 11:11pm #337

It’s not the amounts. It’s the principle.
(Although, even the thought of what would effectively be a $5 leaving charge/ransom fee for a “free” app is pretty ‘black hat’ thinking. Tbh, I’m not confident such an approach is even sound from a trading standards / consumer rights perspective. Are you?)

People can’t be blamed for any reluctance to temporarily sign up to a new agreement (requiring Apple ID/payment details, etc*) as the only way to quit using an app without sacrificing existing projects.

Requiring exiting users to sign-up before they can sign-off feels like some of the more manipulative types of sales techniques.
…a little like the vacuum salesman who starts his demonstration by throwing dirt on the potential customer’s carpet.

 
(* Something that’s clear to anyone engaging with this forum isn’t straight-forward for some.)

 
It’s not clear why you repeatedly choose not to announce reductions to the free offering ahead of time.
People will naturally make assumptions about what seems like ruthless disregard for users who have done nothing ‘wrong’. They’ve simply used your app in ways that you deemed (undesirable, but) acceptable - up until today.

They may not have been financially beneficial as users, but they have typically been loyal advocates of the app, the team and the brand. They’ve produced music that has helped spread awareness of Auxy in the process. They chose your product.
Even though you feel you can no longer sustain them as free users, there are ways to ‘let them go’ that show that you and the Auxy brand respect them and are glad they came - even if they ultimately weren’t able to stay.

But, rather than treat them like unfortunate losses, you effectively punish them for not converting to premium users; something that needs to be scraped off.

The last thing you want is people walking away thinking choosing your app was a mistake.
What good does it do you or the company to turn any advocate into a critic?
What do you think that does for the chances of them returning to Auxy if/when their situation improves?

This is the second time in six months this approach has been taken.
What assurances do users have that the goalposts won’t shift again six months from now?

The mistrust it creates can only hurt Auxy - and that mistrust in the Auxy brand will likely extend beyond free users.

Which is all the more of a shame because it was all easily avoidable.
