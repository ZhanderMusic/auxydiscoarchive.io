akabillposters May 2, 2018, 7:44am #579

No, but kinda yes.

Backlashes often occur when a change is too sudden or substantial. There have been backlashes (some major, some minor) over several aspects of this update.

Lenberg’s words, acknowledgements and apparent reconsiderations suggests that user feedback wasn’t sought before the roll-out.

It’s generally sub-optimal to first test new ideas using your live product – at least, not unless you’re openly labelling your product a beta.

Otherwise, it’s better for many reasons to test the effectiveness/impact of changes with a limited, broadly representative sub-set of users. This gives you time to assess the impact, make further changes if required, and then go live with changes that are supported by user-tested evidence that they’ll be successful.

It’s not a guarantee. But, it generally reduces the occurrence of major missteps that could hurt the product and the company.
