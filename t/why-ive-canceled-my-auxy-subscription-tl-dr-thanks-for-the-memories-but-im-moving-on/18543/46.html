To sum up your experience being promoted by Auxy:

  • You miss being featured by Auxy as a way to get more exposure, which you have “greatly benefited from” in the past
  • Getting featured has been a pain in the ass because the exposure has lead to some people with no followers posting your tracks on their profiles
  • Some guy uploaded your track to Spotify and somehow got on a playlist that generated 140k plays
  • Being heavily promoted by Auxy sucks
  • Having people look up to you is annoying and you prefer to rather just be an artist and create music

My thoughts:
It seems like the really annoying thing was that one guy stole a track, distributed it to Spotify and got lucky getting featured in a playlist. This is of course unfortunate, but I can assure you it’s not something happening to Auxy users only. There are tons of copyright disputes on every major platform every day. The best way to prevent these issues is to be the first person uploading to these platforms.

I think we could have done a better job clarifying that people are not allowed to steal tracks, and that is why we locked the export feature for demo tracks a while back.

Overall, it seems to me that the value of getting your name and tracks out to hundreds of thousands of people is still worth the occasional guy stealing the track and eventually having to take it down and not getting any money for it. If a track would really take off, or end up as the beat for some big rap hit or something, you would be able to actually process and retain the rights to that track. And this would be great given how hard it is to gain exposure as an artist today.
