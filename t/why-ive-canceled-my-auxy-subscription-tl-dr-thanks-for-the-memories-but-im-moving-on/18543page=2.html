aUstin_Haga March 28, 2020, 8:03pm #25

I didn’t really want to make a post about this topic so much myself, but I think there are some important points to be made.

Yes, recent inactivity of the devs has made me a bit wary as well. I miss the monthly staff picks playlists, but that might have been partially because I was almost always in them. In the past I have greatly benefited from featuring in the app and the like, which many other users I’m sure resent me for.

However, even for me it’s gone downhill, even before I began experimenting with ableton (at the beginning of this year). The devs have been more than generous to me which I’m greatful for, but I’ve experienced some real rough patches as a “featured artist” or whatever. The featuring of Float and Changes (the two most popular tracks of mine for those of you newer users) in the app were the coolest thing ever to me. But it caused some real frustration. Demo projects let tons of users rip off the project and try to bootleg the song as their own “creation”. This was outrageous to me, but as far as I know, any plagiarism of these tracks was limited to either people who took them down or they got like 3 plays total, so they were reasonably harmless in hindsight.

Sand Castle was a completely different story, though. Looking back, it was a mistake. I don’t really think it’s the devs’ fault for the most part, but more definitely could have been done. Sand Castle was a song I made that I let become a demo project for the app, which I thought was cool at the time, but now has probably caused me more frustration than any other song I’ve posted. For starters, I’m fairly certain it was put in the app without my name on it anywhere at first (this was later changed, but still). So that caused tons of users who simply didn’t know that it wasn’t made by Auxy to use it in ways that were much more annoying to me than either of my other two featured songs/projects.

When I agreed to let it be a demo project, I was under the impression that by doing this, Auxy would somehow link to the track on soundcloud or something like that to give me some real exposure like the last projects. It got much worse when some guy using distrokid ripped Sand Castle (and other demo projects for that matter), amassing some 140,000 plays on my song alone. This would have generated upwards of $800 dollars worth of royalties on my song alone, not to mention the other songs that were in the tens of thousands of plays on his page as well (all of them stolen from other auxy artists). After extensive emails with his distributor and Spotify (the “owner” of the account had already apologized and was willing to cooperate) for months, I didn’t receive a penny for those royalties. All I seemed to get from henrik was “I’m sure it will work out eventually”.

Personally, being an “elite” actually sucks. For one thing, because Auxy has a pretty bad plagiarism problem, mainly because almost nothing was taken as a precaution to stop people from just taking all of my hard work. At one point we might have gotten “special treatment”, yes, but largely our suggestions were dismissed as much as everyone else (which, I mean, the devs have a right to choose what they do with their own app, but I still think being able to automate release is super nessecary lol). Anytime I get a notification from this forum, 90% of the time it’s either from someone “fangirling” and wanting to collab or remix, which you end up seeing as gold-digging, or it’s someone pinging you to say “hey, this guy gets special treatment. That’s unfair!” I just want to be an artist and create music. It gets old. There’s a reason I rarely use the forum to post stuff anymore.

about the app itself:

Auxy is still a great price for what it is. I stand by that. Using ableton, (Standard base price is like upwards of $400?) is a lot in itself, but it’s stock instruments and samples, in my experience, don’t match up. Auxy has done a great job of bringing in quality sounds monthly*. For ableton, one of the best options for sounds is splice, which by itself costs more than an Auxy subscription for the samples alone. On top of that, plugins for instruments and effects also completely drain your wallet by comparison.

That being said, exploring outside of Auxy is almost a must if you plan on working with vocals or other audio samples that aren’t one-shots, really. Auxy’s just not built for that. However, I love Auxy because without it there’s a chance I either would have given up on music or been completely incapable when trying to learn ableton or a similar program and also given up.

There’s definitely stuff worth being changed on the platform, not just what I’ve said but others too, but it’s not a black-and-white issue. I’m not mad at the developers or anyone else, but I do think that there needs to be more communication and empathy from both sides.

Arimyth March 28, 2020, 9:03pm #36

This is a topic that, at its core, has been brought up on numerous different occasions and in my mind the answer always remains constant, with this being no exception.

As a corporation (even a small partnership) it is strongly encouraged to maintain a level of communication with your users such that they don’t feel the “radio silence” effect that often follows right before an exit or an otherwise completely silent disappearance. This is especially the case when your product or service is on a subscription basis, as there’s now a constantly changing balance between money the user pays and utility that the user expects that is not present in a one time payment system.

As far as Auxy goes, I personally feel that the software is powerful, unique, and robust enough to justify a price of $5 per month even if it were never to receive another update in its lifetime (or until another music application were to surpass its feature set).That being said, we as consumers support and speak with our dollars, and I do not want to encourage the type of communication that seems to be the “resting state” with Auxy that I’m seeing now (lack of communication, people speak up and there’s a mild resurgence before eventually returning to a lack of communication).

So while I do not intend to cancel my subscription in the foreseeable future, I would like to take this as an opportunity to remind the developers that communication is arguably the most important thing you can do as a company, even more than deliver features (I’d much rather have a several-month hiatus from soundpacks provided that I knew that time was being used to deliver features or enhancements of other kinds), and that there is validity to those that are cancelling their subscription as a method of stating that the recurring radio silence is not an acceptable or sufficient way of communicating with users.
