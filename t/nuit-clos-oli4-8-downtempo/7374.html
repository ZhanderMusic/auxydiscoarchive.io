Side note on your genre tagging.

The previous version is tagged as #triphop when it’s not remotely close to that genre.
(The reworked version, which is very similar, is tagged as #electro, which is a much closer fit, imo.)

Having inaccurate genre tags will almost certainly limit your channel.

  1. Triphop fans might check out your track, but then, discovering it isn’t triphop, they skip it.
    If SC is anything like other content channels, there’s a reasonable chance that SC are tracking skips and ‘mark down’ your channel if you’re content is skipped a lot.
    This reduces the likelihood that they’ll automatically suggest/recommend your content in future.
  2. The counterpart to that is that fans of the genre your music actually is (or is closer to), might not find it because it’s primarily tagged as something else. Similarly, the algorithm won’t be automatically suggesting/recommending your track to electro fans.

It can be hard knowing which genre tags to use, especially as the primary genre tag. However, it’s always better to make tags as accurate as possible, even revising them on older tracks, as you become aware of a better fit.

If in doubt, use a genre from one step back up the genre family tree. e.g. in this case, #electronic would probably have been better than #triphop.

 
Just a thought.
