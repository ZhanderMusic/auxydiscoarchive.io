Aha, this was you. Nice!

For context about why I chase 5 stars instead of 4: In the App Store, you need to be somewhere between 4-5 in average rating to be considered a quality app (I think). Thus, rating the app 4 stars is unfortunately dragging the average down for apps that manage to reach above 4. Different people obviously think differently about what a certain grade means, but for most people, it seems to be quite binary with “great app” = 5 stars, “bad app” = 1 star. Thus it gets frustrating to hear “fantastic app” and get a rating that drags down the average.
