wagneric March 29, 2019, 6:33pm #145
Auxy:

Tempo Automation

Some people want the option to change the tempo throughout a track. We think this is something you “think you need but actually don’t”. At least we have a hard time finding many great tracks that utilizes this feature, except perhaps if you do film scoring and similar genres. So in essence, this is a pretty low priority since it would be a pretty big feature that would only be used in very few cases.

I follow the logic of this view, but I feel it ignores what music is. Music is organized/quantized sound in both the time and frequency domains, formed as a means of human expression. Before we were even born, we were listening to some pretty serious bass rhythms in the form of heartbeats whose tempo fluctuated according to conditions.

Auxy is as much a musical instrument as it is a production tool, and there are as many uniquely-amazing musicians/composers for it as there are users. My opinion is that if you hand us as many of the the essential tools of music as you can, we are eventually going to make something that stands out.

I remember hearing the tempo slowdown/speedup near the end of “Come On Eileen” by Dexy’s Midnight Runners when I was young, which didn’t stand out for me on its own (having heard lots of classical, jazz, and Irish music, etc.), but hearing it in a popular genre was really impressive and memorable.

If someone reads this in 10+ years, they’ll probably have heard many more combinations of existing genres, styles, and gimmicks than we can fathom now. As a kid I would never have believed that a popular hard rock band would go on tour with an orchestra, but 10 years later it happened. Today’s tracks that are going to stand out will be the firsts to try things, and the attitude of “you think you need it but you don’t” is going to look short-sighted.

What I love about Auxy, and subscription sounds, is that I’m given things I don’t think I need or want, that then become indispensable. I do understand the need, as developers/marketers, to limit genre/feature scope, and maybe now’s not the time for such a big resource commitment. I just implore you to keep stretching as much as you can, and keep things like this in mind, toward the goal of building a future-compatible tool that is beyond your present target.

P.S. How about a “bounce” (per multitracking) feature, where you could conveniently render a scene as audio, so as to then mess with its pitch as a sample? Being able to grind your track to a halt would be fun to do without having to export/trim/etc.

stereofield March 30, 2019, 2:02pm #152

There are three main MIDI-related features which I’m going to rank for reference (and explain the reasoning behind the ranking):

  1. MIDI import. (Being able to open pre-created MIDI files in Auxy) The reason I think this is the most important is because Auxy is primarily a sequencer app. MIDI import is a lot closer to sequencing than to live recording, which is what the other two MIDI features would be related to.

  2. MIDI in. (Being able to play notes on a keyboard and hear them played in Auxy) As I said in the last point, this would be less necessary to add because it’s more related to live recording, when Auxy’s a sequencer app. However, if a “record” option would be added in conjuction with quantizing the notes (more or less snapping them to 1/16 or 1/32 lengths), this is definitely doable.

  3. MIDI out. (Being able to hear Auxy’s notes played on an external source) The only benefits this would have is for “jamming” (playing for fun outside of the sequencer) or for using Auxy’s sequencer to play in other places. Neither of these are really within the scope of the app’s purpose, so this is probably pretty unlikely to be added.

Tl;dr – MIDI import is probably the most necessary & least talked about MIDI-related feature that would make Auxy much more powerful (and still just as convenient & easy to use).
