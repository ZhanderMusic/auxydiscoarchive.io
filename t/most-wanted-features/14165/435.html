OIL July 17, 2020, 3:52pm #433

Welp. Regardless of whether this gets read or not, I’ve got some suggestions (which might have been mentioned before but there are a lot of messages here)

  • Front and back sample trimming. I don’t even need to see the waveform, you can make two knobs or two sliders, one each for front and back, or you can make one two-knobbed slider which can control start and end points of the sample. It doesn’t need to be hectic.
  • A similar feature to the “warp” function in Ableton, where you can change the length of a sample without changing the pitch.
  • Finer velocity control. Please. You can frame it as softness/hardness of played notes for people who might not be familiar with the concept. It will help make compositions so much more dynamic and rich.
  • Envelope automation.
  • A decay slider for synths.
  • M A S T E R T R A C K (with automation)
  • More scales/modes. Yes, I’ve heard you say not a lot of people use them, but perhaps that’s because they aren’t more widely known. Putting a variety of scales/modes in will open up more melodic avenues to users who aren’t as familiar with them, allowing for more room for experimentation, which will lead to more engagement with the app due to the more plentiful musical avenues to explore. Also it will make the app look and feel more robust.
  • Tempo automation, for two reasons: 1) More dynamic, professional composition, and 2) It would also take care of the desire for the 1/64 note division, as you could simply increase the tempo and use the given note divisions instead.
  • A free move option for automation, maybe.

So, yeah. That’s stuff. These were (mostly) written keeping in mind that you’d probably want to keep the app lightweight and non-cluttered, while allowing for more powerful and robust features. These suggestions would mostly expand on existing frameworks.

Oh, almost forgot: @Auxy (so that you see the post)

iammane August 8, 2020, 12:57pm #445

I’m conflicted.

Half of me is in the same boat as you ultimately. There’s quite a bit of lost opportunity here that is going to be snatched up by someone else. All of the things you listed would be welcome additions.

At the same time, I acknowledge that anyone looking for these kinds of features are simply not the target audience for this app. And in all fairness, I’m pretty sure Lenberg has made it clear in the past that power users should either move on, or use Auxy in tandem with something more powerful. And they encourage this (which I think shows in their decision to integrate Live set export, Studio One export, Ableton Link, etc).

On the AC discord I was curious to see how many people even got into music production because of Auxy (or apps like Auxy) and there’s a healthy chunk of folks who never even would have considered trying it out, but have and found a hobby they really enjoy (or even a potential line of income) thanks to Auxy. A lot of folks try Auxy, use it for a bit, and then say “Hey, what’s this FL Studio all about, what’s Live, what’s Reason” and honestly, I think if the best purpose for Auxy is to be a “gateway drug” to other methods and softwares, to help someone learn technique and form a baseline understanding of a samplers function, a synth, what a filter does etc then is there really anything wrong with that? (and along this persons journey, Auxy makes a few bucks).

The more I think about it, I don’t really know what I (personally) want out of this app. There’s so many apps out there that do so much more, some better than others, but maybe Auxy fills one specific, good niche, and it does it well, and maybe it doesn’t need to do any more than that…

Just spit ballin.
