Long overdue, and posted before, but deserves repeating, imo.

  • ‘Tap tempo’ to get up and running more quickly when inspiration strikes. Or…
  • A metronome when pressing on the tempo slider dot.

…for those of us who often kickstart a project with a tempo (pace) in our heads, but who can’t accurately translate that to a BPM number intuitively.

The current required workflow is, frankly, bad; it’s convoluted and gets in the way of capturing that initial moment of inspiration quickly and accurately.

Current process: 10 steps…

  1. New instrument (any instrument)
  2. New pattern
  3. Lay down simple 4-4 as a metronome
  4. Press Play
  5. Come out of pattern
  6. Open project settings
  7. Dial in the tempo
  8. Close project controls
  9. Press stop
  10. Delete metronome instrument/pattern
  11. Start creating (assuming you can still accurately recall the idea you had in your head)

Preferred process: 3 steps…

  1. Open project settings
  2. Dial in tempo (using either tap tempo or metronome on press)
  3. Close project controls
  4. Start creating

 
Side-thought: I know the minimalist aesthetic is intentional, but there’s more than enough space in that top bar to put the tempo there (too?), and trigger a slider with metronome on press.

An earlier list, with some descriptions…

New consolidated list, in a soft order of preference — with links to earlier descriptions/discussions.
Some more easily achievable than others.

I genuinely believe all of these would bring a heap of value to users.

  • Full ADSR control for instruments (a boy can dream, right? (See also) (Decay probably more immediately useful than Sustain)
  • Monophonic mode option for instruments
  • More granularity in note volume control. Current ‘full/soft’ is insufficient, especially when adding nuance to chords. (See also)
  • Free mode in pitch automation. Disables horizontal/vertical snapping to grid. (See also)
  • Ability to loop sequences of scenes, up to the entire track (rather than only single scenes) (See also)
  • Enhanced Mute/Solo controls - e.g. swipe right across main instruments/patterns screen to reveal simple mute and Solo controls in a column on the left. (See also) (See also)
  • A way to mark a note as non-retriggering
  • Reintroduce x-device project syncing or have some way of flagging if a newer version is found in the iCloud backups (and provide an easy/one-tap method to update the local version of the project)
  • Formally flesh out FX options with tremolo, vibrato, phaser, flanger, compressor, additional distortion types, saturation, etc…
  • User-definable measure lengths to allow for quarter/half/three-quarter measures, as well as 5/4, 6/4, etc… (increments of quarter measure, if more achievable than full step granularity)
  • Add option to view numbers when adjusting FX, volume, LPF, etc… (See also)
  • More granular/free mode for automations in ‘Scene length’.
  • Ability to ‘draw’ animations using finger.
  • Ability to ‘record’ automations via ‘performance’ — e.g. record an LPF automation by using finger to adjust the control while in an automation ‘record mode’
