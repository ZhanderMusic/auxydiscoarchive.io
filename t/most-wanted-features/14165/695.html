Explain what you mean by a soft clipper. From what I understand, that feature is already present by using distortion on the drum kit.

TheRealJFalc May 11, 2021, 2:20pm #703

A soft clipper is a little more nuanced than full out distortion. It has a curved rolloff around the knee, and introduces additional harmonics to the sound but to a far lesser extent than a hard clipper, what you would consider traditional knee-based distortion. Soft clipping is usually modeled after analogue gear, and the most recognizable form is analogue tube distortion.
