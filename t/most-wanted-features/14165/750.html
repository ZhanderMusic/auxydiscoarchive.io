iammane May 23, 2021, 12:56pm #747

@Antbrick @ThoughtfulNoise I’m in the opposite camp. Would prefer the app to instantiate an AUv3 version of the synth engine that could be used in other hosts. Sort of like how Reason works - you can use Reason standalone, or you can host the rack in your DAW of choice. Since you can run multiple AUv3s you’d basically pick one instrument (or drum rack) per instance of the AUv3.

This expands functionality into other hosts, lets people use the sounds they like and opens up a road to using other techniques like linear audio recording (ala Cubasis / GarageBand), different sequencing styles etc.

All I can think of are all the nightmares devs have trying to figure out if their AUv3 host is broken or that the dev of the offending plugin has coded something incorrectly. You could probably talk to both the dev of Drambo and for sure the dev of Nanostudio and they’ll both tell you it’s a nightmare. Team Auxy having to spend cycles on troubleshooting issues that might not even be their fault is a pretty unappealing prospect.

TL;DR - Auxy as an AUv3 is a much more support streamlined expansible option.

(@iammane might also have some references as he’s more committed to a broader iOS-based workflow than I am)

I’m a sucker for classic vintage synths like Moogs, ARP Odyssey, etc… but I mostly adore the sound of Rhodes electric piano. (I particularly love Moog + Rhodes together :chefs-kiss:

For iOS apps, Moog have their own suite (all of which are currently available for free for a limited period, iirc), including ‘Model D’ and ‘Model 15’.

Arturia also has iMini, another MiniMoog/Model D emulator.
For the Odyssey, there’s ODYSSEi. Korg MS20 has iMS-20.

For Rhodes, there’s Neo Soul Keys, Korg Module, and IKM’s iLectric.
They all do a great job of capturing the subtleties and range of sounds and styles the originals can produce.
Of course, a lot comes down to support velocity and note volume variance, including how hammer/bell action and distortion responds dynamically. It’s those things that drive the nuance and performance range.

(Vintage Keys is the Auxy instrument that I’ve rinsed the most, but it would need another 6+ Vintage Keys packs to even approach the range I’d like access to within my Auxy workflow. Additionally, the fact that Tremolo and Phaser are available only on a sub-set of the instruments in that pack is a particularly confusing and frustrating decision.)

Galileo 2 is a great organ with AUv3 support.

For modern synths, there’s Layr, Audiokit Synth One, Volt Synth, and SW1 Synth.
