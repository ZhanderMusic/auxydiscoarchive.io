Hey @Auxy, firstly I wanted to say: love this app to death. I use Auxy since 2016 and have always been blown away every update. I think this app still has great potential and could even grow to be an app that professional producers would use. I have a couple of suggestions:

The Effect Panel
I think it would be great if instead of the effects (like the chorus and reverb, etc) being pre-selected there would be a + symbol that you could click to add your own effect. Effects like a compressor, flanger, phaser that kind of stuff. A simple visualizer for the effects could be nice and an effect panel on the master bus would also be useful.

Synth Engines
I would love if there were synth engines that you can fully customize. One that has simple sound waves would be nice. Maybe place the ‘Synth Engines’ button under the instruments one.

External App Support
External sound app support for effects and instruments. I don’t know how resource intensive this would be though.

LFO’s in Automation Clip
Make LFO’s possible in the automation clip. Instead of drawing your own automation the computer generates a sine wave or square wave. Maybe even a custom formula input or preset. Also an automation output to effect settings (like the size of a Reverb for example) could also be cool but I don’t know how CPU intensive that would be.

Toggle
A toggle to switch between ‘beginner’ and ‘studio’ mode. Maybe set it to beginner mode first to not let people be overwhelmed.

I know app development is hard and this may seem like a lot, so please take it with a grain of salt. This is just me venting some ideas I had.

ThoughtfulNoise April 1, 2021, 7:59am #621

Given that Auxy supports relatively few individual FX, I suspect it might be optimal for users to present them all by default, and let the user decide which ones to ignore by leaving them at 0%, as is currently the case. i.e. I think the current model is probably the most user-friendly.

But as you correctly point out, there’s the additional consideration of what impact zeroed FX in the chain have on CPU. If substantial, then a true bypass option would be good.
(I’d assume it’s either negligible or well within performance impact thresholds, as Lenberg and team try to keep the app internally efficient. 0% might be treated as complete bypass.)

I doubt Auxy is ever going to support dozens of individual, standalone FX – and tbh, I don’t think they’d ever really need to. They could probably cover 99% of use cases by fleshing out the array of basic FX as standalones — flanger, phaser, tremolo, vibrato.

Others would/could probably be made available by enhancing options within existing FX, e.g. adding fuzz and overdrive via new options in the Distortion FX (even though they’re not strictly the same thing).

A Compressor FX would/could be handy. (It’s currently only available on the Piano sound pack and on the sample kits.)

I’d love to see amp FX in there, but I imagine there’d be very limited demand for that amongst Auxy users.
