ThoughtfulNoise March 12, 2021, 2:03pm #582

I agree that it likely wouldn’t need or benefit from super-granular volume level control. I do think that three might still fall short of offering the nuance that many might seek - and probably wouldn’t be enough of an uplift for the effort.
Six might form a decent base, but 10 would probably offer all the flexibility needed. (Although, with the right UI, it could support 100.)

I’d also like to think that we could move away from the “mode” way of adding Soft notes. I’d much prefer to work with the notes themselves, rather than switch modes. This means that working with notes - including note volumes - could be handled entirely in the notes area, rather than skipping in and out to switch note volume modes.

e.g. long press on a note to have rudimentary 10-level vertical slider.

Although, maybe some would appreciate a simpler, 3-level mode as well. i.e. There could be a note volume mode switch directly within the sequencer screen, but it would switch between ‘Normal’ mode, with three volume variants, and *ahem* ‘Pro’, with 10.

(The mini vertical slider could use the UI pattern used in the tempo, swing, etc… screens or even be ‘Free’ with 100 levels, as with Tuning samples.)

This would prove useful for Drum Kits/samples too, to add a range of nuanced grace notes without having to duplicate samples.
