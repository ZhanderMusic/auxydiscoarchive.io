The reason we don’t expose decay and sustain is that they are already mapped to a macro control (shape) in a lot of sounds. But maybe we can sort that out. The goal of shape, though, is to just give people a control for changing the length of the sound, as opposed to having to understand how an ADSR envelope works.

And if we default sustain to near 0, it will be impossible, by definition, to have sounds with long sustain. Our decay time is capped at 5 secs.

Auxy February 24, 2021, 10:17am #537

Here’s an example, if we add decay automation, we either need to set sustain to high or low. If we set it to high (max), you will be able to draw long sustained notes, without the sound disappearing. However, nothing will happen when you turn the decay knob or change the automation. Conversely, a low sustain means you cannot draw long notes. So then we’ll force everyone to understand the setup, just to support the comparatively rare case of automating the decay.
