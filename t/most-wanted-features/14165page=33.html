ThoughtfulNoise April 25, 2021, 6:15am #671

Long overdue, and posted before, but deserves repeating, imo.

  • ‘Tap tempo’ to get up and running more quickly when inspiration strikes. Or…
  • A metronome when pressing on the tempo slider dot.

…for those of us who often kickstart a project with a tempo (pace) in our heads, but who can’t accurately translate that to a BPM number intuitively.

The current required workflow is, frankly, bad; it’s convoluted and gets in the way of capturing that initial moment of inspiration quickly and accurately.

Current process: 10 steps…

  1. New instrument (any instrument)
  2. New pattern
  3. Lay down simple 4-4 as a metronome
  4. Press Play
  5. Come out of pattern
  6. Open project settings
  7. Dial in the tempo
  8. Close project controls
  9. Press stop
  10. Delete metronome instrument/pattern
  11. Start creating (assuming you can still accurately recall the idea you had in your head)

Preferred process: 3 steps…

  1. Open project settings
  2. Dial in tempo (using either tap tempo or metronome on press)
  3. Close project controls
  4. Start creating

 
Side-thought: I know the minimalist aesthetic is intentional, but there’s more than enough space in that top bar to put the tempo there (too?), and trigger a slider with metronome on press.
