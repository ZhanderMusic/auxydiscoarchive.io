akabillposters June 6, 2018, 9:58am #13

…will require a license, as well as to give credit

Just to be clear for the OP, for cover versions and interpolations, it’s the publishing license you’ll need, and the publisher(s) (usually the songwriters) you’ll be crediting, not the original recording artist.

Typically, rights to cover and interpolate are very easy to get.
Often, direct permission isn’t required. You just do your cover/interpolation and pay the royalties.

Some digital distributors will take of that for you for a simple, yearly fee per song – e.g. distrokid

 

…if you intend to use it for commercial purposes.

Whether or not the new work is commercial or non-commercial isn’t directly relevant to whether it’s an infringement or not.
It can impact damages awarded, if found to be an infringement in a suit.

It only matters if the work is either properly licensed or falls under fair use conditions – none of which relate to the new work being commercial or not.
