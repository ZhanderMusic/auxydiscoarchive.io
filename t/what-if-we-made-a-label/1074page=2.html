akabillposters October 19, 2017, 8:18am #30
NYC:

Is anyone going to try to make this a full time thing?

I don’t really care for the profits and such. I just would love to be apart of a good group of (Auxy) producers that care for your production and not how many SC followers you have.

What do you mean by ‘full time thing’? Full time (Auxy) musician, right?
If so, while SC followers alone are not important, ‘popularity’ is going to matter. You don’t have to be the most popular or even mainstream, but you’ll need enough to sell enough or get enough ad revenue to be able to make it ‘a full time thing’.
(Unless you’re independently wealthy)

Additionally, I’m skeptical about whether the music world outside of the Auxy community cares enough about the world of ‘Auxy producers’ for it to be a promotional booster.

However, there is a certain novelty/curiosity value to the fact that it’s mobile-only. (I’m sure @Auxy and @Fredrik understand.)
That could be an interesting angle. (Even as an Auxy user, I’ve got no real interest in ‘Auxy-made music’. However, music made on mobile is a bigger story that even people who don’t know or care about ‘Auxy producers’ might find interesting.)

If you position/promote it as ‘the next generation of music makers’ (and open it up to all mobile music makers – not just Auxy users), then you’ve potentially got a ‘story’ that some music outlets might pick up on.

You could have a site that promotes music made on mobile-only. The side-content would be about making music on mobile, promoting apps, tutorials, articles, interviews, etc…

(Sh*t, I think I’m talking myself into wanting to make a site like this happen.

 
As for getting some benefit out of being a collective (something more pro-active than the community created by this forum), the Team Supreme collective is worth checking out. It was a very pro-active thing, with lots of cross-promotion. There were weekly ‘beat cypher’ releases (similar in concept to BTC), which in turn helped promote members fuller releases, with some members being played/promoted on Mary-Anne Hobbs’ show on BBC Radio 1.

It also benefitted from members playing sets live, etc… – i.e. getting their music and faces out there.

(Check out the ‘Strength in Numbers’ video in the previous post – and then dig around online for their archived ‘cypher’ releases.)
