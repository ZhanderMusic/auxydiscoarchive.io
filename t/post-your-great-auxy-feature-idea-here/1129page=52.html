Now ever since January, I’ve been on a pretty long hiatus. The reason? I’ve become a full on anime loving otaku, and since then, it’s been sparking a lot of discoveries, but most importantly, my main interests in Japanese electronic music. There’s quite a few artists who’ve mastered just a bit of everything in that area: Speedcore, drum ‘n bass, you name it, they’ve probably done it.

But what I’ve noticed is this: Many of these artists use a lot of BPM changes (usually with the second bass drop, cliché), to spice things up, either slowly accelerating or just goes from slow to blazing fast, and there’s even a few songs I know that begin with a nice calm 3/4 time signature @ 90 BPM to a pumping 4/4 @ 190 BPM

I’ve always wanted to try these techniques, but even with having FL Studio Mobile at hand, Auxy’s always been the one I work with due to its minimalistic simplicity, and I personally wouldn’t wanna guess the amount of these mobile made masterpieces on SoundCloud using BPM changes.

An example of a famous well known song that has a time signature and speed changes is TouHou Project’s U.N. Owen Was Her? (I tried to remake this on Auxy at some point, but I realized that it was 3/4 that became 4/4 later on, and using 3 bar scenes made the song 4/3, VERY BIG difference!)

But other than that, I understand that Lenberg and the team are still whipping us a new update with all our ideas on the list, and we should give it some time, but the idea of changing BPMs in songs is something I wanna suggest

but as for bpm changes for your tracks, you’re better off just exporting to an actual DAW. Even something like Garageband would work for bpm changes.

If you do want to make things seem like they are slowing down or speeding up, one good way is to change the pitch of the drums.
