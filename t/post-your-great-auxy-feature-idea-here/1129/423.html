What’s up guys, I already made a separate topic on this but I would like to share it here because more people will see this.

Online Library for User-Made Samples?

Wouldn’t it be great if other users could share their own samples and experiment with other peoples’ samples? Why not have an entire online library of user-shared samples? Think of it as something like Valve’s “Steam Workshop”. People can vote on samples, report them, etc.

To help support this idea, I made a crude drawing-

27846522_1473668422762501_1768447167_o.jpg2001×1500 48 KB

And to help explain what’s going on here-

27846708_1473668432762500_2127674645_o.jpg2001×1500 81.3 KB

As you can see, samples are compiled into packs (to save space). You can view sound packs based on their rating, their creation date, and maybe we can have popular creators like aUstin Haga or DeCruz to curate these sample packs. The library is complete with a user rating system, and when browsing the library you can preview some of the samples. To report or rate sound packs yourself, you need to download and try out the samples first. And that’s basically it!

Feel free to add onto this idea.

akabillposters February 15, 2018, 7:35am #432

In which case, Auxy will simply be evolving too slowly - and they risk losing whatever advantage (and audience) they currently have to existing or future competition. (They’re already at risk of this due to the team size.)

I know that prolonging their relevance to users as they develop is part of their thinking, as well as appealing more to serious music makers. At some point that’s going to mean supporting things like alt time sigs / custom bar lengths and ADSR. Otherwise Auxy will plateau and forever be stuck with a transitional audience - not quite beginners, certainly not pros.

It’s not about being all things to all people. But, there are certain features which are not Launch Day priorities, but which become conspicuous by their absence after a while.

If you keep kicking the can on them, it’s going to start having an impact on how relevant the app is.

Now, I might well be unaware of a dozen as-yet-unsupported features that would widely be considered as more important. (I’d love to hear about them.) But, it seems to me, they’re running out of features to add in service of their, frankly undemanding, core audience.

Here’s a few…

Search in Sample Packs
