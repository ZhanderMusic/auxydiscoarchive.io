akabillposters March 25, 2018, 10:56am #530

I agree with a lot of what you’re saying here, but imo, it’ll never happen, for one or two main reasons.

While I believe some decent UX thinking could easily provide an unintrusive and scalable option for velocity*, I don’t think Lenberg recognises the value to many users. However, given the vast majority of the app’s audience consists of beginners, hobbyists and semi/pseudo-serious enthusiasts, he may be right.

I suspect the % of Auxy users who would make worthwhile use of true velocity options is likely to be very small. (It’s just unfortunate and frustrating that you and I find ourselves in that very small %.)

Even the concept of velocity might be more complex than they’re willing to integrate — compared to the universally understood concept of volume. Even the language of ‘soft’ occupies a kind of vague blend of the two things, functioning as volume, but using the language of velocity.

(* It’s a topic I’ve raised previously in this thread.)

It would be great to see some features become more scalable – with deeper options available, including more note velocity/volume options, ADSR, etc…, but it’s an aspect which Lenberg has repeatedly failed to engage with meaningfully – despite his protesting that the app ‘isn’t aimed at beginners’.
