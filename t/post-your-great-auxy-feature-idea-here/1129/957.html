Artichoke June 23, 2018, 4:01am #974

I think the addition of a small drum pad could really enhance the app. A keyboard on a touchscreen is pretty stupid, but when it comes to more practical drum pads, I find my workflow so enhanced when it comes to allowing for as much liveplay as possible.

Perhaps where you’re able to name, copy, delete, move a drum kit, another option reads “record” where your 8 available sounds (perhaps expanded to 9 to allow for a clean 3x3?) map onto a full screen drum pad. You can then record a drum pattern into the scene you’re in as it loops, allowing for layering. When you leave the drum pads, a new loop has been created with your pattern, quantized.

The only issue I can think of is triplet vs duple time detection. Perhaps there is a setting in the drum pad interface that allows you to switch between 1/16 and 1/32 time as you record. Most likely people will just record a 1/16 rhythm, for example, and then switch to 1/12 to layer the next time the loop plays.

A master control is also highly in order. Perhaps a bar at the top of the screen where you can add automation? I suggest something that could just overwhelm the CPU of a mobile device, but:

Introduce a new sound kit called “Master Effects”. The instrument names go like: “volume” “low pass” “hi pass” “pitch” “reverb” etc. and other parameters like “stutter” and “bitcrush”. These instruments operate like the sweeper effect: create a loop for the bitcrush instrument, for example. In this loop, lay down one full-bar note. Now, all instruments by default will be bitcrushed at full strength, or “volume”, but you can edit whether or not an instrument is in a particular master group from the delete/move/color/label menu. This saves from creating yet more automation options in every instrument, but I think importing these effects directly into drum machines with the downloading of “Maser Effects” makes sense—sample finetuning is great right now, but I don’t think anybody would mind another row of options. Bitcrushed drums would be beautiful, but making an empty drum kit save for the one sample you want to join the master group seems inefficient.

Also, speaking of drum samples, nowadays you have to enable pitch automation. I think enabling other types of existing parameter automation (especially especially pan) would make a ton of people, myself included, very happy. Making 3 identical sample slots in a kit with varying pan degress is the best we have now, and that is hard.

 You guys know best, and I’m probably just parroting twenty other people of the near-1,000 comments in this thread. If that’s the case, just add my vote.
