akabillposters September 16, 2017, 11:13am #166
Auxy:

Which type of device are you using?

iPhone 6 Plus and iPad Mini 2. Both predominantly in landscape, while editing.

 

Adding 5 or 10 different soft levels wouldn’t really simplify the soft mode.

The ‘simplify’ thing was more about better ways of adding Soft notes. I believe there is a way that the addition of soft notes could be integrated directly into the steps/piano roll through gestures, while also offering more than a single level of softness.

I believe that a sweet(er) spot could be found.

Options that would (imo) represent a reasonable step forward – i.e. without necessarily ditching a ‘soft mode’ model…

  1. Add ‘delete note’ as a third tap. So, adding/editing notes cycles through ‘add (full) note’, ‘_make note soft’, ‘delete note’. This one feature would effectively make ‘Soft mode’ largely redundant. Alternatively, you could use LongPress to ‘make note soft’.

  2. If you kept ‘Soft mode’, it could be repurposed as a mode wherein you can edit levels of soft notes – a kind of ‘Soft Control Mode’.
    When in Soft Control mode, soft notes could show a small softness level number somewhere inside the note (visual reference: the bar length marker in the Song mode sidebar). Tap/long-press on a note while in Soft mode brings up level options.

This may not be the best/final UX solution, more some quick thoughts. But as said, I instinctively believe that there is a UX approach out there that improves on the current UX for soft notes, adds control for those who want/need it, whilst still keeping a reasonable focus on simple, effective UX.
