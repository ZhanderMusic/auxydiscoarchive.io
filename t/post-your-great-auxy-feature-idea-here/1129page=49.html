Db909 June 23, 2018, 4:15pm #985

A good touchscreen keyboard isn’t the same as just mimicking a piano. You mentioned Groovebox which is a good example. They need to be able to be restricted to a selected key or scale, the nature of the smooth surface demands this. Also, they need legato,perhaps even as a default, aka sliding from note to note without retriggering the note attack. Again this is because of the touchscreen. This way you can slide to and fro, within a key, and you simply lift up only when you want another note attack. Playing Animoog this way is a pure joy. Certainly Moog believed in it enough to craft that synth specifically for the touchscreen. Then there’s also the idea of having the key widths and octave range adjustable, ideally these adjustments are within easy reach while playing. Additionally the idea of having the y axis of the keys assigned to some expressive parameter like velocity or filter cutoff. It can and has been done really well before. And it is absolutely different from playing any other instrument, hardware midi controllers included. But I’ve been making tunes with such tools for several years now and it’s been a lot of fun. On my phone too. If you take it for what it is, these touchscreen keyboards can offer their own unique pleasures.

Yes and yes. I always cut my volume on all instruments to at least 2/3 to 3/4 of the way to account for this. Many people definitely consider -6db to be the magic number though.
