akabillposters October 8, 2017, 8:55pm #215

I agree, detuning is a genius idea

At the time I was thinking being able to toggle off the snapping to note in pitch automation, but I’m now thinking a simple duplication of the pitch control, but the top and bottom edges are ‘+1 semitone’ and ‘-1 semitone’ respectively.
(Much like Pitch control offers you +1 octave and -1 octave.)

Leave out the snapping – or give it ~30 increments, to offer enough detailed control — and you’d still be able to find a sweet-spot that suits you tastes and, thanks to automation, we could fake out some really nice LFO shenanigans.
It would give you the tools to mimic some fat Moog sounds, without actually needing to create any new Sound Packs.

You could also include the standard dial in the static instrument controls for simple up/down detuning.
12 o’clock being no detune, CW being detune upwards, ACW detune downwards.

I’d like to think an execution like that would be relatively simple to implement, as it’s basically a duplication of existing Pitch controls, just with different calibration.

akabillposters October 9, 2017, 5:34am #218
NotMiles:

In chromatic mode, it would be really helpful if the root note was a different color (how about the same color as the instrument)

A number of challenges there, as I see it.

How would it know the root when (e.g.) C-E-A could be either C Major 6th or an A Minor inversion? To even attempt to guess, it would need to know your key. As you’re working with an open deck in Chromatic, it would need to start analysing your chords to identify Major/Minor - and then reference that against the key set in the Project settings - which isn’t always relevant or useful.*
There are other considerations on top, to identify a Root, within the context of any chord, such as context.

* I’ve never changed it from the default G. I always use Chromatic and just jump in and write in whichever key and chord structure suits the piece.

 
You’d need the app to be musically smarter than it likely is at the moment. I suspect it’s little more than a ‘dumb’ note playback machine (in that regard), not really ‘understanding’ chords, per se, just playing back a selection of notes, as instructed.
On top of that, you’d need users to acknowledge and use the Key setting in the Project settings - for every Project. That means sacrificing the quick-start ‘ease of use’ that is the guiding ethos for the app, requiring users to know enough about keys and chords to use the Key setting meaningfully every time, raising the bar for entry.
