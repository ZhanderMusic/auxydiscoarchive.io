Peregrihn December 28, 2019, 2:41pm #336

Wow wtf my last post here is so outdated and cringey, I guess I have to update it: so like I said up there, I was originally G33KZ because if I were to stereotype myself then I’d be a geek, but that sounded stupid when I tried to tell people about it. It was around that time that I started staying up into the wee hours of the morning so I chose Insomniac. It was cool and I was on my way to cool branding and a deep house/future bass style… except I sucked at art, deep house, and future bass. When I tried to upload my debut album to Spotify, Amuse detected another band called Insomniac and completely screwed up the release by putting it under them. It was at that moment I knew I had to find something no one else claimed. I had already gotten used to the nickname Inso so I was looking for something that started with that. I went through a ton of duds (including “Insomuchpain” because edge) and then I found this cool word: Solidarity. It meant in unity with common interest. I chose to create the word “Insolidarity” to be my identity for the next year or so with the thought in mind that it would represent me finally finding a style and uniting with the community under skill and interest. The truth was, under this name I never found a cohesive style and I felt outcast still until the very end, so Insolidarity really meant without solidarity. It was this past summer that I decided to change my name once again. The tracks that I had made as Insolidarity were subpar compared to my new demos and 2 most recent tracks, Path of Prophets and everhaze, and they did not reflect the retrowave style that I wanted to finally go for. The name Insolidarity has already been marred beyond recovery and i didn’t feel comfortable in the name anymore. I remembered that I had to find something nobody had claimed (but with less syllables this time). It took me forever because I had never intended to change, but I knew what I wanted. I wanted something mysterious, something exciting, something that rolls off the tongue, and most importantly, a word I could misspell in a cool way. This after some research and contemplation Peregrihn was born. The word peregrine, before being used as a type of falcon, was an adjective meaning Outlandish or foreign and I wanted my sound to reflect realms of strange beauty and wonder, of intense character and story to take you far from this plane… so there you have it. I hope I’ve been living up to my musical intentions. I hope you enjoyed this rant by your very own,
Peregrihn
