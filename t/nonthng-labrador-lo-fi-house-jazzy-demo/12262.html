akabillposters September 24, 2018, 7:25am #3

Thanks. It came together quite quickly.
Conveniently, as it’s a genre that’s partly defined by a (purposeful) absence of high-end production finish, it’s a genre that I feel can potentially be taken to release-ready state entirely within Auxy.

I need to review the lead sounds and also put in some detailing on the transitions before I’m happy to push it out properly.

I’ve got a few more on the back burner, so I’ll possibly put the time in for an EP.

 
As a genre, it’s charmingly raw, giving it a slightly primitive, retro feel, and works well with Chicago garage and deep house and analogue jazz and soul vibes.

Reminds me a bit of early house from bedroom producers in the early days of pirate house stations in London.

 
If you’re diving’s into the genre, your first stop would/should probably be someone like DJ Boring and/or Ross from Friends.

Enjoy
