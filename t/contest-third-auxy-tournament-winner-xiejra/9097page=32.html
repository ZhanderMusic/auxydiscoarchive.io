paxman April 27, 2018, 1:27am #639

I understand why you think this challenge is unfair, but if you compare it to other musical instrumentation, this challenge isn’t that crazy.

A few weeks ago, during a music theory performance I was given a really crappy Epiphone guitar to improvise a jazz solo on - the action on it was terrible (a myriad of frets were buzzing), the intonation was super off, the strings had to be at least two gauges lower than I’m used to, and the strings were flatwoods, a texture I was not used to at all. Not to mention that I’m used to Stratocasters, so there was more space between the strings than I was comfortable with.

I can’t tell you that I played an amazing solo, because I didn’t - I can tell you however that I stepped outside of my comfort zone and played something different. I learned a few things too. While Strats seem to easier to shred the blues on for me, playing chords on this hollow body Epiphone felt so nice, and the tone from Epiphones that I had so longed deemed inferior to that of a Strats actually didn’t sound bad at all on a hollow body with the tone turned halfway down.

Don’t get mad at the challenge because you’re running into problems - think about all the new tones and motifs you might be able to create with something you’re not used to.
