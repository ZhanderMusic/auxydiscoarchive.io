TheRealJFalc September 4, 2018, 6:40pm #2

The best way to do this inside the app would be to use some highpass on your chords so they don’t interfere with the kick. That will help bring out the kick’s frequencies, allowing it to punch more.

If you’re exporting from Auxy, you can just do this with EQ in another program to make sure they come out.

Zeph September 4, 2018, 7:06pm #3

EQ the low end out of everything, duck aggresively, create strong grooves, and use good samples.

Zeph September 4, 2018, 7:09pm #4

Layering subs is not a good idea because it might cause phasing issues (the volume cuts off when the frequencies interfere). Just use Lake on the lowest octave for the lowest sub that will shake your house, and layer it with a gently (one tick) lowcut bass with higher frequencies so that cheaper headphones can hear it and expensive headphones can feel it.

I definitely don’t like using Auxys sounds by themselves, to bring out the sounds I need, I layer stuff like crazy.

For example, in my most recent track Restart , for the bassline, I layered a few sub basses. I had one bass with minimum low pass for the low, punchy part of the bass. Combing those two layers helped me get a heavier bass.

Db909 September 4, 2018, 10:34pm #8

Now before you go cutting all the warmth out of your track haphazardly with high pass filters, let me ask you this? Do you produce on good headphones? Because if your headphones boost the bass, then you are basically always having the low end at a lower volume that it actually should be due to your headphones making it seem louder than it is. Which would lead to the phenomenon you describe.

If that’s not a factor, I would try some simple level readjustments of the different instruments first. Auxy already has multi band compression happening under the hood to make things punchy so you may just need to turn things up and/or down. If that fails, yeah, filter the hell out of everything or do a better arrangement.
