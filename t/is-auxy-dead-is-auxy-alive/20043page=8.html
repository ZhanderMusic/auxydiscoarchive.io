Ok so, a bit of a brain dump instead.

I hope this isn’t super disjointed, I’m a little inebriated so I’m gonna try my best.

image2160×1620 470 KB

Ok so, per lane, you can specify how many times a specific loop will play. There’s not a larger size than 16 bars, which is kind of standard fare. You can specify how many times a loop from an instrument will play, and then others that fit inside that will just continue to play UNLESS they are set to one shot. If it’s set to one shot it will play once, however long it’s set for, and then that’s it.

image2160×1620 366 KB

At the end of the day though, an ENTIRE line will play. That’s just what it does. So if you want variations you either need to build them into a bar length, or, you duplicate and pop it into the next lane down. It is what it is.

Now, on the flip side, GarageBand actually has “live loops”. It looks like this:

image2160×1620 201 KB

This actually DOES let you select independent clips per instrument and you can kind of mix and match across the board. Those selections I made when I recorded the track look like this:

image2160×1620 169 KB

IMO, this is very much like Live. You select loops, you can hit record on the track and whether it’s a loop of audio or midi it will record to the main timeline.

Honestly, myself, I don’t do this. I think VERY linearly when I’m working, so I chunk stuff into clips from left to right rather than clips in a grid. But, for what it’s worth, this might jive with your workflow of actual clips that are independent of the timeline. You pick what you want and it records to the timeline.

If you’re using Live though, honestly, Auxy is probably the way to go. There’s a few things that do dump to Live (iKaossilator I think, TriqTraq) - but there’s not a ton out there that does.

Neither Gadget NOR GarageBand split automation from the note midi. Everything is baked into a single clip for better or worse. Nanostudio actually CAN split automation out from note midi, if you choose, but at the end of the day it is not a clip based paradigm.

The only other iOS/iPadOS DAW that I’m aware of that can do this is Cubasis, on paper it’s pretty damn good, but I just can’t get on with it. It does proper bussing, automation, the whole nine yards, but to me, it feels like a desktop DAW that got crammed into a touchscreen and it’s just not fluid. (Or as fluid).

I guess that’s all I got really. I hope this helps some.

I do think Auxy is a pretty good tool, I don’t know that I vibe with the cost / vs features. I think it’s VERY capable of coming up with stuff that’s near final ready, in my own experience I do feel like I need a little extra oomph at the end of the chain but I’m not an expert with this stuff. I think for what it does it does really well and really fast, I’m hard pressed to find a better all in one “groove box” style app that sounds this good.

I’m also worried about the future of it. I don’t know it needs more, unless we’re talking about time stretch and re-key. I think the whole sample pack thing has really been beat to death, unless there’s some tricks we’re not aware of.

This has even gone on too long lol. So I’m gonna wrap it up. But this is my hot take. Hopefully it makes sense for you and other folks.

I’m not an expert, I’m not a guru, but I’ve been producing exclusively on iOS since 2010 so I feel like maybe I know a thing or two about the platform and what’s going on? But anyway, take with a pinch of salt as you see fit.
