Access to the synth engine never needed it to be an internal Auxy feature.

If the engine is open source, then the only thing Auxy needed to do was make it possible to import user-created instruments/sound packs into Auxy, but…

Serious users would suffer with an ugly synth UI if it meant we could create and import our own Auxy instruments/sound packs.

Auxy team could choose to use their UX/UI skills to improve the engine UI’s usability and make it more appealing to users — with the target beneficiaries being Auxy users, and in turn Auxy.

As a subscriber-only feature, instrument/sound pack ‘Import’ could potentially be a game-changing enticement to sub, from a business and profitability perspective.

Heck, Subscribers would likely tolerate a 20–40% price increase.

This could potentially mean that Auxy no longer ‘needs’ to create packs (to maintain the impression that Auxy isn’t abandoned), but they could offer custom premium sound-packs directly from the Auxy site, setting their own price.

Same, or likely increased subscriptions revenue (bc of the new feature) + additional revenue from direct sales of ad-hoc, co-branded premium sound packs.

These features would likely foster a small internal marketplace which would keep users sufficiently topped-up with new packs that they wouldn’t need Auxy to release any.

Imo, this could be a very low risk strategy for Lenberg to…

  • revive Auxy: re-enthuse the existing user base
  • revive Auxy: establish Auxy as a living platform for potential new users (not a zombie app)
  • improve production capabilities of the app
  • improve chances of achieving original mission statement (“Auxy’s mission is to put a studio in everyone’s pocket and push music forward as an artform.”)
  • increase long-term profitability
  • improve revenue sustainability
  • require low/no maintenance after that first round of updates

iammane August 27, 2022, 12:17pm #147

It’s pretty much my go to for production on iPad, and believe it or not, GarageBand too. Everyone slags on GarageBand but I don’t know why, I think it’s because they just assume you jam a bunch of live loops on a track and call it a day. You can do that, but it also handles recording real instruments admirably, it hosts AUs and the built in touch instruments sound REALLY good (especially in a mix).

Gadget is the CLOSEST you will get to a stand-alone iOS version of Reason (sorry Reason Compact). You can automate any knob. You can record audio. You can dial in your own sounds across a multitude of different instruments.

Now for the bad.

The sequencer, like you noted, takes a bit of work to get used to. It’s not even like Live, clip based yes but you can’t mix and match clips from different lanes. Lane 1 will play through, and then Lane 2, moving down down down. Personally, I hate it. I had to just bend to its will to get anything done and once I accepted it for what it was I got stuff done.

I would NOT use Gadget for handling recorded audio, unless you’re importing pre-cut loops. It’s a total nightmare. You can only see 16 bars of a sample, but for stuff like vocals spanning 16+ bars you either need to chop em up or, you can load a long sample, but you will have no control or visibility over it past 16 bars lol.

There is a mixer but oh my god, it leaves a lot to be desired imo. It’s very sub menu-ey, not all instrument effects are applicable across different gadgets or as insert effects (which is REALLY annoying).

It’s a unique offering. It’s got to be a loss leader because all new updates have been in the form of rompler packs for their Fairbanks instrument, which is fine I guess that’s how the market is.

I suspect it’s a way to get people in the door cheap and hooked on the KORG sound, then get them to buy a synthesizer. And I mean whatever that’s a valid strategy.

So yeah, that’s my hot take I guess. Tl;dr there’s A LOT of tweakability under the hood, but the weird mix bus and sequencer does take a bit of getting used to. You can of course drive it with other stuff (I hooked up Nanostudio 2 to it at one point, works fine) - but then you’re juggling two apps and it’s annoying as hell.
