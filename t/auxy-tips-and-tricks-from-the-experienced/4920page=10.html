Toveii April 21, 2020, 7:23pm #189

Okay here’s a tip from me. Take advantage of the gain and shelf mechanics in Volume. Idk if it’s just me, but I from what I’ve seen, it’s not used as much. Probably because of confusion. If you’re not sure how to use it, this is how I do it in my ambient/house tracks. Anyways, say here for example that you have a loop of a 4 chord with two different layered saws (could also be pads or piano). So then you have a lead on top of those saws. The saws are too much in the high frequencies, which causes your lead to be muffled. Well, good thing we can fix it. Go to the high and shelf mechanics in Volume of the saw instruments. The only thing you will move is the “High Gain”. So now, you want to move the High Gain bar to the left at most anywhere from -4.0dB to 0dB. Any lower than that will filter out the saw which causes to lose its brightness. Okay great! You’ve just cut off unwanted frequencies for the saws, and the lead has popped out more. Now, we will also change the dB High Gain for your lead. So, go to your volume lead instrument. Of course, since a lead is always in the high frequencies, you need to move the High Gain bar to the right anywhere from 6.0dB to 12.0dB. Now you’ve just cut off low frequencies for the lead. I can hear the mixing in your project is off the charts right now. Hopefully my explanation was specific and understandable lol. That’s all I have for now. LAYER YOUR SNARES AND LEADS.
