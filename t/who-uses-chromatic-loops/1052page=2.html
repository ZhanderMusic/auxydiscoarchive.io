Auxilium:

It’s hitting those wrong unplanned notes that have led to some of my best (imo) ideas. By having access to more possible ‘mistakes’, you also increase the number of ‘good’ possibilities — and could end up taking a piece in an exciting new direction you never expected it to go. (That’s where I think it can actually have a real impact on your music, for better or worse, without you even realising it.)

I can see the attraction of having a scale set out for you, and there’s still so much you can do with that ‘limitation’, but it’s a little too much like colouring inside the lines/painting by numbers, and tbh, lazy, for my tastes.

 
I think @GioMakyo’s advice is good, for those unsure about using chromatic scales from the get-go…

Try programming something in minor mode then switching it to chromatic and try moving one of the notes up or down half a step, and you’ll start seeing the possibilities.

 
And, then when 12 notes are too clean for you, there’s detuning and ‘microtonal’.

Auxy August 7, 2017, 3:24pm #26

The reason we started out with the scale mode as default was that we wanted to use the limited screen space efficiently and most popular songs (pop etc.) always stay in scale and very rarely break out into different keys or modulate between major/minor etc. One issue with the current design is that it can be hard to make sure that you’re actually starting out in the right scale. I.e. you might start a melody and then you have to turn on chromatic to get to certain notes, and most of the time this is because you’re actually not in the key that the app is currently set to.
