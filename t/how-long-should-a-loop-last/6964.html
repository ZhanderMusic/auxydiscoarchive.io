Akyut January 29, 2018, 3:53pm #2

At most, I keep my loops to 8 bars long. It’s enough space to have fun with variation and drum-work without it getting dull, and it leaves room to build on top of in the next set of loops. Melodic sections are around 32 bars total (8 for the buildup) and 16 bars total for the drop.

The key is to layer, and introduce more melodies on top of those already present. Not too much of course, but enough to constantly be introducing something new and interesting to the listener.

akabillposters January 29, 2018, 4:35pm #10
enby:

Also, how patient are you with the dynamics of a track?

I’m more patient than most – which is actually to say, that I’ll enjoy a second sweep of a loop, as I’ll be able to pick different things out from the first time. This means I’ll gain a deeper appreciation of the detail and subtlety in the track.

I’m guilty of allowing things to ‘drag on’ more than they sometimes should – but that’s for the same reason above.

That said, even on textural pieces, you should consider introducing small changes at least every 8 bars. Doesn’t have to be dramatic.
The styles I generally work in lend themselves to evolving the sounds, using filtering to add movement.

Of course, one factor is tempo. If you have a particularly slow tempo, south of ~75, then 8 bars can feel like a long time.

It depends on how you’re using your bars, what sort of song you’re writing, and overall how you are choosing to structure your song.

Produk:
