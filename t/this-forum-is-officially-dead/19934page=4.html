ThoughtfulNoise November 12, 2020, 5:13am #74

None of what I mentioned relates to the quality of the music that can be made using Auxy or the enthusiasm we might have for it.

The fact that some very good tracks* can be made using Auxy doesn’t take away the fact that it has its limits. Those limits mean that certain musical ideas are simply not possible - or are so difficult to work around in Auxy that it becomes a bad choice of tool for creators wanting to work with ideas even slightly outside the mainstream (or many mainstream ideas not supported within Auxy).

* There is still a significant gap in the audio ‘finish’ of Auxy tracks compared to pro tracks. The soundspace in Auxy feels very limited compared to professionally produced tracks — especially in the stereo space. Maybe it’s the quality of the source sounds. Maybe it’s the quality of the effects or built-in ‘mastering’. Either way, it’s a noticeable characteristic and limitation of Auxy.

Anyhoo…

The main theme of my comments was that with more attention paid to the Auxy creators, including this community, the Auxy team would have a deep well of content to pull on — and put to use as marketing content. Limitless free marketing content in the form of Auxy tracks and Auxy creator stories.

After all, what better way to market your music creation app than by using (read: supporting and promoting) the music created with it and the creators using it — all of which they can access freely (and eagerly from the perspective of the creators).

In my view, Auxy doesn’t deserve to be more profitable, because they’re willingly not doing many of the easy and obvious things that companies seeking to improve profitability do — the main one being marketing.

 

The other reason this Auxy forum will likely continue to struggle is because members are generally too self-serving. Posting your own music far outnumbers any other single type of post, while the amount of engagement each posted track gets here is getting lower and lower.

This forum is largely becoming a dumping ground for links to our tracks. Somewhere to seed our SC listens, rather than a community that we all want to productively engage with — or learn from, or contribute to. (Simply being a cheerleader for the app isn’t enough to sustain a healthy, worthwhile community.)

Feedback on tracks is generally shallow and uncritical. That’s great for beginners and people seeking small affirmations from online strangers, but not really helpful to creators who genuinely want to develop and grow.

Speaking as one of only a few members actively posting about production or branding and promotion tips, I suspect there are fewer still who actively read those shared posts — and close to zero here that actually follow any of the advice given in those shared articles and videos.
(I’m as guilty as the next person on that score.)

Lastly, or perhaps consequently, this forum skews heavily towards beginners, while more experienced and more serious/dedicated Auxy creators typically move on to other places. That means that there are few advanced creative role models here, because this forum has very little to offer them in return.

(As the saying goes… If you’re the smartest person in the room, find another room.)

 
Imo, all of that is what’s limiting this community’s sense of identity and purpose, and ultimately, its ability to thrive.

Auxy engaging with Auxy music creators, and supporting the Auxy community while also helping themselves by actively leveraging/promoting Auxy tracks for marketing would be a great way to revive and re-engage this community in a way that benefits both Auxy and the user community.

I wasn’t really looking to comment on what seems to be the 8th iteration of beating the “forum is dead” horse, but I believe it’s worth pointing out that comments like this and ones made stemming from similar mindsets are likely a large part of why the forum (and the app / business as a whole) is in the state it is today (even more so than the so called “not optimistic” voices around the forum).

While I appreciate you trying to be an advocate for Lenberg and his team, what you might not realize is that by covering up and glossing over the rather obvious issues in Auxy’s leadership both in the areas of software development and marketing (to name a couple), you are actually hurting Auxy’s future as a whole as it does not leave room for constructive discussion let alone improvement. Not to mention that the majority of what people say around here as far as constructive criticism for Auxy is for the most part based on valid points.

So, to summarize:

It’s not about being part of an active form it’s about being a creator of music. – Being a (good) creator of music is not mutually exclusive and is in fact largely dependent on being part of an active forum of like minded and passionate fellow creators.

The community has grown s(t)ale because people who are not optimistic dampen the vibe. – Actually, it’s more likely grown stale because of a negative feedback loop of: complete lack of interaction from the developers / forum officials -> those with knowledge and experience to offer leaving / becoming inactive -> traffic being redirected to almost exclusively self promotion -> people complaining about lack of activity -> developers making a brief reappearance before disappearing again -> etc. etc.

So many instruments and everyone asking for more instruments, but have you used all of the instruments yet? – This is roughly the equivalent of Tim Cook going on stage at Apple’s WWDC and announcing that there will be no new updates to Apple’s software because users have not yet used every single feature of the previous update. I’m reasonably confident that you can see the flaw in that.

There are some musicians who have only ever had a piano in their life and they have composed great music. We have no excuse. We thankfully have a lot of sounds. – The majority of Auxy users are not coming in with the intention of being classical pianists, and beyond that, sound requests make up a small fraction of feature requests / general comments about Auxy (some of which transcend the app entirely). Nothing really more to say here beyond it being a case of (unintentional) tunnel vision.

In summary of summary:

Positivity and optimism is great, but not at the expense of ignoring / skewing what’s in front of you for sake of painting a prettier picture of reality.
