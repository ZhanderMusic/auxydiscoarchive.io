Auxy August 28, 2018, 2:01pm #65

Just want to add some comments, which I might also bring up in a separate thread eventually:

  • Thanks for the kind words @DJ_La_Rocca! I’m really glad we’ve been able to inspire and educate you. This is part of our core mission and it’s fantastic to see that “Auxy works” in this regard. I also want to say that I’m impressed by your hard work and the way you’ve been developing your tracks over time. In addition, you’ve been a supporter of the community and helped push other producers forward. Thanks!

  • I think it’s awesome to see that you have set high goals for your musical journey and we fully support getting a DAW to further develop your production skills. You should always use the tools that help you make the best music. That’s what matters the most. Just watch out for letting too many options get in the way of your creativity though.

  • Auxy was never meant to be a “one app to rule them all” type of product. Yes, we do want to make a professional product that gives people the power to create “real” music just using their phones, but it will always be the case that different musicians will use different tools to make their art. Very few producers use one tool only and we’re extremely happy to hear that Auxy is an important piece of the toolbox for many producers. A recent example was a note from Sketchy Bongo who “laid the foundation” of his new album Dream Lab 9 on Auxy, which you can hear if you listen to it.

  • It seems like different people have different thoughts on what the forum is about. I like the comments above about it being a place for producers, regardless of whether you use Auxy alone or combined with a DAW. Or Medly for that matter. Hah! I think it’s a community for music makers who happen to “love and use Auxy” but happy to get your input on how to formulate that better.

Will open this thread some more in case people have some more thoughts to share…

iammane August 28, 2018, 2:26pm #69

I think that because the workflow of iOS is kind of garbage (and it’s gotten exponentially better, but it’s still rough) people (myself included) are looking to keep things in the box as much as possible. As soon as you start needing to involve too many apps and crazy workarounds the workflow ends up taking more time than just writing music, which is what I want to do.

For people who aren’t mobile centric, it’s all good - any tool that can export stems and midi will work great. For people who are exclusively mobile it’s a much tougher game - we need more flexible features. Auxy comes so, so close to everything I need. The sound is great. Sample import is a game changer. Longer sample import, more effects, a basic synth and some more effects and I’d be all in again.

I feel like I’m rambling and maybe getting off topic. Curious to see what others think as well.
