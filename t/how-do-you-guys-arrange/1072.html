Tarekith July 25, 2017, 9:52pm #3

I get a basic groove going first, just one pattern in one scene with a bunch of instruments and drums. Once I get a really solid groove going that I like, I duplicate all the patterns into a new scene. Then in the second set of patterns, I start making changes to what I already had. Remove some (or a lot of ) notes, add some notes, use a new melody line, make everything stacatto, whatever. Once I get something there I like, I duplicate all the patterns again. Rinse, repeat.

Once I get about 8-10 scenes worths of all these different variations, I go back and play them back to back in different orders, trying to work out the song structure. This is where things get messy for me in Auxy though I have to admit. Once I get a song structure together out of all those pattern variations, I want to see if visually progress one pattern after the other in order as it plays. And usually the patterns are all over the place in relation to each other once I have a song structure figured out. So generally at this point I take a deep breath and carefully put all the patterns in the right order so the song plays back correctly, then adjust the scenes to trigger the patterns in their new, correct playback order.
