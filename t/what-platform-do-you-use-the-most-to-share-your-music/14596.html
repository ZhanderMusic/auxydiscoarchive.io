Arimyth March 15, 2019, 12:17am #5

Realistically, if you mean “platforms” as in outlets where people can actually stream / purchase your music, I doubt most people here go beyond SoundCloud / BandCamp / the standard distributed services (Spotify, iTunes, etc).

Since you brought up social media, I do want to stress that having artist pages for social media can be very beneficial to your overall brand and building a loyal following. I’ve personally stayed off social media until very recently just because I don’t like how busy it all is and what a time suck it can be, but I also recognize the upside to having relevant pages on various popular platforms. So if you have a few minutes to spare here and there, I’d recommend hopping on some of the more popular platforms to reach more people than you would be able to by limiting yourself just to one.
