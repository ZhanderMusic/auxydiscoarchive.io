NickElle January 19, 2018, 6:05pm #24

I name tracks on how the track feels.
That’s why it’s hard for me to name an instrumental song. It’s hard to put the track’s vibes into a creative, good title.

It’s easy when I sing for my tracks though. I just use the hook of my vocals as the title. But, it is hard to write the lyrics. I usually write about what’s going on in my life. For example, I wrote an EP off of a breakup I had. It was a Deep House/Tech House EP so it was pretty deep, and the lyrics were about the journey of my relationship with that person.

Ps. The EP is not out yet. It’s been my best project so far, so I want it to have a good impact when I release it.

Cheddr January 21, 2018, 12:09pm #33

Either

  • take a nap listening to the song. Then what is literally the first thing that pops into your head when you are partially awake

  • words that would fit the rhythm of the melody. Or what lyrics could be to the hook if I had vocals

  • use the first letters from the auto generated title and use that as a starting off point for word association to fit the mood of the song. For a made up example: Moody cow —> more clouds -> Mostly overcast, or maybe rain, or must be grey.
