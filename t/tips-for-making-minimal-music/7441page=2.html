TheRealJFalc:

Downtempo is pretty hard to define.

One only needs to look at exemplar artists to develop an understanding.

Zero 7, Kruder & Dorfmeister, Thievery Corporation, …

 

I subscribe to the definition provided by Ishkur on downtempo music, and that is electronic music not designed for dancing

But, that’s an over-simplification. Much of it is danceable (e.g. Tosca). It’s just not club-oriented. Although, even that said, bands like Koop (who were on the verge between Downtempo and Nu-Jazz) were popular in the Latin dance nightclubs.

At its core, it’s a chilled, smooth, soulful groove, subtly jazz, subtly funky. Sometimes there’ll be Latin influences (e.g. Thievery Corp. and Ilya), sometimes hip-hop influences (as with K&D).
Bands like Oi Va Voi will do their own take on it, blending Yiddish/Jewish music into the mix, to great effect.
