akabillposters February 10, 2018, 8:53am #7
NickElle:

Minimal is a sub-genre of Techno.

* ahem * Small point of order…

‘Minimal techno’ is a sub-genre of Techno.

Minimal / minimalist as a music genre predates Techno.
Ref: Terry Riley, Philip Glass, Steve Reich, …

 
Let’s not forget there’s a world outside - and a history before - modern electronic music.
Heck, there’s a vibrant and fascinating history of electronic music that predates techno, of which Terry Riley forms a significant milestone for his minimalist works.

 
-

@NotMiles

Richie Hawtin / Plastikman is indeed a great / archetypal example of minimal techno.

Fwiw, other forms of minimalism in electronic/EDM include tracks like Nathan Fake - The Sky Was Pink (James Holden Remix) (2004), which is often referred to as an early minimal (EDM) track.

 
Beyond that, if you’re serious, I’d recommend trying to get your head around ‘modality’ in music (as opposed to ‘tonality’).

en.m.wikipedia.org

Mode (music)

In the theory of Western music, a mode is a type of musical scale coupled with a set of characteristic melodic behaviors. Musical modes have been a part of western musical thought since the Middle Ages, and were inspired by the theory of ancient Greek music. The name mode is derived from the Latin word modus, "measure, standard, manner, way, size, limit of quantity, method" (Powers 2001, Introduction; OED). Regarding the concept of mode as applied to pitch relationships generally, Harold S. Powe...

I’d also recommend checking out ‘modal jazz’.
(Chances are, as you dig, you’ll recognise many of the jazz classics that were written using modality as the framework.)

 
The (glib) takeaway is that you simply riff on a simple structure, phrase or harmonic framework, and use variance to create movement. Think ‘texture’, not ‘tone’.

 
I’d also strongly recommend checking out works by some of the progenitors of minimal / minimalist music…

Terry Riley - Rainbow in Curved Air (1969)
Philip Glass - Glassworks (1981)
Steve Reich - Electric Counterpoint (1987)

You’d be doing yourself a favour by developing an appreciation of these artists/pieces.
They can be a bit of an acquired taste, but understanding and enjoying their music can enrich your life.
