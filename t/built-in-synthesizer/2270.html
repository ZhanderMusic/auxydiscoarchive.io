I think it would be great for users, but possibly not so great for the business model, which currently relies on selling soundpacks because users don’t have the tools to generate sounds from scratch themselves.

DH0:

had a dream where the new soundpack was one fully customizable synth.

Could impact future sales of soundpacks.

That said, it may be that this section of the market – the non-pro, ‘serious hobbyist’ – might be the one market segment where that would work.
Enough users might not get into the synth*, and so might still find themselves falling back on buying ready-made soundpacks, and tweaking from there.

The same point could probably be made about adding a sampler.

 
* It’s not actually so simple to get a fresh, usable range of great sounds out when you’re starting from scratch. There’s a bit of a learning curve to synthesis.

Keep in mind also that one of the driving principles of Auxy is not to be an ‘anything goes’ music production tool/instrument/mini-DAW – but one aimed at making (quality) music production more accessible by making it simpler. This means a full-featured synth is less likely.

 
Just thinking out loud. Could be wrong.

Auxy September 11, 2017, 7:20pm #9

This is something we’ve been thinking about and it could make sense for specific cases, e.g. we could perhaps design a simple (in its controls) little bass synth. There is already a powerful synth running in the background. We use it to make the sounds and, to simplify, you could say that it’s a combination of a sampler and Serum (i.e. Serum the commercially available plugin). It has lots of knobs so making everything available would be more chaotic than useful. I also think it’s quite hard to make sounds that good and unique. It won’t be a simple as giving people a lot of knobs and get better sounding tracks in return unfortunately.
