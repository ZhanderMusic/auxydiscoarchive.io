akabillposters February 19, 2018, 12:59pm #31

I’ve zero experience with the music industry, but I actively read and research to understand the industry better.
I like to know what I’m getting into.

And perhaps age and my work (I work in the creative parts of digital marketing, which often involves pitching) allows me to know what to look for in an offer and judge it in terms of an investment.

 
I asked the person to give me some outlines about what I got for the money and assessed it against what I understand about the market.

I didn’t necessarily ask the right questions first time (still learning). Even though he answered the questions that I asked, he didn’t offer up any of the info that I would expect someone with a good offer to mention.

He didn’t mention monthly plays on the playlist*, number of shares via the playlist, etc… – which would hint at what kind of ‘reach’ and exposure my track might get.

He didn’t mention a benchmark sales figure based on the best selling artist on the label.

* I made sure to look at the number of plays of tracks on there, included the most played. All those I checked on the playlist had fewer than 1000 plays (Spotify doesn’t state the exact number if it’s lower than 1000)

If the plays and sales numbers were good, he’d mention them. The fact that he didn’t was a flag.

 
He didn’t mention whether he would promote it on the YouTube channel (30K+ subs), but only around 2000–5000 views per video.

 
Similarly, I looked around the label’s online presence, including social channels. Wasn’t much to find. No website and little to no social activity by the label.

The label was a sub-label and had no site of its own and no section on the parent label site.
The parent label site wasn’t available in English and was broken in parts.

Flag. Flag. Flag.

 
The very limited promotion on offer had no real exposure value, so this effectively boiled down to an overpriced distribution offer – to get one track up on the usual outlets.

And why do that, when I can do any number of tracks for free on something like Amuse.io?
