Question relating to one of my WIP tracks and copyright infringement

Production Talk
Nexarra August 18, 2018, 1:41am #1

Before I got at all serious about music production, I started to make one of the songs that ended up becoming part of my upcoming album around 4 years ago. I didn’t know really anything about music production, and I was using Apple loops in GarageBand because they were the only good thing I could find for free at the time. When I found Auxy, I decided I wanted to remake the song, revamp the lyrics, and make it more original. The only parts that I want to sound similar to the loops I put together in GarageBand are the two verses.

Sense I plan on making those parts sound so similar to the loops, I’m worried about facing copyright strikes or legal penalties for copyright infringement when I commercially distribute that song.

I am aware that Apple loops are royalty free, and can be used in a track that is sold commercially as long as single loops aren’t sold by themselves, but tracks using Apple loops cannot be monetized on YouTube, which I plan on using.

At the same time, I am also aware that while chords and instruments aren’t copyright-able, melodies are.

Because of this, I am not sure if I can safely use melodies from Apple loops that I near replicated in the verses of my WIP track in Auxy. Every other part of the song will be different and original, but I really like how the verses sounded in GarageBand. I will change the melodies if nobody knows with 100% confidence that I won’t run into issues with copyright and that I will be able to post that track on YouTube.
