akabillposters September 1, 2018, 9:03am #3

Really liked it.
Beautiful, simple refrain. Sparse and atmospheric.

A few thoughts…

  1. I think the percussion sound levels are a little higher than they should be. Just a little too present in the mix, imo.
    Or possibly shave off some brightness by dropping the LPF on them a little.

  2. At 1:24, I was anticipating a counter-melody to enter. Something light and airy, slightly arpeggiated, that wanders around a little, possibly busier, using every note-step as a brief counter to the sparseness of the main refrain.
    It lasts for a little while then

  3. I’d pull back the delay a little. It adds a ‘swing’* that maybe doesn’t work in the context of this simple piece.
    * You seem to double-down on the swing at 1:48 with the main piano chords adopting the swung feel, when emphasising only the downbeat on your main 3/4 pattern has worked so well up to that point.
    (Not sure if it’s in the pattern or the delay effect.)
    May be just a taste thing, but that took something away from the piece for me.

  4. Feels like it needs a little building up to the impact at 1:47. Not too protracted, just something to build the anticipation ahead of the impact/pause. Maybe use the bass note on the piano chord repeatedly and bring up the volume gradually.

  5. Additionally, I’d remove all the delay on the instruments underneath the impact for the duration of that impact. It will purify the moment, imo.

  6. I’d cut out the section 2:48–3:12.
    This would result in going from a ‘peak’ to a sudden stripped back section – heightening the emotional impact.
    Currently, you use the above section to start winding down, but I’d recommend trying the sudden option to see if it creates more emotional impact (e.g.).
