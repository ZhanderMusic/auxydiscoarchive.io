iammane May 3, 2018, 8:51am #3

I really like this, I agree with El though it’s a tad repetitive. Your drum programming is ON POINT, after trimming down some of the bars before the strings come in I’d LOVE to hear you just let loose on the drums at full 175bpm. You’re generating a lot of tension in what you have here and sonically it’s super interesting, it’s just a bit drawn out.

That first sweeper seemed loud, the rest seemed ok. Listening in the car so it’s not exactly the best environment. This has a great groove for sure, I think tighten it up and even if you wanted to wait to blast on the drums until after the last two scenes that’d be cool, you’ve built up the tension at that point.

If you were looking for processed breaks to cut up (ala Amen break, the Think break) check out raggajungle.biz. There are links to sample packs there, complete with loops, hit based samples etc. I was getting heavy into jungle production but I think I’m taking a different route now. However, I found these in the interim and wanted to share - it’s a great resource.

Looking forward to what you change! But you’ve got a real solid foundation here

Yeah, it’d be a bit of a pain, but you could import / copy&paste a sound to GarageBand and use the slice function in there. It’s going to be a chore though since GB only exports the whole track.

For chopping stuff up I use AudioShare, it’s a real swiss army knife of audio goodness. It’s not free though (I think it’s like $3.99? 4.99?). It’s worth it.

I know some folks also use AudioCopy (the Retronym’s branded app) - I have no experience with it though. That is free. There are probably others but those are the only ones I’m aware of
