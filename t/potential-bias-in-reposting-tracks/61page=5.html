akabillposters November 15, 2017, 10:13am #104
Southborne:

If you make great music they WILL pay attention.

Forgive for saying, but I’d say that statement seems somewhat naive. (The many thousands of fantastic musicians you or I will never hear about are testament that the route from ‘great music’ to attention and appreciation is by no means certain. That’s as true here as it is in the broader music world.)

“Great” by whose subjective criteria? The inherent problem with that is that it’s currently the judgment of a very limited few and their criteria for “great” isn’t at all clear.

It the Auxy team consider it purely a quality issue (and nothing to do with a genre bias), then there might be an opportunity for them to establish a thread sharing some thoughts on what makes a great track- ideally, while being as objective as possible.

At least then, those who would appreciate having their pieces reposted on the Auxy brand SC channel would have a clearer idea of what Auxy (the company) expects to see in any track they consider reposting or featuring.

 

My advice is to spend less time talking about how they need to change and spend more time producing and honing your skill and sound.

My earlier comments on that line of thinking

Of course, improving and developing one’s sound is its own reward. And anyone who’s relying on an Auxy repost for exposure probably has the wrong priorities.

Still, it behoves Auxy to either be more clear about any preferences they do have re: reposts/features and how that relates to their target market - or try a little harder to better represent the full spread of genres and styles that Auxy users are working in (so as to not leave chunks of users feeling ignored and disgruntled, but also to broaden market appeal).

It’s clear that Auxy is absolutely a great app for instrumental FB/EDM. But there’s value in Auxy doing more to let the world know that it’s good for even more.
