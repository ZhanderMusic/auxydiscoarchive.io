Auxy November 18, 2017, 11:18am #165

Here’s my general reflection on this discussion, which I feel is growing a bit out of proportion…

The biggest challenge when making something, like Auxy, is that you’re forced to make a lot of tough decisions. You have to decide whether some things should be like this or that. Whether some things should be or not. And you have to prioritize what to do, since you have limited resources.

Very few decisions are easy in the sense that all arguments point in the same direction. In most cases, you have to deal with the fact that there are totally valid arguments for and against your possible options. In turn, this means that whatever you choose to do, there will be valid arguments for the thing you decided not to do.

I often feel that I don’t have enough time to give people the full story about why we make certain decisions. I apologize for this and will work to get better at giving feedback!

At the same time, I feel that some people have a very hard time accepting when we make certain decisions and continue pushing the discussion ad infinitum. Here’s my general respons to that:

Yes, you’re making totally valid points! We have heard your feedback and we take it seriously. Thanks for sharing it! For reasons, that usually include data/facts/insights that you might not be aware of, we have decided to make a decision that goes somewhat against your reasoning. We hope that you can accept this and trust that we’re doing our best to act in everyone’s best interest.

Makes sense?
