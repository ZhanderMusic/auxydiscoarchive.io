akabillposters November 9, 2017, 7:43am #43

TBH, I don’t see much sign of them trying to represent the breadth of styles and genres. I believe personal taste and bias is overwhelming their choices when it comes to the Auxy SC account reposts (which, afaik, is currently the only avenue where they’re actively using music that’s #MadeWithAuxy to market the app to potential new users).
If they’re sincere about wanting to represent the breadth of styles and genres, they need to make more effort to distance their choices from their own personal taste - to make choices ‘as Auxy’ (a company), not as individuals with their own personal preferences.

Current biases are possibly already damaging both the community (as members/users feel ignored) and the success of the app itself as potential new users (who aren’t all about EDM/FB) pass by Auxy thinking it’s ‘not meant for them’.

Not a huge issue, but something that should be addressed, imo.

 
Alternatively, if it is intentionally and almost exclusively prioritised towards the mass-market of popular EDM genres because the belief is that the app will gain more users than you lose with that approach, be open about it.
(There’s nothing innately wrong about prioritising raw numbers to the exclusion of ‘breadth’. Perfectly valid and understandable business approach. The issue then becomes about whether it’s right or wrong to pay lip-service to topics such as ‘breadth’.)
