Well, I guess we all knew this one was going to be an essay…
Read or don’t read. S’up to y’all.

 

 
@Auxy

Firstly, thanks for taking the time to respond.
And both thanks and kudos to you for reopening the thread and trusting the discussion to bring value and clarity. :tip-of-the-hat:

 
I didn’t mean to suggest that every track in that thread would be checked out. Or that there is any sure-fire route to a repost on the Auxy SC channel.

 
There are gaps in styles of what’s reposted. You only need to take a reasonable look at the primary genres of what’s historically getting reposted (or just listen for the wubs).

When I took a semi-analytical look a month and some back, at the reposts over the previous three months (iirc), the numbers were extremely favouring FB/EDM, ~90-95%, again iirc.

 
I do understand and appreciate that it’s a drain on time that could be better spent on the app/service itself.
But, it’s part of Auxy marketing, so it also needs to be done well.

You mention that people are welcome to recommend tracks they feel are worthy of a repost. If that’s been the case up till now, that’s not been at all clear. The route from posting a track here, or on SC, to getting a repost has been quite opaque.

If it’s the case going forward, it’s worth clarifying whether you simply mean PM you here, or you’ll see stuff pop up in Auxy SC’s feed.

(On this point, I actually don’t understand why you’re not more keen and pro-active about showing the best quality and the best spread.)

 
That said, maybe there’s an opportunity to empower someone who’s more active in the community, who’s able to have a more pro-active view of what’s being shared, what’s attracting positive feedback and comments as well as reasonably good (e.g.) Play-to-Like or Play:Like:Repost ratios on SC – with a spread of genres and styles that truly reflects what the Auxy app is capable of supporting.

My impression of BSJ’s original thread was something like that, at least based on what he’d said at the start. But the link between BSJ and reposts on Auxy SC channel was never clear.

Of course, you’d brief the person as to what you consider good ‘quality’, after all, it’s your company and your brand.
(That said, I do think it would be pretty cool if you also shared your thoughts on ‘quality’ here too. Transparency on that issue would certainly help build trust and help reduce, if not actually prevent, concerns about the Auxy brand SC channel effectively being used as a personal account, and operating under the subjective biases of the app’s owner(s).)

* unless, of course, in your mind, Auxy brand ≤ Lenberg, which might raise other flags.

 

akabillposters
chunks of users feeling ignored and disgruntled

Who are they?

I’m not going to highlight people by name. Enough of them have already voiced a desire to see more variety for different reasons in this thread.

It’s worth noting that I wasn’t the one who created this thread (I’m certainly the most vocal about it now, but this concern was being raised before I’d even joined the forum. I just happened to notice the trend too – and spoke up about it (a lot)

Even so, I’m not the only one voicing a desire for more variety.
Some would like to see more variety for the sake of increasing their chances of being considered for a repost. (The appearance is still that there’s favouritism in genre and/or makers.)
While others would like it as evidence that there’s no genre preference that might be preventing their output from being considered for reposts.

 
The problem with identifying worthy tracks

I understand it’s not simple to identify what’s truly worthy of a repost, free from the distorting lens of pre-existing popularity.

One issue with using the number of Reposts a track gets as a criteria for whether Auxy SC then reposts it, is that popular Auxians(?) will naturally accumulate a higher proportion of reposts amongst their own following, which increases their visibility to you – which you then repost — which boosts their visibility — which increases their Following — which increases…

It’s a similar problem to ‘Sorting by popularity’. After a certain time, it become self-fulfilling as the ones that were most popular yesterday are the ones getting the most promotion today. It makes it increasingly difficult to get noticed and ‘break into’ that top tier.

To ensure a good spread, it’s important to have some way of allowing tracks from those with smaller followings, but which have good Play:Like:Repost ratios (or whatever) to get visibility.

Another option might be a member’s poll. (I know, some will hate the idea of Y/A poll, but this one might actually do some real good.
If there was some way to have a ‘people’s choice’ poll, whereby Auxians with smaller followings could be nominated and then voted on, leading to an automatic repost on the Auxy SC channel. It could potentially be that the Auxians themselves are nominated (and the poll winner gets to choose which of their tracks is reposted) or more simply, members nominate tracks (max one per nominee).

Just spit-balling now.

 

reposting is, compared to other things we do, something we do a bit more casually and for fun.

With great power comes…

The issue here is that the impact and value to those receiving the attention is potentially greater than you might anticipate. When a repost on Auxy SC, done for fun by you, can potentially kickstart or boost exposure for an Auxy producer, then it’s perhaps understandable that some potentially on the receiving end of that exposure think of it a bit more seriously.

As mentioned earlier in this post, Auxy reposts effectively serve as a part of Auxy marketing. That fact should highlight it as something more purposeful than simply ‘casual fun’.

 
All things to consider, imo.

 
And now, having said it all to the one person who actually matters in this issue, I’m done. (Huzzah!
(I’m happy to add thoughts if wanted, either here or in PM.)
