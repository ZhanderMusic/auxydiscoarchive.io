What gets me with Gobekli Tepe is its age and symbolism, and the fact that it’ll take another century to uncover all of the circles, whereas Stonehenge is just one, and far newer. I just read that its builders were evidently farmers who had travelled from near Turkey, so maybe there’s a direct lineage between the two. The pyramids in Egypt (and those crazy-huge/ultra-precise boxes at Saqqara - I devoted a track to them), and the earthquake-proof architecture in Peru are a different mystery in terms of their engineering prowess. I saw a little in Mexico as well. I want to learn how they did all that so I can build megaliths in my yard (seriously).

I recently happened to observe that a given star’s twinkling is completely different over the distance between two eyes, which surprised me. While it wouldn’t be fun to have to fend off dire wolves and such, it would sure be nice to be able to devote one’s being to simple and true observation and realization, finding ways to pass along knowledge and awareness directly.

What Auxy is lacking the most for me, in terms of quality, is proper EQ – either individual instruments or final mastering. The defaults don’t work well for most of the genres I like to play with and I end up low-passing things to reduce harshness, but then the clarity suffers.
