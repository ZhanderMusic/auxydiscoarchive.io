TheRealJFalc November 16, 2018, 6:25pm #12

I’d also recommend getting some plugins for it, although apparently Live’s selection of built-in stock plugins are far superior to FL Studio’s shrug

Specifically, I’d recommend Serum and The valhalla family of reverb plugins, specifically Room and Plate. Do keep in mind these are more of an investment for sure and since you were just given approximately 2000$ worth in birthday gifts, I’d say hold off on them until Christmas perhaps. Serum has a vast library of presets, some developed by 7 Skies even. That’s where the Destiny pack comes from. Not to mention that, according to Lenberg himself, Auxy’s in-house synthesizer was based off of the design of Serum. As for the valhalla reverb plugins, they are extra juicy ;3

I do not condone piracy of these VST’s, even though a lot of producer’s do. They are fantastic plugins that are absolutely worth the investment

Eswyn November 16, 2018, 7:26pm #13

Yeah, external VSTs are nice, but I wouldn’t recommend them for beginners. Especially since suite comes with its own built in plugins, I’d recommend learning the Ableton workflow and learning sound design with those first. Once you have an idea of what you’re doing, then I’d recommend trying external plugins. Also - if given the option… ALWAYS TRY BEFORE YOU BUY. I can’t stress this enough. Know EXACTLY what you’re buying, and try a demo when possible.
