Its not converting it its compressing it. Basically, when its compressed it compresses it into a bitrate that is usually lower than listening to it as a project. And so it crunches it into this bitrate which (most times) ends up making hi hats sound a little distorted and basically just makes the project not as good. Whereas, uncompressed will export it exactly how you hear it as a project.

Alyen November 1, 2017, 1:11am #10

@DontPartyWithGuns
Not what I mean, but if it you plan on getting your project mastered then it’s best to export uncompressed(which I’m assuming is .wav). Now it’s not necessary to export wav if you’re only going to upload it to a streaming platform, since (to my knowledge) I don’t know any who are streaming wave files. MP3 as I know of, only takes out inaudible information from the high & low ends. With that said, in a blind test, most people cannot tell the difference between mp3 and wav. Not only are the average person’s ear not sensitive enough to pick up the subtle differences, but they are always (likely) listening to it on cheap sound system(computer speaks, iPhone earplugs, etc.) Now if you’re a 20+ year pro mix master in a treated studio with $5k+ monitors… then maybe a difference can be heard.
