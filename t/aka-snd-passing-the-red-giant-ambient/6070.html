Trey:

the snap sounded like it was like a 32nd before the other melody

I purposely put it in an off-(off-)beat*. I wanted it to simply feel like a sound in the loop, with the same pacing, but outside the main (sense of) rhythm, rather than a percussive instrument - to avoid it taking the role of a snare or a clap, or a pulse starting each bar.

By putting it where it is, (imo) it gives the loop a more abstracted, organic foundation. Rhythmic, but not percussion, per se.

* It’s actually on 9of24th step, while all other parts use the standard 16ths pattern. It’s a pretty awkward (syncopation) slot, if you’re trying to discern the ‘1 and 2 and 3 and…’ rhythm. It’s one of the reasons I chose it and like it.
