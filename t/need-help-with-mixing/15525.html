honestly just experiment with different stuff until you like the way it sounds. there’s no one set way of mixing, it’s all up to personal preference. i tend to use a lot of low pass and high pass filters on my sounds to make sure there’s no clashing between frequencies. try that out and make sure you modify the volume on every instrument, don’t ever have anything turned all the way up.

El1011 May 18, 2019, 8:24pm #3

EQ is going to be your best friend when it comes to mixing. High/lowpass, boosting areas you want heard and lowering harsh frequencies or areas you dont want to hear.

As for a way to keep your tracks at a more manageable volume, lower the volume of all new tracks you make so that the instrument sits at around -12db and so that the master sits below 0db. Then you can adjust the individual volumes to get the track sounding how you want, and at the end, put a limiter on the master to help bring the track’s volume back up for export. at least that’s what i try to do.

bedlegs May 20, 2019, 6:09pm #10

I’ve got studio monitors now but don’t have a cable to plug my phone into the interface. So I actually haven’t released any music using them. I just stick to my phone and earbuds. I use the Apple wired earbuds, beats solo 2 I think, or a single Bluetooth speaker. For the mixing you just have to keep experimenting. Don’t compare your music to other works constantly. If you have a track you’re stuck on, try creating a separate section. Don’t be afraid to make it quite different. It’s okay for music to be unexpected. Don’t worry about how it sounds compared to the scenes before it, just start something else. Once you think you have a good thing going, try and work it into your song. I’ve had parts that I rearranged completely, thinking that it would be the end of the song, to putting it in the middle and creating a whole new ending. Look back at other tracks your stuck on. What parts do you like about them, what don’t you like. Can you copy the patterns and see if you can incorporate it into another song you have, or maybe even a new song with the old pattern. I know you can do it, and you know you can too. It’s alright if you need a break. Find inspiration in other things beside music if it helps keep your mind off of it for a little bit. Sometimes it can take a lot of time but you’ll get there.

Db909 May 21, 2019, 8:30pm #12

All great suggestions here. I would also suggest playing around with mixing at a low volume, as low as you can go, so that you can really pick out the transients/loudest parts. With these sitting together right, things will sound great turned back up. You may have to occasionally turn it up and down to get a handle on the bass. Also you can play around with setting the device to mono audio in the accessibility settings, think of this as just another way of limiting your point of view to really focus in on how things are sitting together. Mixing is how you craft the perception of the audio, so it can pay to play with your own perception like this.
