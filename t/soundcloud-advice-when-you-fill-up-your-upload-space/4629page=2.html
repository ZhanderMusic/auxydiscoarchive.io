akabillposters May 1, 2018, 3:49pm #34

Assuming you’re not considering upgrading to Pro/Pro Unlimited…

From the various reading I’ve done, the advice I’ve seen is generally…

You’re best option is to delete your worst-performing tracks; tracks that simply aren’t getting m/any plays each month.
Doing this on a regular basis, even without waiting to run low on space, is a good thing.

It’s better to periodically prune away your worst/least popular tracks, to make space for new, better material and raise the average performance of your account.
(Accounts/artists with universally good track performance are more likely to be respected and taken seriously, than accounts dotted with poorly performing tracks.)

Instinctively, it feels painful, but trust that the tracks you’ll be deleting aren’t actually undiscovered masterpieces that will make you $$$ if only the right person heared it.
They’re just the stuff we create on our journey to becoming better writers and producers.

Good, for their time. But, it’s not that time any more.

(although, you can always pillage them for their best parts in future, better, tracks)

 
Conversely, if everything is still performing well, then maybe it’s time to step up to Pro/Pro Unlimited.

 
I’m adopting a new approach myself.
If it isn’t good enough to put on Spotify, etc… and it isn’t a Demo that I’m making public in search of collaborators, I’m not putting it on SC either.

i.e. I won’t be using SC as my dumping ground for every idea.

That’ll cut down massively on SC uploads, bringing each account well within the free 3 hours.
I’d revert to a free account if it didn’t mean losing the ability to replace/update tracks.
