GioMakyo May 31, 2019, 6:31am #28

Make sure you have some sort of EQ that can give you a visual spectrum read out. Take a few sample EQ readings of tracks similar to the style you’re trying to make, and then dump your Auxy mix in there and make sure you haven’t overloaded the bass or the highs or something I don’t think visually using EQ is a good way to mix a track, but it is good to catch something that may be wrong. If you have really good monitor speakers, you won’t get any surprises, but for people mixing on smaller set ups, this is the way to go.

Personally, I find working in a 100% digital environment often leaves me with way more energy in the 10 K to 20 K region than is usually needed; a soft hi shelf often helps.
