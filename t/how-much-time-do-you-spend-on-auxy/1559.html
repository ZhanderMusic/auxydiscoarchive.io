brandnaqua August 23, 2017, 8:55am #5

I use it everyday, sometimes for hours and sometimes for just minutes every so often throughout the day. Guys i’m a photographer and one of the biggest parts of that job in addition to taking the photos was lots of editing. Not to be confused with retouching or color editing in this case. I mean just the portion of looking through and selecting the photos I liked from my photoshoots in an organized fashion and keeping the great ones while deleting the bad ones. Then retouching that was tons of work too, but basically guys I would sometimes at that phase over retouch. With music it’s different. It’s like sometimes you think something sounds really good and then you come back to it and quickly get annoyed. I would suggest, if you’re using it for very long periods at a time, to take breaks and come back with fresh ears to evaluate then continue. That helped with my photography vs my prior approach of just full on non-stop work. Now my photos are better and less stressful to work on because I take many breaks to get inspired and have fun doing other things in between. Also, with this music stuff I found that taking the coffee to cologne approach also helps. When you are searching for a new cologne at the store they often have coffee grounds they encourage you to smell in between scent checks. That’s to cleanse your sense of smell and strengthen your discretion on which one to get. Well let’s say you’re working on a house track, go to your favorite music streaming app and listen to heavy metal or maybe trap or hip hop, then come back to the house track you’re making with clean ears ready to determine whether you actually like the direction you’re going in or not. Anyway, i’m not like a super pro. I’m just a beginner, just thought i’d tell you guys that so music could stay fun for you guys and never feel like burdensome work. Always spice things up to stay excited, this is like a relationship lol
