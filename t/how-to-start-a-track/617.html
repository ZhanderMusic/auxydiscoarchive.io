There is no strict guideline, but chords are always the easiest to start with and the hardest to add after. But then again, it is always what is best for you. Once you have a good progression put down, you then follow them with a melody, typically starting and ending each measure with the root note of the chords. Once you have the most important pieces of your idea, you can start structuring a song. Don‚Äôt go by what you think, but by what you feel when structuring a song. Usually your instinct has better flow than what you are able to think in your head. If you feel that your song should start with a drum solo, go ahead. Don‚Äôt ever overthink it and decide against your instinct. And always take regular breaks, it will give you time to come up with fresh ideas and mitigate the effects of ear fatigue. Good luck!
