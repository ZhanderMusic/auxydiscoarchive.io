Hey, the chords were nice, but the tension was never resolved with the tonic (the tonic is the key of your song, so for example you were in F minor, so your tonic should be an F minor chord) You had the notes of the Fm chord, but you played them out separately and it only increased the energy that you had no way of removing. I see you used inverted chords and that added a nice touch, but all that is not useful if you cannot RESOLVE them, that was only one problem. The next major problem, was your use of the beat lines. Your whole project was not on beat. It was off by 1/16. The thicker black vertical lines are there so you can keep track of the rhythm. So, every time the playhead touches a black line, your head should move down to the rhythm and up in between them. The third minor problem, was your bass. Most of the time it should follow your chords, but in this case, it only repeated the Fm chord. That got boring quick since there was no progression to the song. I hope you use this criticism and keep working at it. This is not meant to discourage you, but said in hopes that you get better, and eventually start giving US tips hahaha. Make sure the whole audible spectrum is used. Don‚Äôt neglect your high and low frequencies. They have the most energy and make your drop LOUD. Reduce the volume on your chords or whatever is in the middle and turn up your bass and high end. Make sure you have a sub or something like it, and cymbals to every beat, preferably Charge‚Äôs as it has the best high frequencies, add some layers to your kick, something with a big thump to it, like North‚Äôs or Craft‚Äôs. Use some lush chords if you are going for something like future bass. Like OCTAVES apart chords. Don‚Äôt use much percussion other than claps or kicks, as they muddle your sounds if what you are going for is loudness. Make sure you turn down the volume on your buildup and that it is a decent length, no shorter than 4. ALWAYS have 1 bar of emptiness before the drop, with a sweep up. Make sure you duck some instruments to every beat to get people nodding their heads as well. And, that‚Äôs about it! Best of luck.
