Arimyth August 25, 2018, 9:46pm #19

I’d recommend investing in a decent art program, as it has many uses outside of creating cover art. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Affinity Designer (~$48) Like a better version of photoshop. A bit of a learning curve but if you invest about 15 minutes into it you can easily get the hang of making professional album art among countless other things.

  • Pixelmator (~$20) Not as full featured as Affinity Designer but you can do some nice things with it for less than half the price, including well designed album art.

  • Trigraphy (~$25 for a subscription) Essentially just adds filters to your photos. I wouldn’t recommend it as you can’t really go anywhere beyond photo editing.

  • Canva (Free for most uses) I used to use this service but stopped because of the limitations. You may as well invest in real software if you’re willing to pay the fee to remove those limitations.

I wouldn’t recommend photoshop unless you plan on getting into digital design beyond cover art as it has a very high price tag and a somewhat steep learning curve to it. Paint.NET is an okay choice as well but is windows exclusive.

(For what it’s worth, I do all my designing including cover art in Affinity Designer currently. I’d definitely recommend it.)

Here’s my process:

  • First, I visit unsplash.com and download a royalty-free photo to use. They’ve got some super high-quality pictures. (I hope to use pictures that I take one day, but not yet.)
  • After that, I go to Trigraphy (iOS) and blend it with some snazzy modern-art effects.
  • I finish it off with Over (also iOS) by adding a track name in one of their amazing fonts.
  • Occasionally, I will also add my logo.

Hope this was helpful!
