I think what Nick is partly talking about is that the Auxy community can be quite insular, perhaps showing a little too much deference to achievements within the world of Auxy - a tiny bubble that few outside the bubble know about and even fewer care about - rather than considering broader habits and practices, and how they might reflect on what even the most successful Auxy users have so far achieved.

To some extent, that goes for the app too. It’s isolating itself - and again, to some extent, its users - by design. It’s built specifically to be a standalone app (a walled garden) that doesn’t integrate well with larger workflows. This encourages users to think of it as the A to Z of their music creation process. That thinking might not be the right one for creating our best music.

Or, to put it another way, there’s probably a reason why you don’t hear about established artists using Auxy exclusively.

Auxy does some pretty impressive things, for sure. And, there are users making some impressive music. But whether something that’s ‘impressive’ inside the Auxy community actually translates to ‘impressive’ outside the Auxy community, well, that’s still a question at this point.

I think the more aware we are of the standards expected outside the Auxy community, the more we improve our chances of achieving real success.
