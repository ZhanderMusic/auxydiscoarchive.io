Arimyth March 20, 2019, 7:34pm #8

I see this question more often than one might expect and I think it’s because there’s a lot of confusion around what a collective actually is and why they’re important.

A collective is essentially a joint artist union that has a similar function to a label, but without necessarily being a registered company. i.e., it’s a brand that releases music from other people, often a roster, and has promotional capabilities beyond each individual member (hence the “collective” nature of it).

Depending on how long you’ve been around the community you’ve probably seen the rise and fall of many different collectives / labels. More often than not, this is due to insufficient planning beforehand on what running one would actually entail. Spoiler alert, running a collective is a fair amount of work if you’re doing it yourself (talent scouting, distribution, copyright protection, artwork, promotion, etc.)

It’s also a good idea to have a specific purpose when making a collective; what vibe are you trying to create on that page? What kind of genres do you want to curate? Is there an extra function you want it to serve? For example, melo. only accepts chill music, vvvvv cultivates darker, more bass heavy tunes, Ridgeline is dedicated to cataloging a collection of creative commons protected and easy to use music for content creators, Auxy Collective focuses on greater community interaction by only releasing megacollaborations, etc. etc.

Also make sure that you’re in a position (music wise) where you have the ability to gain sufficient traction on a track you release. Ordinarily it wouldn’t matter so much and it’d just be up to your commitment level, but when you start bringing other people into the picture and releasing their tracks, you have a greater level of responsibility to ensure those tracks do well and reach a certain audience.

tl;dr Naming, artwork, and tracks to release are only a small part of building / running a collective, and if you’re aware of that and know the full extent to what it entails, go for it.
