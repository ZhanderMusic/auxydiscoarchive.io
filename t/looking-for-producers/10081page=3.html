It’s worth looking into.
I’ve worked with both stock music and commissioned music for projects.

‘Freelance composer/producer for hire’ may not be the coolest musical pathway, but it can be reasonably lucrative if you can develop relationships with ad agencies, etc…
(Obviously, you’ll get more commissions if you’re not working in only one genre.)

And it’s still ‘making music for a living’, which can’t be bad.

Not typically huge figures, per commission, but £1000-2000 for a 90-sec piece wasn’t unusual.

Even if only as a side-project, the figures are good for some substantial extra income - certainly enough to pay for this musical habit we have.

Why not include music on other commercial platforms? Because coming down with a good licence deal for everyone included would be too complicated, since the artists music would be too valuable, to much of ”their darlings”. I just look at myself: if my own song, under the name of Simon M Harris, was to be used somewhere I would like a waaaay better deal than selling it off cheap as the music in a stock music store does. I would want a much more complicated license deal ensuring tht my music was only used for things that benefited myself and my brand, since it would all be connected straight back to me.
That’s how I feel about it, and so I want to seperate artists ”real” music from thing, and only have music intensed ONLY for this purpose.
