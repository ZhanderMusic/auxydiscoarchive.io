Bassment_Chemists January 7, 2018, 3:05pm #249

https://soundcloud.com/dylan-quinn-smith/auxy-o-beat-the-clock-25

I wrote this in memory of my father:
My mother had just left him. He was lonely and looking to fill the empty void in his heart. He turned towards the comfort of a dark crowd, and started smoking crack. I was finishing up my time in the union and just needed one more year to get vested and get my retirement. I knew something was a mis when I got a late pocket dial from him and could hear nefarious goings ons in the background. Things took a turn for the worse that summer as I worked 6 12 hour days a week and he was hardly home, nor I. I’d check the answering machine and it was his work wondering where he was, or he’d be passed out through Monday night sleeping off the bender of his weekend.
He lost the house to debt, his lively hood, his self worth. I felt I was not able to do enough, and started smoking myself. Not drugs, in the common sense of the word, but tobacco is just as addicting if not more.
He has since bounced back from everything after nearly a decade. Payed off all his debt he acquired from supporting that dark crowd and his own personal demons. We have grown much closer because of it. I still struggle with cigarettes and have made a vow to quit before my next birthday.
This is what emotional experience I drew from when writing this song. I hope y’all enjoy it, as much as it was cathartic for me to write it. At one point I felt myself welling up… But I’ve cried all those tears along time ago, and honestly it is just a piece of my emotional past that has made me a stronger man.

Good luck to everyone who has made an entry in this 25th beat the clock. And thank you @Auxy and company for giving me an outlet for my musical therapy.
