ThoughtfulNoise June 21, 2020, 2:01pm #43

I’ve actually got more use out of the DX100 pack than I expected. Certainly more than I’ve been getting out of recent packs.

It sounded a bit cheesy on first audition, as I’m not usually a fan of FM synthesis. But I’ve warmed to it.

That said, it’s mostly been the same few sounds. I’ve been using Soft Horns and Good Vibes sounds heavily. Both work really well in the retro, jazz-oriented music I’ve been creating recently.

Soft Horns is a great, warm sound, especially when used for rich, extended chords in the mid-to-low register, with subtle LPF automation to slowly swell from muted* to subtly buzzy, and the chorus turned up to spread it all out and give it a richer, airy, unison feel.

* Manages to sound good at lower LPF settings; muted, but clean, rather than muffled.
