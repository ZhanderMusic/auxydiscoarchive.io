BlaMeese:

If my parents were present when I signed up

No, because the agreement effectively forms a service contract between Amuse and the person named in the account. The person named as the account holder needs to meet the requirements in the T&Cs.
As you are below the age limit in their T&Cs, you can’t legally be the named account holder.

Your parents can’t bestow the legal right upon you to sign-up to an 18 and over legal agreement.
Just the same as if your parents let you drink alcohol at 16, that doesn’t make it legal.

It’s not ‘Parental Comsent Required’, it’s 18 and over. It’s not your parents’ permission that counts. It’s Amuse’s. And they don’t give you permission if you’re under 18.

Technically speaking, by endorsing it, they’ve signed off on you breaking (civil) law. As your legal guardians, that may actually be a criminal act.

Now, before I get too Chicken Little…

The usual workaround would be that the account is in an adult’s name, but the artist name and tracks would come from you. Amuse would pay-out to the account holder, but if this is handled via something like PayPal, then the account holder would simply use your PayPal email in the Amuse profile - or forward any payouts to you.
