akabillposters February 10, 2018, 9:15pm #28

I’ve got this lo-fi hippety-hoppety piece…

https://soundcloud.com/akasnd/sketch-34-lofi-beats-no1

 
Imported samples…

  • Kick (sampled from an R&B track)
  • Snare (sampled from a jazz track)
  • ‘Donk’ sound (percussive sound from Freesound.org, iirc)
  • Guitar loop* (used in intro. Doubled-up)
  • ‘Buzz bass’ bassline, built from a cut up Moog sample
  • Vinyl crackle (2 instances, one with a clear click bookends the piece to suggest the needle being put on / taken off the record, and a looping segment)
  • ‘A-Yo’ vocal sample
  • ‘Wasn’t that lovely’ vocal sample at end

* It was actually this guitar that inspired the track. It’s the first couple of bars from a live version of a track of a band I’d just stumbled on earlier that day. It just seemed perfect for a lo-fi hip-hop piece (which I’d been wanting to try for a while).
(I’ve still not listened to the rest of the track yet. I switched to making my track as soon as I heard those opening bars.)

 
Library sounds…

  • Closed Hi-Hat
  • Claps (3 different versions, layered and shifted)
  • Vintage Keys (additionally layered up with Bee and Fairy near the end)
  • Gravel layered as sub underneath buzz bass sample
