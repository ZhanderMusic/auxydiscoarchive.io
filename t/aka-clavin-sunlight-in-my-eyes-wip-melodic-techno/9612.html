Thanks, @Produk.

 
Re: Drums

I want them to feel a little like they’re on rails. An engaging, but mostly unobtrusive rhythm groove that just keeps going and gives everything a bit of a shuffle.

 
Re: Stuttering impact

I’ll have a think about pulling that stuttering impact back a little, though I don’t want to smooth everything over.

Conversely, I think the 2:15 – 3:19 section needs to step up a bit more. The chorus (2:22 – 2:50) is meant as a an incomplete step up in energy, because the real peak is that 2:15 – 3:19 section (also appears 4:30 – 4:58).

akabillposters May 2, 2018, 11:37am #15

Thanks, @blakkaz
Was hoping you’d take a look, seeing as Techno’s one of your things.

I started playing with a simple, two note bassline, padded it out with some simulated harmonics, which then led to the ‘ooh’ harmonies.
From that point, I wanted something synthetic for a bit of contrast, so added the skipping plucky synth notes. The vibe about where I wanted to take it sprung from that simple juxtaposition.

I wanted to go with the Techno influence as I thought the contrast was more interesting than simply making it a regular Ibiza-style Trance or Deep House track. I wanted something that felt like an authentic Techno remix of an Electronica/Electro-Pop crossover song.

I’ve reworked and rearranged it a bit since, mostly to bring the duration down, but I’m not sure if I’ve sacrificed the spirit of what I was trying to achieve – something that’s part song, part standalone (Summer) techno piece – with the non-vocal parts being sustained enough to for listeners to enjoy it as they would a Summer club instrumental – dare I say ‘anthem’.

Thinking I possibly need to juice up the Techno aspect a touch more – maybe through the drums.
Just needs a little more edge, more ‘bite’.

I’ll through up a link to a new revision in the next day or so. Would be good to get a second round of feedback, to know if I’m improving it – or going backwards.
