akabillposters March 31, 2018, 2:11pm #8
NotMiles:

I may have some issues releasing Elixirs. In the middle part of the song I included riffs from some video game songs. Would I just have to give credit to the original artist or is there something else I need to do?

If you’ve recreated them, using the melodies, but not direct samples, then you’d need the same kind of publishing clearance as a cover version.
The good news is, with cover versions, it’s fairly standard to simply make the cover, then buy a yearly licence before releasing it*. It’s fairly straight-forward, and it’s pretty unusual for any cover version to be blocked.

Your distributor should have some info about covers – e.g.…
Amuse: May I upload a Cover?
Distrokid: Can I Upload Cover Songs? * Distrokid will handle it all, including royalty payments for $12/year per cover track.

As it’s not a full cover, it may work out less to license it.

 

On the topic of releasing songs to stores, I feel that I may have some issues releasing Elixirs.

Technically, by putting it up on SC, you have released it. The fact that you don’t directly generate income from your SC tracks doesn’t change that it’s still an unlicensed use of someone else’s composition – i.e. a publishing rights infringement.

…fwiw
