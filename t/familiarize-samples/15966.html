Arimyth June 23, 2019, 8:37pm #5

A .wav is just an uncompressed audio file. Most DAWs / recording applications have the option to export in the .wav format.

I’d recommend the app “AudioShare” to trim .wav (or any other type of audio) files on your mobile device. It’s pretty much a must have for any sort of mobile workflow in my opinion and allows you to export the trimmed .wav directly to your iCloud Auxy samples folder.

GioMakyo July 2, 2019, 7:22am #15

Of course if you’re ripping off YouTube, even if you eventually convert, the actual sound quality of the file will still be crappy MP3 in most cases. It’s hard to recommend what process to take unless you give some idea of what sort of samples you’re looking to incorporate. Sampling off vinyl, CD, DVD, or even recording with a good mic are all possibilities. Ripping works too, but is generally better for samples that are not going to be a really major part of your track. It’s convenient, but you get better sound quality using other techniques.
