akabillposters January 29, 2018, 12:10am #9

Thanks for the input…

Pretty sure the term ‘electro-jazz’ predates ‘electro-swing’ by at least two decades, as I remember hearing/using it used back in the mid/late ‘90s, synonymously with ‘Jazztronic(a) and Nu Jazz, that was popular in bar/lounge culture around that time. (Afaik, the term ‘electro-swing’ itself is a relatively recent term, coined only a few years ago.)
Electro-jazz has also sometimes been used synonymously with ‘Space Jazz’, which is synth-influenced jazz funk, but with a more psychedelic flavour.

That said, Nu Jazz was most often a blend of house and bebop/smooth jazz (e.g. St Germain) or Latin jazz forms and house (e.g. De Phazz).
I don’t use Nu Jazz because my stuff, while influenced by the same jazz era, pulls its other influence more from early/retro synth music and sensibilities; not strictly dance-oriented.

The use of talkbox/vocoder vocals also pulls it further away from ‘swing’ towards jazz-funk (e.g. Zapp!).

 
The problem that I have with using the tag ‘electro-swing’ is that, stylistically, it predominantly refers back to pre-1950s jazz - specifically ‘big band’ and ‘ragtime’ (e.g. Caravan Palace). The fusion that is now called ‘electro-swing’ was also around back then, but it was just another of a handful of styles that came under the umbrella term jazztronic(a)/nu jazz.

 
The jazz forms that I like the most and which influence me the most are acid-jazz, hard bop (bebop) and smooth jazz, all of which came after swing and which have quite different sensibilities to Swing. (Swing/Big Band is my least favourite form. Either that or Ragtime, which is the other common form of jazz used in ‘electro-swing’. Naturally, this means that I’m also not a fan of electro-swing/neoswing.)

akabillposters January 29, 2018, 8:04am #19

It’s gonna get harder to identify “Auxy Tracks”, especially when there’s no Auxy tags. Which is great for Auxy.

It’s certainly good that Auxy-made music is becoming less and less distinguishable from music made using more traditional tools. But, without the tags, Auxy might be missing out on a lot of organic promotion and ‘credit’.

I actually stopped using the #auxy tag on my stuff (Andy actually removed it from all my SC tracks), even though it’s typically 100% #MadeWithAuxy (or very close to it). I just feel/felt that it might be limiting how people think of my music, judging/enjoying it as ‘Auxy music’, rather than simply (hopefully good) music.

Not sure what not using the #auxy tag does to my chances of getting some early traction via the Auxy community incl. an Auxy SC repost, which is always worth having.

The new features mean that my ‘demos’ and sketches will stay close to 100% for a good while longer as I can now get even closer to what I have in mind when exploring ideas and developing pieces.

Maybe as I’ve recently marked everything promising as either ‘sketch’ or ‘demo’ - effectively being clear that it’s not a ‘final release’ - I’m less concerned with how using the #auxy tag might reflect on the music itself, and start using/re-adding it again.

akabillposters January 29, 2018, 8:26am #20
MisterMaster:

Love dem vocals samples.

Thanks. Been a fan of talkbox for as long as I can remember, listening to a lot of funk growing up, especially Roger Troutman/Zapp!, who helped popularise it in the mid-‘80s. Always loved that sound.

I knew I wanted to use some talkbox/vocoder in my music at some point. The thought popped back into my head yesterday, as I was fiddling with this piece. With that in place, it all came together pretty quickly.

I had intended to upload just an 8-bar loop as a preview, but started adding a little structure to what I already had and, from there, I had this piece ‘done’ in under an hour.

Goes to show how a piece can suddenly click when you have the right vocal in place.

I like to think that the talkbox vocals were a cool, unexpected choice.

 
Adding vocals/human voice really adds a dimension to a piece, lifting it musically and making it much more engaging.
It’s a component that’s been missing from almost all Auxy music, but that may well change now.
There’s so much good stuff #MadeWithAuxy that could really be boosted with the right vocal - whether that’s samples, chops or a recorded vocal track.

There are some ‘ooohs’ in a layer, which add some nice texture. I’d like to try replacing them with some gospel alternatives and then bring them slightly up in the character of the track. I think talkbox over gospel harmony chords could be a killer sound and give the piece a more ‘pressing’ atmosphere.

I certainly plan to keep using vocals much more often.

 

(The new Tonsil sound is nice, and I’ve used it as layering in this piece, but it may be the first and last time I use it. As the first - and only- ‘voice’ sound in Auxy, it’s gonna get rinsed by the Auxy community and exhausted as an option pretty quickly, becoming another of those recognisable ‘Auxy sounds’ that I generally try to stay clear of.)
