akabillposters June 17, 2018, 5:15am #12

Agreed.
There can be charm in ‘cheap’ emulations. They have their own character which can be interesting.

Whether it’s every non-sample-based drum machine ever or synth presets for Brass, Strings, etc… they all have their merits. But it’s the timbral variance of real instruments* and natural sounds that have proved hard to emulate convincingly. It’s very easy to stray into less than convincing territory.

If that’s something the OP would like to have, the @AzureOnyx’s suggestion might do the trick. If not, then 3rd-party samples or custom field recordings are the way to go.

 
(* It’s partly why I have an issue with most Auxy piano (and guitar) pieces. The lack of timbral variance (and performance nuance) makes most pieces starkly unconvincing to my ears. It takes me out of the ‘listening to a piano’ mode and quickly throws me back into the ‘listening to Auxy’ mode. It more or less conspicuous depending on the style and genre.)

Anyhoo…
