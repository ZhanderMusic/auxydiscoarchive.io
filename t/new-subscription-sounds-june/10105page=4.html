akabillposters June 4, 2018, 6:58am #77

I think your points get to the heart of why I’m no longer a music snob about mainstream music like Pop.

For better or worse, Pop songs routinely ‘connect’ with listeners on a level that massively heightens enjoyment.
Sure, it often relies on ‘tricks’ such as simplicity, ‘catchy’ hooks and repetition. Those tricks often lean on certain psychological impulses known to bring about satisfying pleasure responses.

The question for artists/musicians then becomes - why are you/we making music?

Is it to be maximally popular entertainment?
Is it to stimulate other emotional and intellectual responses beyond pleasure?
Is it to bring about social change?
Is it pure self-expression, isolated from concerns about audiences?
Is it a balance of self-expression, stimulating recognition and appreciation of your expressions and experiences - in a way that connects with large numbers of people?

None of these are bad or illegitimate reasons, imo, because even the most superficial answer still aims to bring about pleasure in as many people as possible.

And that’s not something I’m ready to condemn.

 
For me, the outcome is the point.

While I particularly appreciate the ability and performance of a talented singer, I don’t generally care whether a record uses some auto-tuning.
If the final product works, then I’m satisfied.

I’m not particularly concerned with the musical authenticity of a track. To me, auto-tune is just another tool to help craft the package. If using it creates a better track, I’m fine with that.

 

There’s a BIG difference between writing something for mainstream music and just writing out a check to cash in at the bank…

I’m not convinced there’s any functional difference at all.
If you’re writing something for mainstream appeal, then at the very worst you’re necessarily aiming to trigger pleasure responses in many people.

The cash is a (generally desirable) consequence of successfully managing to trigger pleasure responses in large numbers of people.

 

caveat...

This is not to say that I enjoy all pop music. I personally can’t stand most of it - certainly the stuff aimed at younger audiences. I find certain other types of music more satisfying and more emotionally and intellectually stimulating.

My taste is not superior or more authentic than that of fans of (insert latest teen/tween pop sensation). It’s just different.

Even so, there are still sections within the genre of Pop that stray into my area of appreciation.

An earlier example - Banks - is electronic + r&b filtered through a pop writing sensibility.
I personally find that very appealing.
