Sure, they do a substantial amount of things to do as a two person developer team. However, part of running a company, especially one with its own community, involves maintaining good contact with its paying clients. None of us as far as I know is completely sure about what goes on behind the scenes, but there are several plausible candidates:

blakkaz:

This is definitely possible, but with iOS 12 still in relatively early beta I can’t imagine it being that much of a time consumer and high priority. Graye:

But we can’t judge the guy too much, idk why he’s been silent, and it could be something more personal,

We do know that he has family to take care of and support, which is completely understandable. However there are managers of much larger and more time consuming companies that still manage to keep in contact with their clients and provide even minor updates to them. Complete radio silence especially now with all the methods of easy communication is a dangerous area to go into because it can hurt and permanently diminish reputation.

Regardless of the reason, it’s objectively the case that a product that involves payment, especially frequently recurring payments (i.e. continued and active support of said product) warrants consistent communication from backstage to its community or set of customers. This doesn’t mean expecting them to pump out new soundpacks faster or provide new updates to the app with greater frequency. Even 30 seconds to put out something like “Hey, we’re really busy, but we’re continuing to improve the app!” would be well received and provide a lot of reassurance to many people.
