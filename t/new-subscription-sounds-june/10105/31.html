TheRealJFalc May 27, 2018, 4:04am #35
akabillposters:

I think Melodic Percussion has good variety, whereas Boom Basses and Signature Keys (and, to a lesser extent, Vintage Keys) added options without really adding much distinct variety.

Ok, time for an explanation as to why this is the case!

Ok, for Melodic Percussion, each instrument sampled was different. This, however, is not the case for the Keys. Vintage Keys are based off of two recordings, and same for the Signature Keys. I can go more into detail on the signature keys, as that’s what I studied the most recently.

The signature keys are based off of recordings of a Grand Piano (Origin, Bold, and three of the others) as well as an Upright Piano (Noir, Body and three of the others as well). This is why many of them sound similar. Each different option has a different level of compression attached to each, some have a bit of distortion (Noir and Origin both have no distortion) and two of them have a phaser attached to them (Ghost and I believe Face). So you get very similar sound coming from each.

As a side note, the reason why the boom basses sound similar should be obvious. They’re all 808’s, but none of them really change much in terms of their attack. Some have a slightly stronger attack but mostly their differences are so minute it’s hard to notice.

I can’t say much on the vintage pianos, but I believe in the original AP description they were based off of recordings of a Mark 1 and 200A.
