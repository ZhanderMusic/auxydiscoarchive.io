MrSerpent November 19, 2019, 4:19am #25

In the past 3 years that I’ve used auxy, there are a handful of sounds that I’ve just never tired of. They’ve helped me with countless tracks and have a myriad of uses.

  • Flag is THE ideal synthwave bass. Probably my favorite bass out of the OG sounds, it’s just so versatile and useful when it comes to retro vibes.

  • This is more of a recent one but mask is just an exceptional lead. Extremely versatile and can be used from everything from arps to notes to chords. I’ve had to restrict myself from using this one too often in the past few weeks because I just can’t help myself it’s so amazing.

  • Hour is probably my favorite pad Auxy has to offer because of its versatility. It can emulate so many emotions I try to go for in my tracks and I’ve used it constantly without tire for well over 2 years because of how many different it can serve as.

  • Coin is a really nice airy lead that has served me extremely well over the past few years. It can sound really beautiful when used right, and really gives a floaty sensation to whatever track I use it in. (the vivrant pack is just a godsend in every respect)

  • I’ve used Pepper in pretty much everything ever since I purchased AP 003 in early-mid 2017. Amazing bass for more of a live feel but it’s also really really mellow and perfect for lofi. I always fall back to this bass, probably my favorite bass of all time.

Aadam December 4, 2019, 1:23pm #29

This is hard to say, these are not in order, but here are some sounds that I like a lot.

For a trance/techno producer, here is what I like personally.

  • Riff, this is my number one BY FAR. Maybe not by far, maybe silk, dirt, otter, claw, and beam might come close, but this is still my number one. It works anywhere from Future Bass to Trance.

  • This is one of the older sounds, more commonly used, but you all have to admit you probably use Silk as a primary pad in most of your songs. It has so much variation in it.

  • Beam is one of the greatest basses of all time. It is hard to use, but once it comes out, it is exactly what you want it to be. If you use it correctly, you come out with an epic intense bass.

  • Otter, I have to admit, is my favorite original sound. I use it in more genres than I do with Riff. You just have to pitch it down to get all of its flavor out.

  • Claw, on my list of my top 3 basses, comes out really intense, but in a different way. It can fit in really dark songs (Daft Punk style), and also as an electric guitar.

  • Doodle is Auxy’s biggest tool for sure. If you make a correct rhythm, you can fit it right before a drop, following with a correct pattern of drums. The features it has is like no other instrument.

  • You probably think this is a regular boom bass, but this is more than you think. Dirt is one of the best sounds, and it brings back memories to me. Tweaking the distortion will help.

  • This instruments name is killer long, House Music Is Back. HMIB also has a ton of variation, and I am working on a project using my custom sound, Scissor, which is made out of HMIB.

  • Climb, has even more variation than doodle. You can start with it as a pluck, then change it to a string, and then make it a pad, but the pad version might not be so tasty.

  • Fairy, a very relaxing sound, that fits in any genre. In my classical song, “Lights That Shine” on the second half, you can see how well fairy fits.

  • Although I barely use this sound, Mental is one of the best sounds, maybe top 15 at least. I was trying to make a video game song, and I made a really good rhythm. Tweak it right, though.

  • Blob, this sound is probably what you call, a Dubstep Instrument. It has further use than you think, though. This has less settings, but however, it still sounds like it has been changed a lot.

  • Again, this sound also sounds like a dubstep instrument, but can fit in other places without tweaking. You just gotta make sure that Horror is used correctly.

  • Racer, one sound I use to like. As Auxy 6.0 came around, I stopped liking it because the tweaking effects are no longer good IMO. If you try really hard, it comes out good as usual.

  • Slope, also can be used anywhere as a buzzy-bass in the background. It sounds better with max shape. At one part, you shall take away the other instruments and have a Slope Solo.

  • Intruder, AGAIN a dubstep sound. Can I stop talking about dubstep? YEESH. Anyways, it also fits as a regular, but very very intense lead in a regular drop.

  • Spray, one of the Demo sounds. However, you can make this fit wherever you want it to. I do not have much to say about this one.

  • Blanket, Both a pad, and a lead, if you take the attack off. I like trance and techno, so for techno, I take off the attack, and tweak it so it fits in with the genre.

  • Nobody usually mentions this, but I actually have to say that Sweet Spring is my favorite sweet pad IMO. It has so much flavor, even though it is really hard to tweak.

  • It is surprising that much more people use Sweet Saw, probably because you can tweak it a lot more. It is good as always, and comes useful in a ton of places.

  • Pressure, kind of similar to slope. This is one of the older sounds that might actually fit in some kind of trance songs. It is a pretty tasty bass IMO.

  • Not a lot of people use this either, but flag has so much tweaking settings. I primarily use this instrument in my Drum and Bass songs.

  • Subway, that also comes with a ton of tweaking settings. It is also used in dark and intense songs. If you try these out yourself, you will see why I said “intense”.

  • Strain is very intense, but I like the explosion when you max the bitcrusher distortions crush and set its rate to zero, and compress is full. However, the distortion is disturbing IMO.

  • Cube. I really wish I could write an entire article here, but sadly, I cannot. This is one of the basses that hopped up to my top 3 list. Just put up the shape—and distortion, you’ll see why.

Many more! I will add them soon!
