‘Royalty-free’ just means you don’t pay for each individual instance that you use it.

xavior March 10, 2018, 10:44am #74

Unfortunately, no.

Most photos aren’t actually ‘posted’ to Google. Google is a search engine that brings up millions of results (from websites to news articles to images and videos, etc.) based on what you look up. So, a lot of those results are just pulled from their home sites and displayed, in case it might be something you’re looking for. You can see this here:

unicorn example.jpg2965×1778 668 KB

I specifically looked up “unicorn png”, because I’m a dork and I like silly examples. If I liked this image, even though it’s a .png file with a transparent background, I cannot freely use it. It comes from this Unicorn Wiki site. It’s not mine, and it wasn’t intended to be. As @akabillposters said, there are lots of sites that will give you access to free-to-use images. But, just taking images from Google is not the way to go, unless you can confirm that the image is 100% free-to-use.
