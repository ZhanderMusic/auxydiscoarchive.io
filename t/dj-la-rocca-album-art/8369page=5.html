akabillposters March 10, 2018, 11:43am #95

Not quite.

Parody is when you’re mimicking something comically. (I’m assuming @DJ_La_Rocca’s tutorial videos are sincere, genuine attempts to share how to make certain genres.)

A good example of parody might be those ‘How to make every [insert genre here] track in 2 minutes’ videos. They’re tongue-in-cheek, making fun of the formulaic style of certain genres. Still, they generally mimicking the general style rather than duplicating specific tracks.

If you’re mimicking the general style and not actually using notes from one of their tracks, you’re on safe ground, as you can’t copyright (or protect) a ‘style’.

If you’re using actual notes, then it’s a publishing rights issue, but you could be covered under Fair Use on the grounds of it being for ‘educational purposes’.

That’s not quite accurate.

There is the Publishing issue.
The performance (‘the recording’) and the composition are both protected individually under copyright law.

Typically speaking, Publishing rights owners don’t have - or don’t apply - prohibitive license over compositions. You simply make your cover version and pay the required Publishing royalties.

(It’s rare that Publishing rights holders will veto the use. It only really ever happens when the person requesting the rights (wanting to make the cover version) or the intended use is somehow deeply objectionable to the rights owner.)
