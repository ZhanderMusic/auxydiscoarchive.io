Not my genre, but I like it.
Good energy.

Quick thoughts…

Consider dropping that dubsteppy growl down an octave, until the bassline kicks in. It’ll keep the mood quite low profile by avoiding the mids and highs. Moving it up an octave once the bassline takes over low-end duty will add interest and energy.

I actually like the reduced production, but have you given any thought to having a lead melody come in at some point?

If you’re keep it simple, I’d consider adding movement (automation) to that repeating synth melody, to help keep things sounding fresh.

Also, on the same point, don’t be afraid to drop the repeating synth melody out for parts of the longer track. While it’s part of the backbone of the track, it’s good to rest it, giving the track and the audience some contrast - and then reintroduce it when you want to return to the main theme.
