stereofield June 16, 2019, 11:35am #82

I’d say it was a mistake to say that the composition section is about “removing the automation”. That sorta makes it sound like the composition is whatever is put into the note editor, but in reality as @Peregrihn said, the composition is much richer and fuller than just a bunch of notes. Some automation is related to the composition, and some is related to the sound design. Some is related to both. We can’t just judge it as a specific science (judging the means and not the ends, as @blakkaz said) — that would be really ineffective, and it’s not fair to the artists who submitted. We will judge the composition based on the composition.

That said, this does not mean we are not going to judge the composition separate from the execution. One of the reasons we separate those two categories is because a compositional concept can be GREAT, but then end up sounding bad because of bad execution. On the other hand, you can have a great sounding track that has a very boring composition. These are two distinct categories. Judging them separately is not “taking apart” the song — it’s more about judging the individual aspects of the end result of a song.

So in summary, it is a good idea for us to judge composition and execution separately, BUT we will not be judging based on the means that you used to get to the end result.
