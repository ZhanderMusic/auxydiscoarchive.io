This has to do with how Auxy renders scenes and some sounds having a pre-delay before some things, such as a ducker or automation, can have any impact on them. Some sounds are worse than others. It‚Äôs all about transients and how they get processed, and unfortunately there‚Äôs not really a good way to handle some of these things without planning around them.

Just another great ‚Äúfeature‚Äù of Auxy, putting problematic experiences out of the user‚Äôs control. If you really want to handle them in a render out, a good way is to make sure the automation starts one scene early, and has the volume set to zero. That often helps with getting things to behave as they should. However, you can‚Äôt easily deal with sounds that have awful transient compression, such as Spray. In those instances, simply avoiding the sound is the best bet.
