akabillposters March 14, 2018, 10:09am #30
El1011:

unless you have the explicit permission of the original artist/label, your remix is technically illegal (which is why it saying/believe remixes aren’t all copyrighted)

I’d assumed you were talking about official remixes.

Even so, even if it’s a ‘bootleg remix’ (unofficial, unsanctioned) then it’s still protected by copyright.
The elements from the original release will be copyrighted to the original artist/label/publisher* and the new production elements will be copyrighted to the remixer.

* and that’s a matter for the original copyright holder and the remixer to sort out

A remix would typically involve both copyright of the original recording (i.e. if using direct samples, acapellas, etc…) as well as publishing (e.g. if using the original melodies, etc…, but recreating them with new instruments/sounds).

 

unless you’re trying to make some $$ with it, not many people are gonna bother to get involved or sue u over it.

That’s kinda true. Your mix would still be a copyright infringement, regardless of whether it’s a ‘commercial release’ or not.
But as you say, it’s less likely that they’ll come after you. But, that’s down to the choices of the copyright holder, rather than the law.

It’s worth keeping in mind that even if you aren’t monetising it (e.g. not monetising the YT vid, or doing is as part of a free mixtape for self-promotion), it’s still a commercial promotion item, so still an attempt to generate commercial value. Just downstream.

If you’ve made money, and how much, mostly impacts on ‘damages’ — which is the additional money you can be sued for for ‘lost revenue’ as well as punitive amounts — if that’s the route the copyright holder chooses to take.

It’s also worth keeping in mind how easy it is for social platforms to identify infringing material because of smart algorithms. It’s likely that the DMCA claims are batch processed these days.

The fact that something’s still on YT doesn’t mean it hasn’t been hit with a DMCA. It could just mean that all the monetisation has been claimed by the copyright holders of the original material. Even if that’s only pennies, automation makes it easier to claim that too.
