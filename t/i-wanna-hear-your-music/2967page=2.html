I’m not that good at giving advice(I a lot more people better fit), but I’ll do my best.
IMO, artists naturally get “better” over time. Things that can help make the process quicker is by learning from others who are doing the style you would like to do(watching someone compose a song from start to finish, remaking a song from your target genre, listen to songs and try to see how many different sounds/instruments you can hear. Just to name a few).
I would say…

1.) Be picky about the sounds you use. Think of the instruments you use in your tracks as the clothing of it, you wouldn’t want to put green socks on with a brown shirt. In your tracks Vienna Jodie and Listen, the bass sounds like it doesn’t fit. I say bass but I don’t know if it’s a bass instrument being played at a high octave or an electronic keyboard. I would replace it or better yet you could layer it.

2.) Layer: There are many techniques to layering alongside definitions. For now, I’m speaking of simply copying a pattern from one instrument to another that is on the opposite frequency spectrum. So in the case of your tracks I mentioned, I would copy the instrument and change the instrument(of the copied version) to a bass instrument. Then play around with the volume of it, you want it to be easily heard but not demanding all the attention. This should given your melody sound more body. But you’ll want to turn the high pass on that instrument up a little bit, reason is so that the low frequencies of it won’t clash with any bass instrument you may bring in and kick 808s.

3.) Fullness: Simply need to create some noise that plays throughout your track but can’t be heard very easily. Try using white noise/sweeper, give it one a one bar note, play it with the quietest part of your track and lower the volume of it until you can’t hear it, but you should be able to hear it when you play it by itself, if can’t hear it by itself then turn it up till you can. (I’m quite clumsy with words so let me know if this point is confusing and I’ll just make a video about it.

4.) Listen with fresh ears. Should be something all producers do. When you complete a track, you should always stop listening to it and come back the next day and listen to it. Ear fatigue can numb your ears too many different sounds.

Not sure if you already knew these or not. But I hope any of it helps. Keep at it and I’ll be following.
