def like the hardcore super saw lead.

But i think 2 things feedback wise:

  1. there should be more of a break between each of the sections of your tracks. The drop didnt really have anything leading up to it to build up tension other than a riser right before it. it’s fine if you dont want to create a section for a full buildup, but you could do something like cut out everything but the melody for like the last 1/2 bar before the drop. This way the drop hits a lot harder and there’s that transition between the 2 sections.
  • example: https://soundcloud.com/boxplot/thedreamershotel(it does have a build up too, but it’s pretty short)
  1. i also think for dnb/happy hardcore, you could have a stronger bass going on. most of the track feels like mids/highs which is fine for melodic obviously, but i just thought it felt a bit weak. doesn’t have to be a massive bass, but just give the low end some more presence to round out the overall sound.
