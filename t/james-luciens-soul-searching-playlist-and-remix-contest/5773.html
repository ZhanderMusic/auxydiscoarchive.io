Hello everyone!

tl;dr: I'm hosting a remix contest of some of my older works that aren't perfect but are good in their own way. Peruse the block of text at your own risk.

Lately, I’ve been doing some rethinking about how I should do my music, especially after running out of minutes on Soundcloud. I went back and listened to all my old tracks, and I realized that I had lost something when I began working on my Re/Mastered project. I had become so focused on music and making each song perfect that I forgot what music is truly about. It’s not always about perfection, but creating something you were happy with. Most of the tracks from that EP lacked that in my eyes, because I had become so focused on perfecting them I ended up hurting myself with self-doubt and fear of failure.

This is a remix contest designed to show that not everything needs to be perfect. I’ve compiled a playlist of five tracks which I believed emphasize this aspect of genuineness in music. None of these songs are perfect, and one of them has audio issues and sounds extra quiet (wildlands cough). But, I’ve taken these tracks and produced stems with them for the purpose of showing that music isn’t always about making the perfect beat, the perfect hook, the perfect bridge or the perfect wubs. It’s about expression and showing something unique, which I believe all of these tracks do.

The soul-searching playlist can be found here. There are five tracks: Project HAUS, I4, Wildlands, Fumbler and B-Stract.

Here are the rules for this contest:

  1. Please title your remix in the following format:
    "James Lucien - [Song of Choice] ([Your Name] [Optional Remix Title] Mix).
    An example would be “James Lucien - Fumbler (James Lucien’s VIP Mix)”
    These are NOT simply VIP mixes, this is just an example since I like to give my remixes a title of sorts.
  2. Please also use my logo somewhere in your cover art. It’s in the details below.
Logo is in here

jameslogo.jpg1537×2048 194 KB

  1. Remix as few or as many of the songs as you like. I won’t limit you from expressing your music.

  2. Retain a little bit of the song you are remixing. Make sure it’s actually a remix xD

  3. Have fun, and don’t be discouraged. Some of these tracks are really weird and don’t always make sense as they are completely different from what usually is produced. But don’t let that scare you! Make it your own unique thing, and remember to have fun with it.
