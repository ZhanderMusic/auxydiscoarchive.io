Cheddr March 30, 2018, 1:19pm #23

It’s all good. I played guitar for 20 years completely self taught. None of this really made sense to me until recently.

No one needs to know any of this to use Auxy and that is the beautiful thing.

It is helpful (but far from necessary) to know this stuff for these three reasons.

1 understand to get ideas down faster with less hunting and pecking

2 communicate these ideas with others using a common language.

3 once you get this it can open up a whole world of other topics that can help you understand why something in particular sounds good. Which is either interesting on its own or helpful if you are trying to make something else sound good.

Yes and no, it depends on what style you’re going for, if you’re following traditional harmonic progressions, and many other things. As a rule of thumb, yes you want it to be the root note and to change it up you use inversions to make the gaps not as large between notes sometimes, or if you repeat a chord you can invert it to progress the melody a bit.
