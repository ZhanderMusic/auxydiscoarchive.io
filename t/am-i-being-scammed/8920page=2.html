It does seem like a deeply flawed growth model, but I stopped short of calling it a scam, or even shady, because I think they probably believe it will or at least could work.

As the promo copy suggests, they believe they’re creating the future of the music ‘industry’.

And they might be right. As mentioned earlier, artists sponsored directly by patrons is a model that is being discussed and tried as a possible future, more sustainable model. The obvious examples are YouTube creators who also have Patreon/Premium audiences.
(I’d be surprised if Andrew Huang was making most of his money from his music sales/streams.)

Those discussions typically centre on each artist having their own (e.g.) Patreon audience.
RocketYou seems to be trying that at scale as a middleman between content creators and advertisers/patrons.

It’s actually very similar to the YouTube model. YouTube has the platform and the relationship with advertisers. Users have the content that attracts viewers/eyeballs.

RocketYou is right at the start of that journey, when it’s still a bit catch 22/chicken and egg.
I suspect they don’t have a load of investment capital to attract artists with guaranteed pay-outs while they build the audience through large marketing campaigns.

 
Something to keep in mind…

Right now, you can’t monetise a YT channel with fewer than 1000 subs and 10K views (in the preceding 12 months). YT doesn’t offer/promise you any views. It’s up to you to build your audience.

YouTube was small (and horrendously unprofitable) once. Afaik, it’s still not clear if YT is making a decent profit as a company yet.
