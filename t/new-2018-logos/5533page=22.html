akabillposters January 18, 2018, 6:08am #447
DJ_La_Rocca:

I like it so it doesn’t matter what others think

iammane:

it’s most important if you like it.

(This might be overkill for this place, but it’s a good thing to understand early on…)

Now, obviously, it’s great and ideal that you like the logo that represents you. Ideally, both you and your target audience understand and appreciate, even love, how you identify yourself visually as a ‘brand’.

But, when your opinion doesn’t align with what your target audience thinks, then it might come as a surprise to many, but your own opinion might not be the most important one.

If you’re venturing into the wonderful world of marketing, it’s a mindset worth appreciating early…

When It Comes To Marketing, You Are Not Your Customer
You are not your customer
You are like your users

 
Now, of course, you’re free to ignore any and all different opinions or (strangely) dismiss unfavourable audience feedback as “haters”. After all, you are the client.
And, you might have some insight that’ll prove you were right (and all those “haters” were wrong) in the end. That could happen. Unlikely, but possible.

Your opinion is certainly the most powerful. Generally speaking, I t just might not the most relevant or useful.

Just be aware that by adopting the attitude that “I like it and that’s all that matters”, you might not, in fact, be doing what’s best for your future success.

If it’s more important to you that you like your logo than it is that it ‘does it’s job’ of communicating the things you want to your audience and drawing people in, then just be aware that’s a choice you’re making.

 
Bonus pro tip: Your identity is always going to be communicating something about you. If it isn’t communicating the right message, then it’s communicating the wrong one - and that’s not good.

After all, as the saying goes - “You cannot not communicate”.
