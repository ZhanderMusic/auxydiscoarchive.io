TheRealJFalc April 14, 2018, 4:43pm #44

For this, I will be listening to all the tracks after your name change, as that’s where I had last left off my listening.

Voyage (X aUstin):

I enjoy the use of silk in this one with tone. I also like the Headphones Advised-esque Allen line in the background. It’s pretty haunting in a way. However, that’s one of the few things I really enjoyed in the song. The rest of it wasn’t overly enjoyable to me. I don’t know why, either. I think the hihats were a bit too much personally for me. Also, it’s a bit too much on the long side for me without a clear sense of direction or movement. It just feels a bit all over the place. I know that’s like 9 months ago but I’m thorough ;3

Riverside:

Bloopy bloop bass of joy and happiness. I was bopping around to this one the entire time while typing this and listening. I love when you take your style and diversify it out like this, even if it’s how your style was back then it’s really quite a fantastic piece especially for 9 months ago Auxy. I especially love the strong piano chords, they really make the track move.

Antlantis:

I like the use of pitch bend with Hour IGLOO (thanks for correcting me lol) on some of these chords. It really sets the mood quite well, although it’s not personally too much to my taste. It’s definitely a breath of fresh air from wubby future bass ;3

Acropolis:

Great use of ducker to strengthen the chords in the drop. I really like how it worked and sounded. Not much more to really say about this one, I think it’s a solid track. No real criticism, honestly.

Wave (X Mr. Anderson):

It’s… really wavey. It’s also really slow. Uh… it’s honestly not my style but it’s certainly well produced. I like the sense of atmosphere with it, but other than that it’s just not my personal taste.

Ring of Fire:

Driving. Moving. I really like this one, classic EDM four-to-the-floor track. Even the melody is nice, complete with strong chords to bring out the moving sense. It’s really well done. Definitely one of my personal favorites, right up alongside Headphones Advised.

Prometheus:

Alright, I do like this one, but it’s not as good as Ring of Fire in my opinion. The wubs are a nice touch this time around as well, they really accent parts of the track and aren’t the main focus of the track. I love the use of Silo, Jelly (Pretty sure that’s what it is) and Igloo as well. Good choice of a strong bass as well.

Marathon:

I love the staple hour lead at the beginning, and the chords with the Future House basses really works quite well. The plucks also work quite well too, all around really solid and well done. At this point, it’s really setting me up for the marathon of future bass that is to come, both metaphorically and literally.

And now, time for pain.

Ichorous:

I actually really like the beginning. It’s a great sense of ambient. That… kinda stopped whenn it got into the story wubs. Too much tone made it really… blergh. Other than the drop I really like it. This piece is definitely due for a VIP mix of sorts with the new instruments and sounds. I think it has quite a lot of potential.

Nova:

Interesting intro and stuff. It’s quite relaxing in a way, reminding a bit of Headphones Advised (I swear I’ll never stop comparing your tracks to that song). Even the wubs are mostly stomach-able. Overall, I really enjoyed it.

Dream Island:

I like the use of Silo with tone and the electric pianos. It creates a nice mixture of sound that isn’t present in some of your other songs. I really think it sets up the track quite well.

Singularity:

save me

Ok. Giving this a serious second chance now. I’ve already expressed my distaste for the excessive wubs quite a number of times. I do like the piano line. And the drop is ok. But really, I don’t know why I dislike this song so much. It’s got some good qualities. I just… ugh.

Nucleus:

Well. I can’t really listen to this one as you literally took it down while I was talking to you about Singularity. Damn. I don’t know if I would of liked it at all considering what you had said about it though. Onto the next track.

Leaving:

Well, this is wubs done right. Really atmospheric and fitting. This deserves all of Singularity’s plays. Ok maybe I’m biased. I don’t care. I really like this. I also really like the ending.

MisterMaster April 15, 2018, 3:19am #46

Hey Produk,

You’re a real personality within the Auxy Forum and your music is always positively received.

One of your strengths is strong layering and mixing. Your layers are all super distinct and clear, never muddy. Sounds aren’t lost in your tracks. In particular, the drums always sound great, really punchy. And your leads shine through despite the thick pads that are characteristic of your sound. Strong layering and quality mixing are key skills of the better producers and you’re totally nailing it.

I love your recent piano work, like the leads in Urgency / Memories and the piano chords in Distant Future. Also enjoyed the the unique introduction for Vision And pretty much everything about Meteor.

The only constructive feedback I can offer is that your tracks often rely on a) wubs, b) stammering effects and c) build / drops with automation. You’re super good at these three things - and people admire you for it, including myself - but despite the high quality of your work, it can start to feel predictable. Like, I know you’re going to do those three things. Distant Future is awesome and then it returns to the safety net of wubs; Magnetism has unique and innovative sections, but returns to stammering. These three techniques are repeated across your tracks pretty consistently. Your music and inspiration could possibly benefit from purposely NOT using those three techniques for a few tracks. I’d be keen to hear that, for sure.

But, even without any changes, your music is already awesome!
