ThoughtfulNoise November 6, 2021, 6:04pm #100

Don’t mean to be a downer, but you’re probably better off joining one of the existing Auxy Discord servers, with an established user community, than starting a new one from scratch.

Avro:

piano, guitar, orchestral

Digital music production in those genres succeeds or fails on the quality of the samples and the level of ‘performance’ and nuance readily achievable in the software. Auxy doesn’t score highly on either count for those instruments and genres, leaving output sounding a bit cheap and quite mechanical/MIDI-locked.

It’s likely why there’s relatively little Auxy music beyond electronic and dance, which is where its capable of creating something more authentic and credible.

 

(Making a new Auxy Discord server dedicated — or leaning towards — to those genres is likely to struggle, precisely bc so few Auxy users work in those genres, for the reasons mentioned above.)

ThoughtfulNoise November 7, 2021, 8:53am #102

I didn’t mean to shut the idea down. Sorry if that’s how it came across. There might be other ways a new Auxy Discord can offer value to the Auxy community and be successful. In fact, it’s very likely a new Auxy Discord would need a ‘unique selling point’ to have a hope of becoming successful…

It’s tricky, the community isn’t huge and it’s likely that most Auxy users aren’t aware of community servers or aren’t interested in joining one.
(If it was more engaged, this forum would likely be more active.)

I’d love to see more innovation around Auxy, so if you believe you can create a new server that brings something new and valuable to the space, I’m all for it.

 

As for those instruments and genres… I probably wish Auxy was better at supporting them more than most, as I’m one of those who tries to work in non-electronic genres in Auxy.
I work a lot — a lot — with the Vintage Keys (epiano/Rhodes) pack, often creating very complex solos or rich jazzy chords. It’s very difficult to add real nuance bc the relative volume levels are very limited, and the sounds a little lacking in character (though Lobby is my constant go-to).

(I sometimes bounce out the MIDI patterns to Ableton to use one of the Rhodes libraries I have and to add some performance nuance using the per-note Velocity controls.)

Auxy is very capable in many ways, but it’s aimed primarily at electronic music producers where you don’t tend to find the depth of performance, nuance and subtlety that help define the genres you mentioned.

A number of possible solutions/enhancements have been proposed by users multiple times, but so far, the Auxy team have shown little interest.
