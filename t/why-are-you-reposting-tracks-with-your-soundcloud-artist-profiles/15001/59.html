Arimyth April 10, 2019, 3:05am #59

Very fascinating topic here (interestingly enough it also serves as the direct counterpart to this earlier post). This is something I’ve gradually shifted views on overtime but now have a fairly consistent standing on.

@iammane put it this way before IIRC, but in my opinion at least, SoundCloud (with it’s unique hybrid nature of being both a streaming platform and social media platform) is best suited to be a resume of sorts for your musical endeavors, whether it be creation or curation. In the same way that a dubstep focused promotional page wouldn’t curate the latest in chill / ambient music, I believe that irrelevant and excessive reposts can detract from a listener’s experience of artist pages.

Extrapolating from mine and others experiences, I often discover new artist pages when I hear a track of theirs I like (either through SoundCloud algorithms or someone sending it to me) and thus visit their page to hear their other music. I do not personally visit their page to hear other types of music they like (or I would have just visited a radio station instead ), and though there exists a “Tracks” page to filter out reposts, I’d prefer to keep my stream rather clean and thus excessive amounts of this type of clutter can potentially discourage a follow or future visits.

Now this is definitely not to understate the importance and benefits of (sparingly and mindfully used) reposts. Repost trades (or repost networks on a larger scale) are an incredibly beneficial tool to introduce new audiences to your music and I’ve witnessed many examples of success that stemmed from these tools. Now, I don’t see too many people doing this around here but it’s something to consider looking into if you’re really into the curation aspect of SoundCloud.

tl;dr Reposts were added to SoundCloud for a reason, it’s just in your best interest to know what effect mindfulness (or lack thereof) when reposting has on your listeners.
