I had an idea for a collab and mashup challenge, and I wanted to see what others think. The way I see it working is along these lines:

  • If you want to take part, find an old crappy sounding 4- or 8-bar loop that you’ve never been able to turn into a track and submit it to this thread.
  • After a week of people submitting loops the competition begins, and everyone then has one week to take the loops and turn them into a song.
  • You’re allowed to use as many loops as you want, but you must use at least 2 and one of them must be the loop that you submitted.
  • Loops can be modified to work in your new song, as long as they’re still identifiable.
  • Use any key, any tempo, any swing amount, any instruments, any length (within reason!)
  • Your song needs to be complete, not a demo or an outline.
  • Entries must be uploaded to SoundCloud.

In terms of selecting your 4- or 8-bar loop, this should ideally be something that’s been kicking around forever (I’m sure we all have loops that we want to do something with eventually, but never seem to get back round to). Most of mine sound crappy, but I’m sure there’s a spark of something in there that could be explored. Those are the loops I’d like people to select.

If we start with 5 crappy sounding loops, and end with 5 nice polished songs then that’d be a win all round, right?

So what do people think? Sound like fun?

iammane:

can you copy from one song and paste into another?

You can but you have to copy each instrument one at a time.

  1. From the loop, long-press the title of instrument you want to copy (e.g. ‘Puma’),
  2. Select “Copy Instrument”,
  3. Switch to your WIP,
  4. Click the “+ Instrument” at the bottom of the screen (it should now say “Puma copied” underneath it),
  5. Choose “Paste ‘Puma’” from the menu.

You’ll have to keep switching between the loop you’re borrowing from and your WIP in order to copy and paste the new elements into your track.

HTH
