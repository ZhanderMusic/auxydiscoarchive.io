El1011 August 16, 2018, 7:44am #117

in terms of regular basses, some reverb n stuff is fine, especially if the bass is the focus of that section (ex: trance music) or if you’re trying to do a cool effect.

But for sub-basses, and basses especially melodies on top, you wouldn’t want much (if any) reverb on them. They are usually mono instruments, so adding reverb would widen the sound out and potentially make the overall mix a bit muddy.

The default reverb amount for Auxy basses/sub’s isn’t too bad if you just leave it it, but if you do find your mix getting muddy, especially with the low end, taking it out would be a good place to start.

Idk if you listen to artists like Kotori, Panda Eyes, or Spag Heddy, but they’re good examples of using sub’s without reverb. You can hear how much punch the sub bass has, but they dont have any reverb to them, which would take away that punchy feel.

blakkaz August 16, 2018, 12:07pm #121
NickElle:

Another thing, the human brain does not like stereo sub bass

The human brain can’t localize frequencies below ~80Hz, so there isn’t any good reason to use stereo effects on sub bass.

If you must, then split the signal in two (high/low), and only add FX to the high copy.

And always check your mix for mono compatibility.
