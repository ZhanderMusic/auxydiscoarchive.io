Matik June 15, 2018, 12:59am #2

Whenever I have drum fills, most of the time I use different types of toms and put lowpass and reverb on them, with the addition of changing pitches to each tom.

Other times I just use the same tom and have different pitches set for each of those toms and make an ascending to descending progression in triplets.

It all depends on the mood of the track as well. If you wanna have toms in a big drum buildup, I suggest using the second option and slowly rise it by putting volume automation on the entire drum kit until it finally reaches the climax.

For quick drum fills just before a drop or in between the drop and the second interval, I usually use the first option. In addition to this, you can add a progression of ascending to descending or descending to ascending notes that play during the drum fill.

Also, just experimenting with random sounds and percussion and seeing what sounds interesting for your track can work as well.

Not the best at giving out tips, but I hope this helped!
