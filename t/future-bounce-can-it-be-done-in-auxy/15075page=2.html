INDIR3CT April 15, 2019, 2:26pm #23

Making a track fully with triplets, or with default notes with a swing of 5-7 works pretty well for getting that “bouncy” vibe. For a decent sidechain, use 2 different kicks. Here is what I mean… Have your main kick set with the volume ducker off. Have another kick with the volume all the way off, so its there… but you can’t hear it. With that kick make it a bit offset, and turn the volume ducker on for that. That way the sidechain hits a bit earlier than normal, making it sound more heavy, because when it does ducker, all you will hear is your main kick that doesn’t use sidechain. If that makes sense. If not, I can share a example of what I mean, It makes insane sidechains! Auxy’s built in sidechain settings are a bit… light? So I do this to make it really compressed and impacting. Other than that, conduct the melodies and chords like you would in a future bass track. Another little tip I normally do for a future bounce type track is to layer an 808 bass with a Reese bass, so you have the low frequency, then some ritty gritty stuff on top.
