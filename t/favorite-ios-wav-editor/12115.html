iammane September 11, 2018, 12:37pm #2

AudioShare, hands down. It’s by the same developer of Aum.

Depending on what kind of editing you want to do, like trimming, normalizing etc it’s probably all you’d need. You can also re-sample using IAA effects which is pretty cool. It’s also a decent enough recorder.

You can “open in” Auxy directly from AudioShare.

iammane September 12, 2018, 1:57pm #10

They’re the pre-cursor to audio unit effects/instruments… I can’t remember how many years ago they came out, but basically it was Apple’s answer to AudioBus, in a way. You can apply inter app audio (IAA) effects in a recording chain but cannot apply the effects after the fact (ie, apply IAA effects to recorded audio). In my experience at least… though I think Cubasis does some trickery that might make this possible (via resampling, I’d imagine).
