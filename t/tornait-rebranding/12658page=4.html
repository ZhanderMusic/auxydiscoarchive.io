Arimyth October 28, 2018, 10:49pm #69

FWIW… cc. everyone:

I think it’s important to realize that our brand is a small part of our persona as a whole, be it a musical persona or otherwise. A consistent and memorable brand is crucial when building an audience, but what really defines a producer is the unique sound that they deliver with their music. As such, I think the thread as a whole is unconsciously exaggerating the importance of this visual component for any music producer.

I personally did a slight spinoff of my old brand with some new colors and effects but kept most of the visuals the same in terms of the core logo. And that works for me. Everyone’s different, however, and if someone else finds that it helps to keep who they are as a musician evolving and fresh, then I don’t see a problem with shifting styles and rebranding more than one otherwise would–especially if music as a whole is something done with a large focus on the recreational aspect and not for long term establishment within the music industry.

@tornait I’d say to take the feedback people here are giving you fairly seriously when considering whether or not you’re going to adopt your new brand as in the end they are (a portion of) your audience. But in the end it’s about what helps you grow most as an artist as a whole and if this helps you get out of a creative slump, then go for it.
