wagneric March 29, 2018, 10:16pm #64

Glissando = slide from one note to another without disconnection
Portamento = slide from one note to another WITH disconnection

With portamento, the violinist either uses a separate bow for the landing note, or takes a little pressure off the string to get it away from the fretboard toward the end of the slide, creating a small gap prior to the landing. You can easily make a portamento from a glissando, but not the other way around.

Glissando would benefit Auxy greatly!

akabillposters March 30, 2018, 6:23am #78

The challenge of polyphonic glissando is potentially a tricky one.
Moving from a 3-note chord to another 3-note chord would be pretty straight forward.
But, they’d need a set of rules to handle scenarios where the start and end don’t have the same number of notes. There’d likely be compromises that produce results that don’t suit everyone.

For any crossing over, we’d likely still need to handle that as multiple instruments, as you say.
Or, the direct note-to-note linking (in piano roll) that’s been mentioned here previously might be the most accessible option.

wagneric March 30, 2018, 3:41pm #79

I’m a strong advocate for direct – ideally crossover-capable – note-to-note piano roll linking. This is true polyphony. I’m being openly pedantic here, but anything that would take into account scale degrees for sliding between sets of notes is homophonic (interdependent notes/lines/voices), not polyphonic (independent).
