+1

If you think about how an oscillator works (and just to keep it simple I’m going with subtractive synthesis) you have some controls to shape the sound. All the oscillator does is output a wave form. You shape that sound by using ADSR, filtering (low/high/band whatever) and effects. You want a pad? Increase attack time. You want a pluck? Set attack time to zero, set sustain to zero and maybe ride release depending on what you’re looking for. It’s definitely a quick win for sound design effectively increasing the usability of the sounds as they exist now.

Even if ADSR couldn’t be implemented then a proper two osc synth would be awesome - would effectively negate my need for an app like Groovebox.

iammane:

It’s definitely a quick win for sound design effectively increasing the usability of the sounds as they exist now.

I’ve made exactly that point directly to Lenberg some time back.
As you say, in principle*, it’s a relatively easy way to effectively expand the number of available sounds exponentially. A real force multiplier.
(Iirc, it didn’t get a response.)

Imo, it’s such an easy win, with such obvious and immediate benefits that, presumably, only a objection to the principle of that kind of experience would prevent it from going on the roadmap.

Again, a great candidate for those 2nd-tier power features that we’ve been alluding to.

However, comments he made at the start of the year suggest that he considers repackaging a handful of existing sounds to be a more preferable (easier? cheaper?) solution than user-definable ADSR that exponentially increases all sound options.

It’s encouraging that he’s recognising the demand, but it’s not a very promising sign to those who see universal ADSR controls as the right solution.

* I suspect they’ve kinda painted themselves into a corner with their approach to shaping tools and introduction of pre-trig to compensate for the approach they’ve taken.
Pre-trig is clever, but it seems like a band-aid solution for a problem they should probably fundamentally readdress. As painful as it might be to extricate themselves from this position - certainly a non-trivial thing - I think an overhaul and re-simplification of the shaping system (around common ADSR principles) could get the app back onto a path where more things are possible.

akabillposters:

Seems a relatively cheap way to get much more mileage from the same number of native sounds.

But this would give more stuff for free users, which would discourage people from purchasing premium. Which is what they don’t want.

Anyway, onto the main point of contention.

Auxy:

there are five or so people extensively debating adding lots of features that are common in DAWs

Five users out of around fifty to seventy-five or so regularly active users is a large percentage. It’s nothing to disregard, which is what seems to be happening. At least from a perspective as someone who’s becoming more and more frustrated with the direction of the app, basic features that could expand the system greatly isn’t something trivial.

I think the more important thing to address is features that, as musicians, are important. As a musician, increased control over how an instrument sounds is a no-brainer. If it’s too complex, people won’t use the feature, but that doesn’t make it not valuable or valid as a suggestion. As an option, rather than a requirement. To be completely honest, the fact that users are required to layer sounds to achieve better sound quality is rather complex to begin with, is it not? Would it not be simpler to simply be able to adjust the way the sound plays, rather than adding more sound onto it to try and make it sound different? For some, it would definitely be easier. For others, not so much.

Even just basic attack and release controls would be nice, that way some sounds can be properly layered as well. Silk’s shape meter changes the attack at a higher rate than most other sounds, making it much harder to layer. A simple way to adjust this equally among instruments would be easier to layer, which seems to be an important part of making Auxy sound good at this point.

But, I know my points are going to be falling on deaf ears.
