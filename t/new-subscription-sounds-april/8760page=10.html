akabillposters April 12, 2018, 9:20am #201

I thought you were talking about using volume automation to simulate short decays common to plucked sounds, rather than the attack.

Using volume automation to shave off attack transients makes little sense (to me), as attack transients are a characteristic of plucked sounds, so presumably something you’d want to keep. Attack transients are certainly part of what defines the individual character of short, percussive sounds, so shaving those off would presumably take away a lot of what makes a sound different and distinct from the next one.

 
This is why I think the app needs proper per-instrument ADSR controls.
We wouldn’t specifically need sound packs of plucks or pads as any sound could be shaped into a pluck, a pad, …

Sound packs could then focus on broadening the palette by introducing sounds with new character, new timbres, …
