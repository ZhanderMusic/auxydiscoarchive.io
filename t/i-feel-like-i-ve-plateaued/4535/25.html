akabillposters November 27, 2017, 8:33am #25

My advice would be to keep making your music, be pro-active about improving your production skills (to better translate what’s in your head into tracks) - and don’t be too focussed on ‘success’ as measured within the reach of this community.

This community is still very small, and currently has certain leanings in terms of what’s popular. Due to the small size of the community, that can sometimes have what feels like a disproportionate effect on how much attention one might feel they’re getting.

Expand your horizons. Seek out other forums/groups to join as well - ideally ones that are focussed on the style of music you make or your approach to music, rather than the tool you use.

Calibrate your own expectations for your music. Focus on what’s most important — at this time. Is it popularity or quality?
(Quality doesn’t always lead to popularity, and popularity doesn’t always mean quality.)

Set yourself some goals, whether that’s to improve production, to seek out other (additional) places where your music might resonate more, or be more active about promotion.

 
Another thing I’d consider…

If your music would benefit from production options, sounds, whatever… that aren’t available within Auxy, don’t be afraid to switch to a tool that’s better suited.

I think Auxy is outstanding for initial idea generation and development, but in some cases, it may be holding back your music from being its best form. (It’s why I don’t consider anything that I’ve done in Auxy to be in its final form.)

So, don’t limit your thinking to what can be achieved in Auxy or what Auxy makes easy.
Make your music the priority – and use whichever tool is best for helping you get there.

Akyut November 29, 2017, 6:15pm #32

Music doesn’t need to be technical to be good. Most, if not all enjoyment found in music is completely subjective, hence why people like some genres and dislike others.

Now, for my personal opinion on this : If you make music for the numbers, you shouldn’t bother in the first place. In my eyes, music is passion given form. A little cheesy, I know, but it gets the point across. If I feel myself start to stagnate, then I change it up. If I enjoy what I’m producing at the time, then I take that as a good sign and continue forward with the project.

Out of every five projects I finish, only one will be uploaded to my soundcloud. It’s not for lack of quality, it’s because I don’t feel anything when I listen to those tracks. Hell, I’ve had a couple pieces I’ve spent weeks on, only to at the last minute grow bored with the track and move on to the next. I don’t think it’s a waste of time though, because from every track I create I learn something new.

When the rare track I do publish comes along, I’m then able to incorporate new elements into my style, and then I build on top of that. If you feel yourself plateauing, don’t give up on music, instead take inspiration from elsewhere and create something you feel good about! As long as it means something to you, it can be considered good music.
