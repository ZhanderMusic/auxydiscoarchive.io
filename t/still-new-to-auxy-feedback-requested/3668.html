NotMiles November 1, 2017, 2:23am #2

Hey, don’t worry about being “in the league”, it just takes time and dedication. When you make a new song, have the mindset that it’s going to better than the last one in some way, whether it’s blending the instruments better, coming up with a cooler bassline, or trying out a new technique you learned. Always look for ways to improve. I’ve been making songs on Auxy for over a year now and I wasn’t really great at making songs when I first started (3 years prior to that I started making music with another app called Figure. My first songs ever were hot garbage). Even now I still think I have a ton of room to improve. Instead of hearing other people’s music and worrying about them being better, listen to what they do and see if you can do it too. It’s okay to steal some ideas from others (No, not like plagiarism, plagiarism is bad). I mean it’s okay if, say, you hear some sick wubs in a featured Auxy song and you try making those wubs in your own style to put in your own music. I figured out how to make those sweet, sweet future bass-y wubs just by listening to other Auxy artists do it. Don’t be afraid to ask us questions here on Disco, we’ll help you out. Lastly, and probably my favorite piece of advice, experiment! Fiddle around with Auxy. Try new things. Make weird noises. Do things that make no musical sense whatsoever (why not). You might just discover some cool new sounds to use in your music.

Oh and also, make it fun.

(I really like Showdown, btw)
That is all.

blackocean November 1, 2017, 3:47am #3

Don’t be hard on yourself. I am super guilty of this, I hate most everything I make, but there are a few good ones, which I post. Part of the process of making something that you think sounds good is making things that don’t sound nearly as good. Whenever you feel like something isn’t quite right, ask someone for a second opinion, don’t just give up and say “well that idea didn’t work, I;m just going to throw it in the trash bin”. Also don’t worry about being in the “league”. Trying to make stuff to get you famous gets you nowhere, and fans you gain won;t be as genuine. Create what is you and people who like you will be drawn to your creations.
