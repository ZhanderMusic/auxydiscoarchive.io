AzureOnyx August 11, 2019, 10:55am #34

Honestly, I’ve talked about A6 within the Auxy Collective Discord and through Disco for far too many times, it’s a real chore that I have to keep venting my frustrations with it.

First of all, the update would’ve been great if my old projects weren’t completely F()()()ed by the new sounds. Little did I know, the new sounds don’t sound nearly the same as they used to back in Auxy 5.

I’ve talked to other people within The Auxy Collective discord about my situation and they had the same problems I’m facing; the automation screen feels too loose to even get creative with, and even if we were to get creative, it takes an exponentially longer time to make the same pattern compared to A5

There’s no way an amateur user can understand what these DAW terms mean without a visual. I’ve complained about this during the betas, and mentioned about it when A6 was first released. I strongly don’t like the approach where you have to figure it out since you don’t have a clear understanding on what half of the new settings are, which ultimately kills off a person’s creative flow. I believe this how a DAW fails, especially when it’s towards a mobile market, expecting to help teach people what Sineshaper, Clip, or Bitcrusher is.

Yes, I know, this update is extremely beneficial to those who are familiar on how to use a proper DAW like Ableton, Reason, FL Studio, etc. But Auxy was what helped me get a basic understanding on what some of the terms used within the EDM community, terms like Sidechaining, Distortion, Delay, Reverb, etc. Now it feels like what happened when people reacted when Auxy 5 was first released when no one was expecting the app to be subscription based and locked users out when they don’t pay up for even the most basic instruments, even as going far as removing the ability to use the original sounds if said user doesn’t have the subscription.

What I’m saying is that I’m extremely fed up with the update, especially because I feel destroys creative flow rather than helping users create the kinds of music they want to hear in the present or the future. I’m done using Auxy until future notice.
