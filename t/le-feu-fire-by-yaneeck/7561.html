akabillposters February 14, 2018, 9:06am #15

That would depend on where you intend to play it, no?

If it’s a club mix (literally intended for playing in a club), then yes, bass in the middle for the reasons you mentioned - plus you want to create an even listening experience wherever someone is in relation to the nearest speakers.

But, if it’s a home / headphone / stereo mix, then some stereo panning on the bass might be fine, if that’s the effect you’re going for. I can imagine something like swirling Neuro Bass would work well with some slight panning automation in a full stereo mix - if not overdone.

(Imo, it’s also worth not conflating ‘bass’ with ‘sub’. Each can - and probably should - be treated/mixed separately and differently.)

NickElle February 14, 2018, 3:20pm #17

You’re right about it being different for different systems.

A club or festival sound system wouldn’t be able to take bass panning, but small things like headphones, earphones will.
But, the sickness comes from hearing any bass being panned.
Whether it be on a Funktion One, or Apple EarPods.

And, I know what you may be thinking:
“I didn’t feel sick when I heard it.”
You may not have felt sick, but your ears where getting tired of it. It causes rapid ear fatigue.
And, the last thing you’d want listeners to do is get tired of your music.

About the “bass vs sub” thing:
You’re right.
But…
It’s not possible to isolate mono the sub from the bass in Auxy.
In Ableton, you can isolate mono sub, which is great!
But, with Auxy, your sub is stuck with your bass.
So, it’s probably best to keep your bass (and sub) in the middle.
