MR_ANDERSON August 7, 2018, 11:47pm #749

https://soundcloud.com/mrandersonauxy/final-round

Well, that took a long time. To those who have waited too long for this, I apologize! I hope that this song fulfills your expectations.

I created this song such that every facet of it would reflect part of my summer experiences. The song starts out bright and happy, depicting the joys of the beginning of a new summer. It immediately moves into a more deep, relaxed section, which, fittingly, reflects the deep relaxation I found in the break from school. The song then drops off into a section building tension towards the drop. This part describes my building anticipation of the vacation our family would take in August (which I just returned from ). Finally, after the drop, the song returns to its previous relaxed state, and declines in mood from there as the summer ends and the weight of my senior year in high school sets in.

The key and chord progression were both meant to describe my mixed emotions about this summer, from the happiness and relaxation of family vacations, to the uncertainty of the path ahead of me with high school ending in a year and college beginning. And while this song was not the longest, I meant for it to be this way, as it accurately tells of my opinion that this summer was, well, too short!

With that, I bring you all my final entry to this summer’s tournament! And I also must add, that this tournament will unfortunately be my last. As previously stated, school is starting soon, and that takes priority over music (usually). That being said, I hope that these tournaments continue, as they always provide those who enter with amazing musical inspiration! Thanks to both the judges, @DHO and @Andrew_Adams, who both poured out so much time and effort into these tournaments. And, hey, if you guys ever need another judge for stuff like this, I’d love to help!

Mr. Anderson

TC64 August 8, 2018, 3:59am #752

https://soundcloud.com/ToxicCaves64/Sunfall

When I thought about the kind of song I wanted to create to express the ending of this year’s summer, I wanted something that sounded huge and super fun, but I also wanted it to have it’s quieter moments, to peacefully transition into the fall.

Sunfall starts off with a solo piano intro, and then transitions into a simple melody that is mostly in major, but sometimes switches to minor to represent the feeling of summer slowing down, ready to end soon. The melody continues into the first verse, which follows the same principles with a slightly jazzier feel.

After the verse, the lead-in section plays. It starts off using slower notes to give off a relaxing feel, but before you know it, all of the existing instruments and a newly-added square wave riser and drum fill start to build.

Summer’s not over yet.

The drop uses heavy future bass-style synths, and it’s accentuated with a marimba/pizzicato strings-based lead and an inspiring chord progression.

After the drop, the song slows back down again, keeping the melody but dropping the synth arpeggios and leads. This section represents the final days of summer.

Finally, everything else comes to a stop, and slow piano begins to play. This is the final day of summer. You’re watching the sun set, ready for Fall to start. A quiet “plink” sound in the high range of the piano represents the first falling leaves.

And then the last bit of sunlight fades away.
