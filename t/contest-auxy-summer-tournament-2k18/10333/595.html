http://www.soundcloud.com/ToxicCaves64/Lemonade

The song begins with a simple piano progression, representing the feeling of waking up on the first day of summer, feeling calm, but unsure of what’s going to happen next.

As bass notes, more complex chords, and a Silk lowpass sweep are added, the song gains a sense of anticipation before the song transitions into its first drop. With the drop’s powerful synthwave style and an epic Glow and Coin melody, it captures the feeling of freedom, flying off to go on a vacation or not having to go to school for the rest of the season.

The song then winds down into a verse. The uplifting Pixel & Fantasy melody reminds me of the fun side of summer, walking on the beach, hanging out with friends, and sipping lemonade. In fact, this is where the title of the song came from! The moment where the song cuts out with highpass for a second represents the waves of the ocean.

At the end of the verse, a chord change and a short Violin Pizzicato riff shift the song into into a softer tropical vibe, with a melody made out of a Circle glide lead and Coconut, plus a jazzy Barber and Pixel bassline.

It feels like the song’s winding down, but a sudden chord change and the addition of Violin Staccato encapsulate how during summer, you feel like it’s never going to end. Many instruments return for one final drop, with elements from every single part of the song.

Everything then slows down, and the song ends on a major chord.

Fall might be my favorite season of them all, but with this song, I was really able to express all the things that I love about summer.
