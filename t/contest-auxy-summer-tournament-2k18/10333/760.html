TC64 August 8, 2018, 3:59am #752

https://soundcloud.com/ToxicCaves64/Sunfall

When I thought about the kind of song I wanted to create to express the ending of this year’s summer, I wanted something that sounded huge and super fun, but I also wanted it to have it’s quieter moments, to peacefully transition into the fall.

Sunfall starts off with a solo piano intro, and then transitions into a simple melody that is mostly in major, but sometimes switches to minor to represent the feeling of summer slowing down, ready to end soon. The melody continues into the first verse, which follows the same principles with a slightly jazzier feel.

After the verse, the lead-in section plays. It starts off using slower notes to give off a relaxing feel, but before you know it, all of the existing instruments and a newly-added square wave riser and drum fill start to build.

Summer’s not over yet.

The drop uses heavy future bass-style synths, and it’s accentuated with a marimba/pizzicato strings-based lead and an inspiring chord progression.

After the drop, the song slows back down again, keeping the melody but dropping the synth arpeggios and leads. This section represents the final days of summer.

Finally, everything else comes to a stop, and slow piano begins to play. This is the final day of summer. You’re watching the sun set, ready for Fall to start. A quiet “plink” sound in the high range of the piano represents the first falling leaves.

And then the last bit of sunlight fades away.
