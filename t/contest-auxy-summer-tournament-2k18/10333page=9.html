DHO:

Since this is a summer tournament, create a track inspired by your fondest summer memory . Feel free to share the story with us, but it’s not required. I’d like an atmospheric intro followed by whatever you want. As always, you will be judged on Creativity, Composition, Accuracy, and Execution.

This track is inspired by the summer memories of a specific road trip from 2010. Me and my friends would always turn on the radio, and the two songs that would be playing nonstop every day would be “Break your Heart” by Taio Cruz and “California Gurlz” by Katy Perry. My memories of the track mostly related to us breaking out into song with the lyrics while driving along the highway multiple times during the trip, and the last bit of the song has stuck extensively with me. Listening to the song now, I can’t imagine how 14-year-old me even liked it xD But for me, it was the sense of friendship we all shared along the road trip. So, for an atmospheric intro building into the song, I used the last bit of the vocals to bring up into the song I created, a chilled-out and relaxed House beat with simple structure and drumwork.

Again, I stress that the song really couldn’t have worked without the sample, as the lack of context would render the feeling of the track lost. I’ll gladly create a different entry if this will be grounds for disqualification.

done, Dora is the name of the track inspired by Summer 2005 when I was just a lil kid and my parents had broken up. It was a turbulent time with having so much exposure to adult problems, but moving to my grandparents house where my cousins lived allowed for so much fun and true self exploration for the first time. On one instance my cousin, brother, and I threw my smallest cousins Dora doll in dog poop, left the water hose running in the front yard, and claimed the Dora doll got up by itself after we had left it in the backyard. We were always pulling schemery like that and my grandpa “spanked” us that day. LUCKILY, cuz he did it all soft haha. The track has soft portions, turbulent ones, resolutions and strip downs in order to portray the emotional tone of that summer with the overall theme of things being that everything would work out regardless of the problems and the outcome was to be even better than planned.
