iammane November 22, 2017, 8:59pm #51

You can drive a Honda Civic, or a Lamborghini. They’re both cars, they’ll both get you from A to B. They’re two totally different experiences.

When you get into the world of desktop music software you’ve got quite a bit more power under the hood. VSTs expand your sonic palette, working with audio is a breeze and the tools available are very mature in most cases.

The real question is whether or not you’ve outgrown your software. In that case when you’ve pushed it to the limits it’s time to try something else maybe.

There’s a caveat to all this though… everything has a trade off. In my life right now I don’t have the time to sit down behind a desktop or laptop for hours on end writing music, I have two kids and a very full time career. So stuff like Auxy is a godsend. Hash your time to work on stuff against the content of what you’re writing and it helps to know what tools you wanna fill your toolbox with

Auxy November 23, 2017, 10:52am #54

I started out with Cubase back in the nineties. Since then I’ve worked on projects in Reason, ProTools, Logic and Ableton Live. All these DAWs are fantastic tools with similar features but slightly different designs.

Even though I have access to and understand all these apps, I never felt particularly creative with any of them. When I sit down at a computer with a massive DAW, I tend to get into my engineering mindset. This has been great in situations where I for instance worked on studio recordings with bands.

The problem for me has always been that whenever I felt like making my own music (you know the feeling of having a groove or melody in your head), these apps tend to kill my creativity. I tend to get bored before I get much music done, and the groove in my head is lost to thoughts about which plugins I should use and how to route the signals.

This is why we started Auxy. To build a tool that lets you be creative with state of the art technology, without letting that technology (and too many choices) kill your ideas on the way.

Of course there are many people who are super creative using DAWs and other gear, but we’ve been happy to hear many stories from people who say they make more and better music with Auxy than they ever did with a DAW.

So my recommendation is to explore different tools and see which setup is right for your creativity and aspirations!
