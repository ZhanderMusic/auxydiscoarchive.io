@Barrelmanboy

I’m pretty sure some names will instantly close and open some gates.

While some names may initially feel ‘wrong’, I’m pretty sure that pretty much any name can come to feel ‘naturally’ connected to pretty much any genre.

If Sonny Moore (Skrillex) had called himself Mary Poppins, I don’t doubt that audiences would come to think of that name in all the same ways that they currently think of the name Skrillex.

It’s not so much the name that’s doing the work these days, but the reputation that the work you do attaches to it – that becomes ‘naturally connected’ to it after the fact, and tricks us into thinking that the name is such a natural fit.

Similarly, if Deadmau5 was the name of an acoustic alt-pop musician, I’m sure that would work.

The name Skrillex does lend itself to screaming electronic music, but I think it could work with pretty much any electronic genre. That’s also not to say that other names can’t become associated with that same genre.

Some bands/artists will use different names to distinguish different styles/projects.
But there are many electronic bands/artists that actually work across a number of genres, under one name.
The result is that, that one name comes to be associated with a range of genres (and a breadth of styles).

Or, just look at how many top DJ/producers use their real name (or a close variant of it).
