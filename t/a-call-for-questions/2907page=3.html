moemx:

I know this is slightly of topic - but would you have any recommendations for headphones when it comes to ideal audio transfer using auxy @nrgb?

The most important thing to look for when buying headphones (or speakers) is a flat frequency response.

Most consumer headphones will boost certain frequencies (like a bit of bass boost) because it generally sounds better to the average listener.

This is problematic for music producers because they aren’t hearing an accurate representation of their song.

If you mix your tracks on bassy headphones you may find that they are actually lacking bottom end when you play them on a flat sound system.

If you’re aware that your gear adds a little colour, you can learn to compensate, but it’s generally easier to not have to.

Without knowing your budget it’s hard to recommend something, but once you’ve settled on a pair I would do a little googling to see what others are saying. (the gearslutz forum is a good start)

As a starting point try AKG, Audio Technica, Beyerdynamic or Sennheiser.

The ATH m50x are good and dependable, and are reknown for their accuracy, clarity, frequency response, and budget friendliness (they are the best within the $200 price range). I personally own the ATH MSR7s. The only complaint I would have is that the fit is not ideal for my head size. So if you are looking for studio headphones, make sure that you will be able to have them on for 4 hours at least. My MSR7s broke in soon enough, but they were really uncomfortable when I got them. IMHO don’t look at anything above 300, to a beginning musician, anything over that is hard to appreciate.

akabillposters October 11, 2017, 9:05am #51

OK, been living with a pair of Sennheiser HD 380 Pro for the past couple of days.

Very clean sound. Already helping me find a couple of issues with my mixes, which will help me develop better mixing habits. Very useful. Very comfortable too.

To my still as-yet-untrained ear, it seems like clean representation across the lows, mids and highs. Highs aren’t screaming and lows aren’t muddy.

It’s a bit alarming at first, having such an uncoloured sound. Felt a little clinical. And I wasn’t sure if I liked it. But after a little while messing with my levels on my Audio Interface, I think I’ve got a halfway decent reference now. (My headphone volume dials are no longer maxed out.

They’ll be strictly for the reference and mixing, not for regular listening.

But, I will revisit the settings on my Sony MDR-XB950B1 listening headphones to possibly trim back on some of that extra-extra bass. (Ironically, I bought them for the extra bass, while replacing a second pair of badly treated AKG Y50 to die on me. Fwiw, the AKG Y50 are also quite honestly balanced considering they’re regular listening headphone aimed at ‘style’, not mixing/monitoring. AKG are quite reliable and honest across the board, sound-wise. (I didn’t want that honesty at the time.

It’ll also help me use my Sennheiser Momentum M2 IEi in-ear more knowingly.

I suspect having — and regularly using — a good pair of reference/monitor headphones will ‘retrain’ my ears to better appreciate subtlety and be more discerning — and make me a better ‘listener’.
