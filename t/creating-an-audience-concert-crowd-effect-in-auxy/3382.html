Creating an audience/concert crowd effect in Auxy

Production Talk
RazerFireRiot October 24, 2017, 1:43am #1

You’ve heard some of those songs, sounding like it’s being played live at a concert. And you may have wanted to add that effect in that Auxy track you’ve been working on for almost a month now.

Well…
It’s possible! Just follow these simple steps! The process may take some time, and this is pretty easy to do!

Step one: You only need literally one instrument, or rather, effect: The Almighty Sweeper!

115F9425-AD32-4447-B016-E1F72053350B.png750×1334 14.3 KB

Just go to the editor and you don’t even need to do anything fancy! Just simply leave the editor as 1 bar, and place and extend any note (Recommend: The middle of the scale; Example: The middle G) to make it last for the whole bar.

DF884D49-419B-4821-86B4-D4C2600850CF.png750×1334 26 KB

Step two: Copy and pasting.
In order to do this, you need to copy and paste to get multiple sweepers, have at least three, maximum five.

0C0689F6-3585-4A13-9780-8E57CBE13A89.png750×1334 30.5 KB

Step three: The most important tool: The Epic Automation.
This is the most crucial part to know about making your riot loud crowd! You need these Automations: Lowpass, Highpass, Pitch, Reverb, Pan, and this is optional, Volume.

F69034EE-1D9E-4346-BD29-D6BA05F55C55.jpeg749×1333 121 KB

Lowpass and Highpass: Since this is how you usually make your fade ins and outs, when you kinda play around with it, it can make a lot of effects, but right now we’re focused on making your “big audience”.

Pitch: Notice how in concerts you hear screaming and cheering. This is the big essential of making your effect in Automation.

Reverb: It gives that little echo effect in your audience.

Pan: The listener will feel as if they were immersed in your song with the crowd, especially with headphones on.

The next following steps can be done in any order!
Repeat this process for each part of the sweepers, making sure that you vary your Pitch, Highpass, Lowpass and Pan nodes!

Tip: You don’t necessarily need to use only one bar for making the effect for each Sweeper, that’s boring! Vary them from 1 bar to 4, 8 if you want to!

Lowpass and Highpass:
Lowpass: This is really important for how loud you want your audience effect, so it’s recommended to keep between this area (Imagine a two lines that’s surround the area in these two images):

5BDF1024-5D31-45A3-8A41-40703C325F36.png750×1334 24 KB
28907DA3-467C-4B7D-960F-9218FE5E78EE.png750×1334 22.6 KB

Highpass: Since it kind of alters the pitch of your sweeper, be sure to slightly modify the Highpass from between 10% Highpass to about 60% Highpass:

491BE6F3-8A9A-4A63-B574-BEAC430C4E78.png750×1334 22.5 KB
F189FD4C-57F1-423F-B5D5-FE07B7B8A18F.png750×1334 23.9 KB

Pan: You can do whatever you want with Pan, just make sure you place the nodes a long way apart for each other for a smoother pan, in different lengths and positions to make the crowd seem realistic, while immersing your listener into thinking they’re part of a concert if they’re wearing headphones:

8A19B6C9-4717-45F5-8F41-F8F8FB52C2DB.png750×1334 38 KB
8FCC0096-9B7E-4CBE-BEFB-68EBC042E756.png750×1334 37.3 KB

Pitch: This is the most important and crucial part of making the effect, and this may be the most fun! And the funny thing is, there’s two ways to do this, the normal and formal way, or the random way.

Random way (Faster, but may not give the full effect): Literally place nodes everywhere in random places

D593079D-0DFF-47C6-AA19-FDD5BFC3E642.png750×1334 44.2 KB
B1A9200C-9D05-4A4C-A710-63B8A6A08E79.png750×1334 42 KB
A795B6FF-64FA-4FC1-BCB8-DA404A3514E9.png750×1334 50.2 KB
10E1F022-CABA-4533-814C-CA5FD49D1B23.png750×1334 56.4 KB

Normal way (Slower, because you carefully place nodes): This way, you place nodes in a large variety of places, and you’ll have more control over the effect, and you’ll be able to do many different distances and placements of Automation nodes carefully, ensuring that the crowd sounds a little more realistic.

A28E3174-5BBE-43BE-BC4D-F034A894F4D0.png750×1334 43.5 KB
76E12C3B-1993-4C39-A035-B36A2B0F4D13.png750×1334 42.7 KB

Reverb: This is what makes it sound like your song is taking place in a big hollow-ish area, like a stadium.

Repeat this process with the rest of the Sweepers, and hopefully this helped you make that crowd effect!
And keep making those amazing tracks!
