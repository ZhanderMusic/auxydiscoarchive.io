iammane November 3, 2017, 1:44am #6

Pitch the snares, watch some DnB drummers too (Jojo Mayer is excellent) to get a feel for what would be “realistic” as far as programming goes. 32nd notes for snare rolls are always good (combined with up and down pitch) but don’t overuse.

I don’t know about any of the newer styles but the classic DnB I’m familiar with doesn’t have a whole hell of a lot going on at once. Check out Omni Trio, Aquasky etc.

YMMV though I’m finding Auxy pretty durn decent for drum and bass just really wish we had more drum sounds…
