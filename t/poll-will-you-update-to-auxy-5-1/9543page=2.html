anon73773654:

later on, they’ll start to sound more repetitive and similar because they use the same limited set of sounds

That, for me, is the point when they should recognise the value of moving to Premium.

If all they have in them is one or two decent tracks made from those first 10 sounds, then sure, stick with the free version.

But, if their imagination and creative ambition extends beyond one or two tracks (or multiple tracks that all sound pretty similar because they all use the same sounds), then they can and should be expected to start paying.

The free version needs to support the ability to create just enough music to get the bug, and to appreciate how going Premium will kick things into a whole different gear.

You want to give them enough to create something wonderful (to them), but not so much that they can create ‘wonderful’ pieces too many times before they start paying.

I’m personally sceptical that 10 sounds and a couple of drum kits hits the sweet spot of giving free users just enough to get them en masse to the tipping point.

But I could well be wrong to doubt.
Time will tell, I guess.
