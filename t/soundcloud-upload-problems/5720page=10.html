akabillposters December 17, 2017, 5:19pm #191

I don’t think Soundcloud should force creators that keep the website alive to pay…

Afaik, SC has yet to reach profitability.
(That was certainly true as of June/July this year, keeping in mind the company launched over 10 years ago. Don’t know the latest.)

So, who’s doing whom the favour?

Chances are, profitability will be coming from the paid Pro upgrades, not ads alone. Where ads play a role, tentpole tracks like Rock$tar likely generate as much ad revenue as 1,000,000 users of the type you’re describing.

So, let’s not overstate the value of the types of users you’re describing to SC’s bottom-line.

…to continue producing music.

No-one is stopping you from producing music.

Most people who aren’t willing to pay to continue uploading will most likely move to another website.

I would say that anybody who isn’t willing to spend £35 a year to have 6 hours of their music hosted* or £75 to have unlimited tracks hosted* isn’t serious about their music.
(* not to mention access to increasing amounts of useful listener data)

It might seem important to you now, but if it isn’t important enough to invest £35-75/year on it, then it isn’t actually important to you at all.

And, again,let’s not over-estimate our value to SC. No-one here who’s on the free account is directly profitable for SC. None of us have the audience numbers to generate enough ad revenue to cover the cost of the hosting, development time, etc…

(Fwiw, I’m on the Pro Unltd acct and I’m massively under-utilising the service. So I’m a perfect customer. Like the person who pays for an expensive gym membership, but never goes after the first month

If ads are generating overall profit for SC, it’s not because of people like you, me or even the most successful among us, it’s because of those musicians who have taken their music more seriously, built a worthwhile audience and who generate serious play numbers (and ad impressions).

(It’s only by virtue of the fact that no-one here really has a substantial, regular audience that suggestions like deleting tracks to free up space can even be considered. You can’t really do that once you’ve got traction.

 
So then, why not use those ‘free forever’, presumably ad maintained alternatives now? Why even waste time putting stuff on SC?
