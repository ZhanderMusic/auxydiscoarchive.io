TheRealJFalc July 11, 2018, 4:30pm #37
Produk:

even the most untrained ear can tell that most of these sounds are just minorly edited versions of Stress or Fuze.

I respectfully disagree with this statement.

Fuze is a sound that is similar to this, but these sounds are in no way an edited version of Fuze.

Stress is a sampled sine wave sound with a bit of distortion clip on the front end that produces a square-like sound at the start, and features bitcrushing distortion to turn it into a stronger square-like sound overall. It is not possible to edit it without editing the samples, and that would boost the file size up of the pack dramatically. All the sounds are less than a few kilobytes each, signaling that they are generated through waveforms and not sample based. not only this but Stress was licensed and created by 7 Skies, not Auxy, editing it in such a way could constitute a breach in contract, among other things.

Produk:

Example: dubstep/hard trap.

People like @Thrashie and @Goxenar seem to be doing just fine with what sound we have. A lot of the original pack is intended for a dubstep sound, you just got to experiment more as they are also meant as more multi-purpose.

Produk:

awful sounds like freak.

Produk:

We have a not very versatile sound, Weld.

Freak is not an awful sound, it is fantastic if used well. Automate the shape to get a better sound out of the growl it can produce and you got yourself something great.
Weld may not be very versatile but all of the disturbing leads can be useful for heavy and dark styles. Put some shape on mental and you got yourself a kick-like attack. Crank up the tone up on cone and you got huge distortion waiting to be used. And if you’re desperate, I’ve used Slope as a dubstep sound before, for my entry into Goxenar’s dubstep challenge. I will provide a link to the demo I submitted for it, and once the full song is out you will be able to hear it in its glory. If I were to layer it with other instruments, it could even be more useful.

Produk:

Don’t tell me “you can get it by layering.” Cause you can’t.

Not to mention that other people have created huge dark sounds using layers. If you really need an example, go to the feed and download the featured project for Crimson and pay close attention to the Beam and Racer line. Listen to some IVO, his song Of Blight is a great example of a huge heavy sound using Blend with automation. And if you need some heavy thick distortion, you can double the notes on Stress with some shape. I’m sure that if the new pack were simple edits of it, they’d do this too but obviously they don’t.

Produk:

That type of sound needs to be created from scratch. Not like chiptune.

The options for chiptune are highly limited and none of them are truly chiptune. Basic waveforms from using the highest octaves on the basses only gets you so far, most retro sound is complex combinations that require a lot more than layering. From how I see it, your ability to create dubstep and heavy trap using Auxy is currently limited by your own fixation at having these sounds off the bat, without experimentation. Try messing around with the settings, and you’ll get what you need. For someone like you I’m sure you’ll be able to do it, as you have unique talent that is still unmatched by other producers that use Auxy. Don’t see this as a discouragement, but as inspiration. If it’s a bit harsh, I blame that on waiting for the doctor right now.
