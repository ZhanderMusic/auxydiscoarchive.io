akabillposters November 29, 2017, 10:22pm #6

I think this is a great composition.

To follow on from @yaneeck’s line of thinking…

The notes feel quite rigid, at the moment, due to the rigid quantisation and lack of variety in note volume levels.
While there’s not much you can do about the quantisation, you could explore using more variety in the note volumes.

Of course, there’s the ‘soft note’ option, but two levels is probably not enough for a piano piece.
You might be able to achieve it with volume automation. But if not, try breaking it down into layers of multiple instances of the piano instrument.

Split the piano into two instruments, one at full volume (still using full and soft notes) and another a (e.g.) half volume (still using full and soft notes). This effectively gives you four levels of loudness, which enables you to build more subtlety into the playing style.
Add another instance, and you have six levels, which should be plenty to make it feel more humanised.

Then, it’s just about deciding which parts of the piece you want to add subtlety to.
I’d start by looking at note runs and trills.

I’d also consider exploring more variety in note length. The whole piece feels very staccato.
It makes it feel like the piano version of a dance track, but maybe that’s the purpose, but I think it might weaken its value as a piano piece.

 
[Edit. Didn’t mean to sound as if I was telling you what to do with your music. My comments are just suggestions.]

OK, I’ve listened through intently now a couple of times.

Emotionally, it feels optimistic, with a slightly sad edge (through the combination of dominant chords, and how bright you take the notes).

It feels energetic, quite active, due to the tempo and the ‘bassline’.

It moves along at such a pace, that I feel like there’s not enough time to savour some of the chords and melodies in there.

As originally said, I do think it’s a fantastic composition. No question in my mind.

 
I do still have trouble with it feeling mechanical, though. I couldn’t get past it. The rhythm and the volume/velocities are so regulated, and the notes so tightly packed, that I’m constantly reminded that this isn’t a human playing, but midi playback. And for me, that takes away something special.

If it’s trying to tell a human story, express something human, I wonder if some of it is getting a little lost in translation.
