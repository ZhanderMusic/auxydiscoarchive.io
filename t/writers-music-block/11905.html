akabillposters August 27, 2018, 1:57pm #6

I’ve found it helpful to…

Spend some time setting out a skeleton structure for the track. Having that in place can help highlight the changes in movement needed at each step.

Letting a track ‘build itself’ organically can be great when the ideas are flowing. But that approach can also leave you feeling rudderless when the ideas aren’t flowing.

If needed, find a reference track that you like and respect and analyse the structure - where the builds are, where the variation kicks in, etc…

This might trigger ideas and directions for how to develop the track.
