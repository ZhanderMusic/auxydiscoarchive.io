Cheddr August 16, 2017, 12:24am #15

A.
I like the creative constraints idea. All kinds of artists have used this.

More ideas:

  • write a song that is only 30 seconds long but still has a beginning middle and an end
  • use ALL of the sounds from one sound pack in the same song.
  • or use only 3 sounds
  • don’t use drums
  • pick something to really focus on like transitions/automation, rhythms, chords, progressions, or melodies.

When you pick your head up again after burying it in one of these projects you’ll think of things you had never thought of before.

B.
Change of scenery. I travel a lot for work and I get creativity surges every time I’m in a new place

C.
Watch some YouTube videos on music theory or techniques.

I like Adam Neely, the samurai guitarist, Paul davids, and Andy othling.

Try to work something very specific you learned into one song. Or base a whole song around that thing.
