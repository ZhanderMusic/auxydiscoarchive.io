Jax October 6, 2019, 3:56am #4

I get writers block all the time, but I always get out of it. I say just clear your mind, go on a bike ride, or something like that.
What doesn’t work for me is listening to other music. When I do that, I try to recreate the style and I always get frustrated and then get even more writers block.
To sum up, do something that isn’t music, and then you’ll be making music in no time! (Trust me )

bedlegs October 6, 2019, 4:26am #9

Try creating a track with something you usually don’t do first. If you usually do melody first, try drums first
If you usually sit, try standing. Try playing something live if you have a instrument.
Read a book
Anything inspire you other than music? Try that out. Remember it’s okay to take a break if you feel you need to! If you are in a creative rut you can always take the time to learn about music rather than create.
