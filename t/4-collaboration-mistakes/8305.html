4 Collaboration Mistakes

Production Talk
BSJ March 7, 2018, 2:19pm #1

Yo! I have thought about making this topic for awhile, but I didn’t think it’d be appropriate. Now I think it could be very helpful. We have feedback toward the production of each other’s songs and each other’s logos, but I believe a discussion about proper manners toward teamwork is also valid.

It’s VERY important to have a good relationship with the person you’re collaborating with. Here are 4 mistakes that HINDER the collaboration process.

  1. Lack of communication.
    You can’t expect someone to read your thoughts. They will almost ALWAYS think a little different than how you would direct the song. Communicating with what you think could work and what they think could work is essential in keeping things flowing.

  2. Picking the wrong partner.
    This one is tough. It’s hard to know how a person writes a song. You should assume that everyone composes songs differently than you. But more importantly, find yourself a willing collaborator who HAS A SIMILAR TASTE in music. A progressive house producer working with a dubstep/trap producer naturally won’t have the flow that two deep house producers would. Great minds think alike, right?

  3. Being impatient… and showing it.
    This one is HUGE. This can make or break collab relationships. Listen. The world is still turning outside of Auxy. I’m sure everyone you know here and beyond uses Auxy as a hobby. You must hold the reality that many people don’t compose 24/7. People have LOTS more important things to do. Jobs, families, etc.
    another problem is actually communicating how impatient you are. If you constantly rain down “done yet?” messages upon them, you really are driving them and others away from collaborating with you again. Just ask a very polite message that communicates to them that you are still invested in the project. A friendly reminder, for a better term. This leads me to my next point:

  4. Lack of politeness/manners
    This one is obvious. No one wants to work with a jerk. Be as polite as possible. We’re in a forum where most of us have never met. We don’t know what we shouldn’t say to one another! Politeness and friendliness goes so far in the project and further influences a collaboration in the future!

Hope I’ve been a help to you all. Every person takes things differently. It’s a tricky business to communicate complex ideas and direction to a person without ever seeing them, but there is a right way to do it!

I want to open this topic for conversation! Any points I missed? Any you disagree with? (PM me if you wanna argue ;))

OK, a few responses to that…

  1. In that scenario, just tell them. If it’s a good collaboration, they’ll understand. Creativity isn’t a tap you can turn on or off.

  4. It’s not that important that anyone should be getting their nose out of joint about it.
