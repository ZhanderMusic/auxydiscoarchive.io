Branding & Distribution
xavior April 2, 2018, 12:37am #61
Kerfuffle:

you are easy to get along with and you’re supportive to a loving degree. You’re graciousness towards those around you give an atmosphere of longing for continued exposure.

most definitely the kindest compliment I’ve ever received here. thank you so much. it sincerely means a lot, because my relationship with fellow producers matters to me. i want people to feel the most comfortable they can in an open environment like this, and i want to reciprocate just as much support and encouragement as i receive. trying to make an impact with music is bloody hard, but i think i will always be more fond of the friendships i make with people who share my passion than how many likes i get on my song. i always know there’s someone listening, so i want people to know there’s someone listening to them too.

Kerfuffle:

Will you be seeking extended members on a team for production of the channel?

Depends on how this takes off. I’m pretty confident in my ability to continue my brand as an artist through a YouTube channel, but I’m pretty sure it’s going to take a while before I feel the need to get additional support. On the other hand, I’m shy to the core, so this is kind of a big leap and part of me doesn’t want to do this completely alone. I think the biggest help would be to know what people want to hear rather than making the assumption that potential viewers are actually interested in hearing me ramble about how I do this and that.

Kerfuffle:

What are requirements to be a valid helpful asset to the team?

If someone has the tools to spread the word, they’re automatically in. I think that’s my biggest obstacle looking forward. I can share my YT link with my buddies, through Discord servers, and on the forum, but as far I know, that’s the extent of it (and the same with my music).
And as far as actual production goes, I’m pretty well-versed in lighting and sound, filming, video editing, etc. (I’ve done this all before, but I’ve never been in front of the camera before). I don’t plan on making this some super fancy video tutorial channel (no, I want it to be a lot more personal and fun than that), but I also don’t want it to look like I just scrapped it all together in iMovie in under an hour.

Kerfuffle:

How big of a team, or what goal is the endgame of it’s design?

With quality versus quantity in mind, my main focus is to reach out to the Auxy community and beyond to introduce them to myself as an artist and a quirky human personality, and show them around my personal workspace (mostly within the app, of course ) and share my most important lessons for budding producers that I haven’t seen thoroughly answered here before. For example, in one episode I’ll show how I properly mix my vocals, but in another I’m gonna take you with me on my studio photoshoot for my website I’m launching in the near future and other social media.
I’ve been making “blueprints” for this for quite a while, but team size hasn’t really been a factor I’ve deemed worthy of consideration quite yet. I want to make this as immersive as possible (interacting with viewers and listeners regularly), but I wouldn’t really know how to ‘recruit’ other people to run the show. (If you know a way I could use help, please enlighten me because this is relatively new territory for myself and I will always be learning).

Thank you again @Kerfuffle for your compliments and questions. Hope this helps.
