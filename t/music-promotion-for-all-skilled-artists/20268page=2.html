Arimyth November 21, 2020, 9:34pm #26

I’d like to point out something briefly that I don’t want to slip through the cracks amidst all the back and forth here.

ThoughtfulNoise:

As I’ve mentioned before, the ‘mobile only’ music proposition is interesting, and something which Auxy themselves have failed to really promote on. There’s been a little buzz around the topic for years, but it’s worth finding out which online music and tech publications might be interested in hearing about what you’re trying to do.

This is what I consider to be the real selling point of your promotional channel project. Amidst the thousands of music promotional channels out there including some of the more notable names like NCS, an exclusively mobile music promo channel has the potential to stand out and really break new ground as its promoting not only a certain style(s) of music but also a criminally underused form of technology within the music industry, and subsequently an entire user base around that.

While I understand the limitations and constraints you’re under as a younger student with limited access to social media, this kind of outreach requires nothing more than an email address and a web browser at a bare minimum. Find blogs and online publications that are pushing mobile music and introduce yourself, find out how you may be able to mutually assist each other in the growth of your online endeavors. Mobile music is still a relatively undervalued and small sector of the music industry and so your chances of striking a solid connection with at least one existing influencer in this space is reasonably high.

To put it simply, I think that when you start a project that is at least partially responsible for the success and growth of others beyond yourself, there’s a level of obligation that you should feel to do whatever possible to make that project successful and consequently those invested in the project successful (within reason) as well. And I think understanding how little time some of these steps can or need to take is key so that it doesn’t seem like you’re undertaking an inordinately huge amount of tasks on top of what sounds to be an already busy lifestyle.

ThoughtfulNoise November 22, 2020, 10:28am #28

To add to Arimyth’s excellently put post…

Consider reaching out to online music producers and influencers who appear to be mobile/tablet-based.

e.g. Henny Tha Bizness

 
Additional options might be sample pack companies — including those who already have packs available in Auxy, e.g. Black Octopus, Samples from Mars, etc… — i.e. companies with products that can already work in a mobile workflow.

They may be wiling to support you with a Tweet/RT or IG post, or a mention on their other channels.

Even if not, they might be able to help give some input on how best to promote and build your channels.

 
One thing to bear in mind is that, if you’re aiming at all mobile music production, don’t limit yourself to just Auxy producers — though reaching out to the the ‘higher profile’ Auxy creators is possibly a good way to get the ball rolling.

Go for quality early, as this will attract more repeat visitors.
Better to have higher quality and less frequent posting to start, as people will still sub to a quality channel even if they don’t post often.

Then, once you’ve got access to sources of quality mobile tracks, start increasing the frequency of new posts.

…and, be sure to package new YT posts will pre-announcement posts and ‘go live’ posts on Twitter, IG, etc…

 

(Tbh, I’d consider ditching the ‘copyright free’ part of the proposition, as that’s likely to limit the quality of early submissions while your channel doesn’t yet give submissions access to large audience/exposure.

I’d focus on the mobile part of the proposition. You could always create a channel sub-playlist for those submissions which are ok with being Copyright Free.)
