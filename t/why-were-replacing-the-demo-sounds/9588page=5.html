There are many reasons why we‚Äôre doing the subscription model. One is simply that Apple doesn‚Äôt really allow any upgrade pricing scheme, so then you‚Äôre left with pay upfront or subs. We think that pay upfront is a legacy model and not longer fit for modern software development. It was different before when, like any typical product that you buy in a store, software was shipped in a box and rarely updated. That is simply not the case anymore since you expect updates regularly.

I think subscriptions are much better for aligning both risk and incentives between customers and developers. First, you don‚Äôt have to pay much upfront to get started using the app. Then, you will only pay for as long as you actually use and get value out of the product. With apps that you pay upfront, you run a risk of paying for something that you end up not using at all. And from the developer perspective, there‚Äôs little incentive to update an app that people have already paid for. So in essence, if you make an app that you consider ‚Äúdone‚Äù when you ship it, then charging upfront could make sense.
