A cover version or remix is not plagiarism, unless the remix/cover performer/producer is passing off the original recording or composition as their own*.

Assuming you’d be rebuilding any chords, melodies, etc… from scratch, it’s a ‘publishing copyright’ issue, as you’re using the composition, but not the recording. If you were using samples, then that’s a ‘publishing + recording copyright’ issue.

All that said, it’s extremely unlikely that the publishing owner is unlikely to give a sh*t, unless, by some outlandish chance, one of our remixes/covers blew up, got serious traction and started making significant income for the remixer/cover artist.

 
Fwiw, here’s SoundCloud’s, YouTube’s and a more general outline on the question…

  • Best practices for creating and uploading a cover version
  • How to legally post a cover song video on YouTube
  • Everything You Need to Know About Releasing a Cover Song - Stem

 

 
* I hear there’s a little problem with actual plagiarism here within the Auxy community, with people passing other people’s tracks off as their own. Even so, you’d have to be a special kind of idiot to claim either the source composition or original recording of Shape of You as your own — assuming you’re not actually one of the real copyright holders, which is possible and you’re hanging out on the Auxy forum, I guess.
