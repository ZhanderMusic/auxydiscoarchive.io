Does knowledge limit creativity?

Random
DHO May 12, 2018, 4:28am #1

So, when I was first starting with Auxy, I had no knowledge about music or how to make it. I built tracks through experimentation, and although it wasn’t always great, it was my sound. It was unique and charming, and I loved it.

At some point, I started learning things. Things about chords, music theory stuff, and how to make certain genres I enjoyed. I was thankful for the guidelines and the help. My music skyrocketed to another level of quality, and I was always proud of it.

But something happened. Although it was better music, it wasn’t my music. It was good music, something you could dance to, but nothing about it sounded like the style I had made. It just seemed to lack creativity. It became harder and harder to make unique sounding chords, and I somehow forgot how I used to make them.

I was eventually able to overcome this and now try to blend my knowledge and creativity together, leading me to finally decide knowledge both limits and enhances creativity. It has its ups and downs. Although my stuff did lose some of the originality it once had, whenever I am able to blend my knowledge and creativity, the result is of a higher quality. However, there was a long period of time when I thought it only limited it, so I was wondering about all of your thoughts.

There’s a certain extent to where it can. This comes to where you do not stray from what you know works. Take a look at modern pop for example. Something works, so it’s done ad infinitum until it doesn’t work anymore, then they go onto the next thing. Remember Sea Punk?

Remember dubstep’s appropriation into pop music? Or maybe for people like @iammane, remember when anything electronic was called “Techno” even if it wasn’t actually techno?

Maybe I got a little sidetracked, but if you are afraid to sound bad, your music will sound differently from how you really want it to.

I would say pure fear of doing something wrong limits creativity more than anything else you could think of. It’s always fear at the root of these things. Be fearless with your music. That might mean not being afraid to use the tried and true chords, etc. If you’re really good at that and the stuff sounds great and makes you proud who cares? Or it might mean not being afraid to incorporate more advanced/weird/complicated stuff into your tunes if you feel the need.

See what I mean? Don’t box yourself in. Don’t be afraid. And for chrissake don’t torture yourself over your art. That’s the worst thing you can do. Leave that for the folks who whine all day about searching for “inspiration” and then never get jack done. Don’t be one of those people. Put your ass in the chair, get to work, and HAVE FUN
