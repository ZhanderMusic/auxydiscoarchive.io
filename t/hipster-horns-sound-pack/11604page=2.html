Sunny August 9, 2018, 11:31am #39

@tornait I’d make something out of it first before turning it down. Most digital instruments do sound kinda crap, but they’re really not meant to be alone. Tweaking is key here.

With orchestra instruments, think of them like flat colours, and it’s your job to spice them up. Change that firetruck red into a beautiful tomato, babeh.

A good piano has a bit of reverb happening. A good guitar sound is far more complicated than a single note — using strumming, percussive elements and a whole lotta rhythm (you could probably find a slap sound in the massive drum kit collection). Flutes (well, June), Brass and other windy sounding things sound especially bleh when they’re just samples, but if you try to make it sound organic (reverb, lowpass wobblings to imitate breath, if it doesn’t sound good as a high note work on the low notes), then it sounds like some red faced person that got forced into the wind section EVEN THOUGH THEY WANTED TO DO DRUMS AND THE FRENCH HORN WAS TOO BIG FOR A THIRD GRADER.

I’m a bit more hands on with the viola, and you gotta make some danged vibrato on that babe to make it sound sexy. They wiggle the finger. Also, pitch is awesome on the strings!

Anything you can do to make these bad boys sound pretty decent is a big step. They do sound kinda RRRREEEE at times, but nothing tweaking and stacking can’t fix.
