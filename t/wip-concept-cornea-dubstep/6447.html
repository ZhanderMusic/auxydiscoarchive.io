akabillposters January 20, 2018, 2:36pm #4

I like it. Proper dub vibe in that there dubstep.
(Maybe that’s more common than I realise. DS not something I listen to, but Dub was, in the past.)

Obviously, needs a proper work-up, but I think the core idea has potential.

I’d consider sticking some delay and reverb on those 2-tone piano/brass stabs, to really drive home the dub influence, something off-beat, like 1/12 or 3/16.

(By contrast, you could keep the reverb and delay relatively low during the formal DS parts, to give that a distinct tighter, more up-close/in-your-face ‘space’ in the track.

 
Structurally, I like that it starts with the more dub-centric part, then kicks it up a gear, into more formal DS.
You then bring it back down to the dub-centric part again for outro…
…but…
I’d certainly bring that dub-centric part back again, as a bridge/breather… but then go back to the DS vibe, through to the end (evolving the sound internally, but staying with the DS vibe).

I’d make the DS portion even more hyped. This will add more contrast with the dub-centric part, so that when you go back to the DS part for a second time, that feels like the kind of peak energy that can see you through to the end of the track.

Obviously, what you’ve created so far is a short, ‘proof-of-concept’/prototype, that’ll need to be filled out and lengthened out.

I think it’s a good idea and a good start.
