This has come up before, and I’ll echo what was previously said cause I think it was good advice.

If you’re going to rebrand, be sure you don’t already have big audience, be sure to pick something meaningful to you and also to pick something that is easy to pronounce (within reason).

It’s not a great idea to ask someone else what you should be called. It’s like having someone pick your clothes in the morning. It’s a decision you (and everyone) should make because it should be meaningful to you.

Or you could just call yourself Steve

Quick Edit: The audience part is only mentioned because you could potentially alienate those who already know you as one name. At the end of the day though, your music and your sound will distinctly be you, it can just cause confusion.

I was just checking to see if your friends and family are legitimate/valuable sources of input for what is a personal creative endeavour.

My family aren’t as none of them would enjoy listening to the kinds of music I make. And, the only friends whose opinion I tend to seek regarding my music are those who would enjoy the kind of music I make or who work in the industry.

Even then, I rarely ask, as the right answer needs to feel right to me, not a committee of other people.

I might sometimes seek input to break an internal stalemate, e.g. when I have multiple options that I’m stuck choosing between. But I won’t let the opinions of others make the final decision.
