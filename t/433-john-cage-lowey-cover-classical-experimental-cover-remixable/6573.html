akabillposters January 24, 2018, 8:43am #16
cgedius:

reflecting to a kind of painting which use just water without colours on a blank canvas

A good example might be ‘monochrome’ paintings, such as those by Yves Klein or, even better, ‘white paintings’ such as those by Robert Ryman.

The viewer is asked to enquire deeper, and ask questions about what is there – and also what isn’t there.

What does ‘flatness’ mean in a world defined by ‘edges’ and boundaries?
What does ‘silence’ mean (audio or visual) in a world defined by sound/noise?

How closely do we typically look, listen and think?

There’s information and beauty in the detail.

(It’s part of the reason why I enjoy experiencing minimalist art and music so much.)
