ThoughtfulNoise June 19, 2020, 3:41pm #8

Cool. Yeah, the Auxy sounds can be a little hit n miss.
The brass can be really effective and convincing, when used the way you have.
They work well for those rich, warm chords.

(Side-note: Fantastic chords, by the way. Rich and lush extensions.
It’s cool to hear Auxy being used for more rich, extended chord compositions. With certain sounds, it can be really effective and compelling.)

Cool to hear that you’re interested in developing it further.

In the meantime, I’d suggest perhaps trying out some other basses, maybe even avoid the acoustic/electric bass guitar sound pack. It’s a tricky one to emulate well, and I don’t think the pack is particularly successful at achieving a convincing sound.
As it’s such a core part of your track, the cheapness really cuts.

Maybe there’s more that can be done while still in Auxy. Maybe a layered option; a sub with texture to flesh out the low-end (currently missing in your bass) and a little grit, then maybe layering on the slightest top-end of an electric bass for character?

(You might still need to subtly automate the LPF and volume of the electric bass, just to give some variance. Part of the main problem/giveaway of the current bass choice is the lack of variance in the slap/twang, etc…)

For a track like yours, it pays to have a clear idea of what bass you’re aiming to emulate.
For instance, the one you’ve used is quite slappy, which might not be the right sound for your track.
Perhaps listen again to some Zero 7 and others from the Downtempo genre to hear what choices they’ve made on their basses.

Do you also use a desktop DAW?
There are some really good, affordable electric bass VSTs around, and loads of great free/trial synth basses available.

Sax is more challenging. The good VSTs are expensive, and even then you need to really know what you’re doing to get something really convincing out of them.

(I’ve also got some sax sections in recent pieces that I need to step up on too. Some uses work better than others, with some parts still sounding very midi-fied. So, I’m looking at the options.)
