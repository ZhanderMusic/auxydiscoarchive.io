iammane May 16, 2019, 11:09am #3

I’m no expert at this, but given the climate of the industry, I think you’d have more success running through repost networks and self promotion. You can release music for free through sites like BandCamp and distributors like Amuse. There’s a few to choose from.

A label (in the traditional sense) is going to be interested in making money. That’s what they do, that’s what they are there for. There’s quite a lot of people who write music, so it’s more likely they’ll want to pick up already established artists with a following so that they don’t have to take a chance paying out of pocket on an unknown. The only advantage I can think of with submitting work to any kind of label as opposed to self releasing is promotion.

I’ve been told getting your music out there is 10% production and 90% promotion. It’s probably true. I don’t say all this to discourage you, by all means, I’d shoot out my work everywhere, but, definitely don’t PAY UP FRONT that’s ridiculous. That’s like paying to have something done around your house in full before the job is even started…

Arimyth May 16, 2019, 6:57pm #6

It sounds like you’re getting “labels” and “promo channels” confused.

Labels are the accounts that will release your song, usually on their account, and should have a demo submissions link on their page, for example:

Promo channels are the accounts that will repost your song, sometimes for a bit of green like you said, and usually don’t require more than a follow / like on their page to become “eligible” for their promotion. For example:

I personally don’t go around messaging promo channels (let alone paying for them) because I find them to be a complete waste of time due to the high volume of tracks that they actually promote (your track will get buried almost instantly and it provides no real engagement). I’d stick to growing your audience in a more organic manner and building up contacts that you know to help promote your music.
